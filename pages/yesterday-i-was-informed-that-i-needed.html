Yesterday, I was informed that I needed to make an Oracle thread because some people see them as usesless in #pathfinder2e. They aren't.useless, I think they're more of an advanced choice than the other four APG classes, and require a mindset fairly uncommon to d20 fans. ðŸ§µ

In #dnd and Pathfinder, players hate risk and trade-off. Even the players that are champions for game balance want all take and no give. The oracle is about that give and take. Over almost 10 years of play, I've watched players in every d20 system I've played horde spell slots

consumables, even inspiration and hero points. Oracle is not for players who can't will themselves to take risks and I bet that's most players. "But Az, we roll dice, that's not risk." But if I told you, "Roll a 15 or higher to attack, otherwise you die next turn," you'd balk.

The Oracle is a nod to stories of self-sacrifice performed by powerful people. Non-Oracle style players assume the oracle will stand in for the Cleric. I'd argue they're no more a stand in for Cleric than the Bard, Psychic, or Sorcerer. So, how do you build a successful Oracle?

1st. Find spells that accentuate your playstyle. Battle Oracle, use spells like Heroism and Seal Fate. Contingency can be an incredible pick for Mysterious Repitoire. 2nd. Find items that will do what your party won't. If they see your Oracle as a liability, gear hero yourself.

Does your Mystery grant you Nature or Occultism? Best pick Trick Magic Item. 3rd. On the topic of feats: Assurance is your best friend. Half the curses you'll get will inflict status penalties, Assurance covers that + Trick Magic Item.

4th. Don't be afraid to use archetypes. If your GM is using Free Archetypes, solid. But if not, still isn't a bad move. Acrobat is an incredible pick for Cosmos Oracle, as is Wrestler for Battle Oracle. Champion is always a great choice if your stats are right.

5th and finally. If you're scared of getting your Oracle's curse effects, ask yourself "why are you playing an Oracle?" Embrace the Oracle's flaws and push for risk. To me, Extreme Curse sounds dope as hell. Death Ward surpresses the doomed condition. Swing for the fences!

Bonus: Rare backgrounds are awesome. They just make sense mechanically and a lot of the power they afford helps big time. Amnesiac Oracle gets a HUGE boost to start play with. Chosen One's fortune effect is very on brand. I strongly recommend them for Oracles, hands down.

*than the other 3. Oops.

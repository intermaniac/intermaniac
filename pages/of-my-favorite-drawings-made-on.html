Thread of my favorite drawings made on Integrated Circuits (IC) at microscopic level from the "Silicon Zoo" of Michael W. Davidson. #science #Art #chip #computers #Linux https://t.co/bxTmO4WEcM

"Guitar-Strumming Tyrannosaurus Rex". This dinosaur was found next to the mask design credits for Silicon Graphics' MIPS R12000 microprocessor mask. (https://t.co/CPz3UXgVKd) https://t.co/TQCHntcGiE

"The Pepsi Generation" This Pepsi was found on a Hewlett-Packard CPU support integrated circuit. This is probably the smallest Pepsi ad ever. Beat that Coca-Cola lovers. (https://t.co/GcpOqucjys) https://t.co/lVc3O5NjMw

"The Rolex" This luxurious little Rolex was discovered nestled in the clock circuitry of a Hewlett-Packard PA-7100 microprocessor. It may be the cheapest Rolex ever seen. (https://t.co/ad1FtDGvzQ) https://t.co/oC1KsU7IjB

"Kestrel (Sparrow Hawk)." A Hewlett-Packard design team led by Howard Hilton was responsible for placing perhaps the world's smallest representation of a kestrel (sparrow hawk) on an integrated circuit. (https://t.co/2rcTR9dYNX) https://t.co/WmIkBTFcWf

"Tux, the Linux Penguin". A beautiful miniature replica of Tux, the Linux penguin nesting in the pad ring of an integrated circuit of unknown type and function. I think this one is my favorite. (https://t.co/BjLTy46T3b) https://t.co/XDdcV0koVH

"We've Got Roaches. Our greatest enemy, a bug. In this case, in a Hewlett-Packard integrated circuit, made by Craig Robson. (https://t.co/yQdeGu8aGk) https://t.co/dpm1AnuDAS

"The Dream." This is a beautiful winning poker hand on a Digital Equipment Company (DEC) CVAX microprocessor, the brains behind the DEC MicroVAX 3000 and 6200 series of minicomputers. (https://t.co/dIeCdxs1bD) https://t.co/EQ7XuykebE

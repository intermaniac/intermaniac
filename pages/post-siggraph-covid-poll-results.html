Post-SIGGRAPH COVID poll results: https://t.co/gFvRAt1BCz The GitHub repo has all the details, as well as the anonymized raw data and the python code that computed the statistics. Some highlights: üßµ 1/

* Almost 20% of polled attendees got COVID-19 (pos test), >20% if you also count people with COVID symptoms but either didn't test at all or got a negative test. * If 20% is representative of all attendees, then we estimate that 2000 or more people caught COVID at SIGGRAPH. 2/

* 15% of polled attendees who were pos for COVID reported that other household members who did not attend SIGGRAPH subsequently tested positive as well. It is possible that as many as a few hundred non-attending family members were infected. 3/

* It didn't seem to make a difference if attendees flew to Vancouver, arrived by ground transportation, or lived in Vancouver and didn't travel at all. Travel itself, or the mode thereof, did not appear to be a significant driver of transmission. 4/

* Surprisingly, whether people individually masked in the conference venues did not make any significant difference. (But maybe it would have helped if everybody did.) 5/

* The number of crowded social events did not make any difference if they were OUTDOORS or if people were MASKED indoors. BUT... ü•Åü•Åü•Å 6/

* The number of crowded UNMASKED INDOOR social events was important -- those who went to 3+ such events got COVID at **twice** the rate of those who did not attend any. The difference between these groups is statistically significant with p = 0.02. 7/7

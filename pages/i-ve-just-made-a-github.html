I've just made a GitHub repository (https://t.co/WPAzsM2b0r">https://t.co/WPAzsM2b0r) where I addressed the problem of finding Line Breaking Passes using @statsbomb 360 open data. This thread briefly discusses an algorithm that individuates line breaking passes and presents some tendencies at Euro2020 https://t.co/49D3u94QZA

This problem was often overlooked for the lack of tools (tracking data) and not for the lack of importance, is the ability of player or a team to find teammates behind the enemy lines, i.e., the ability to make passes that dissect a line of the opponent‚Äôs formation.

StatsBomb360 data allows to have the positions of a subset of defenders and therefore I decided to apply the ideas presented in https://t.co/tzcFQAA59U by @KubaMichalczyk to StatsBomb360 instead of tracking data.

A pass is considered a Line Breaking Pass (LBP) if: - The pass is successful; - Progress the ball forward at least 10 meters; - It breaks at least one defensive line, which is defined as a ‚Äúvertical line‚Äù between two defenders that are at most 8 m apart.

Note that it may break more than one line, but we are interested only if it breaks at least one. https://t.co/17kXy4pOgk

Scheme of the algorithm: 1) The positions of the defenders, which may be less than 11 in the visible area, is taken by considering the SB360 frame corresponding to the start of the pass (we freeze the defenders at that time). 2) We exclude the goalkeeper from the defenders

3) The defensive lines are identified with a 1-D clustering Fisher-Jenks algorithm and by ordering along the y-direction. The number of clusters is set between (0,3) depending on the number of defenders in the frames.

4) We compute if the segment line of the pass intersects with one defensive line Time of execution for 51 games with a very common notebook: approx. 10 minutes!

Is it worth to look at line breaking passes? As a metric to quantify actions value I considered StatsBomb‚Äôs On-Ball Value (OBV), On the right we can see that the distribution of LBPs have a higher median OBV than not-LBPs, i.e., the usually produce more values. https://t.co/Zej2idKwrq

Since the OBVs are not publicly available, you cannot find these values in the repository. I'd like to thank Statsbomb for providing me these values.

As expected, this is also a sanity check for the algorithm, CBs and Midfielders usually make more LBPs than the other roles. https://t.co/n8St51rOJW

Here instead the percentage of LBPs of total passes divided by roles. https://t.co/Fj5X5tms71

Now some player standings about the LBS. Interestingly, Bruno Fernandes had the highest percentage of LBPs. https://t.co/wQniYvgQ0f

Same standings but for teams. https://t.co/aSBUNpdQxr

My work was also inspired by this article https://t.co/NJPguhFCIX by @jair1970. I believe that Statsbomb algorithm performs better than mine but It would be interesting to see some comparison, if it's possible.

Perspectives: -To incorporate height of passes in the algorithm. - To discriminate which defensive line is broken by the pass. - Investigating which players are the best line breaking pass receivers. - Testing different 1-D clustering models .

- To narrow the ‚Äúvertical‚Äù distance between defenders to find which players make narrow line breaking passes

For those who are interested in playing a little bit with Line Breaking Passes, you can download a csv that contains information about all passes made at Euro2020 with the added information by the algorithm. https://t.co/WPAzsM2b0r">https://t.co/WPAzsM2b0r

You can also reproduce the analyses since the intere code is available ( I numbered the notebooks for clarity). Any feedback would be greatly appreciated!

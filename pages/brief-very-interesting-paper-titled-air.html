A brief thread on a very interesting paper titled; " Air Quality, High-Skilled Worker Productivity and Adaptation: Evidence from GitHub" by Felix Holub and Beate Thies. Beate is on the job market now. https://t.co/W663ByXovS

As Sam Boysel has taught me GitHub, GitHub allows observers to see which open source software coding project progress takes place in which cities on which days by which coders. Does air pollution slow down the productivity of the coders? This paper presents OLS and IV estimates

Pei Li and I wrote a relevant paper here that did not appear in Top 5 Journal. https://t.co/YDwbv8M1Mc

One comment about the paper. If coders become aware that PM2.5 lowers their productivity (perhaps because PM2.5 grows worse due to wild fires), they will invest in self protection such as an air filter and the smoke induced productivity loss will shrink to zero.

In a lumpy adaptation economy, small shocks cause more damage! The larger the expected pollution shock ==> lumpy investments such that the "climate damage function" flattens. One shouldn't extrapolate from the original damage function. See my 2021 book. https://t.co/axDg0lXTIL

The API3 protocol allows data providers to run oracle nodes themselves, thus eliminating middlemen. Due to no middlemen, data providers can no longer remain anonymous providing full transparency and accountability into where the data is coming from.

API3 has a modular design with three layers built on top of each other: the protocol layer, the middle layer, and the top layer. Depending on development needs, a dApp can consume API3 services at each of the three layers. https://t.co/ScZPwfhVPU

API3 offers service coverage against dAPI malfunction to mitigate potential security, governance, and reliability issues. API3 might compensate dApps for API downtimes or data feed inaccuracies based on potential malfunction risks and the quality/quantity of the data.

As of September 13, 2022, the total supply of API3 tokens was 115 million. Out of this total supply, 100 million API3 was initially allocated in November 2020, and the remaining 15 million API3 has been minted as staking rewards to date. https://t.co/kH1WZuNmzH

API3 released the QRNG API to its platform in May 2022 and is able to provide truly random numbers to smart contracts. From a security standpoint, to optimally address the Sybil attack risk, it is critical to be able to generate truly random keys.

Being a first mover in terms of transparency and service coverage, API3 plays a significant role in the technical advancement of oracles. However, as a project still in its early days. The real test for API3 will be gaining sustainable traction against competitors like Chainlink.

The best way to stand out in data analytics is with an amazing online portfolio. Here is everything you need to build your perfect portfolio today. [THREAD] #rstats #python

I started out in data looking for work to apply my skills. It occurred to me that I can do data analytics without a boss. You can too. Let me show you what you'll need...

1/ Pick a skill It could be Python, Excel, RStudio, PowerBI, etc. For now let's just focus on one.

2/ Problem For your first project, pick a topic and related problem that meet these criteria... • You're interested in the topic • You can find data on the topic • You have some confidence in solving the problem Next...

3/ Audience This initial project needs an intended audience. Later I'll cover how to bring viewers to your portfolio. Build it for one person and many will be interested. I promise.

4/ Goal Will your project help one person make a decision? Will it simplify a complex topics? Once you have your problem, audience, and goal, it is time to look for data.

5/ Acquire the data I love doing work on my own data. It makes in much more interesting. Go in search of your own financial or health data. If that's not what you're looking for, check this out from @avikumart_ https://t.co/k36zsIrhTl

@avikumart_ 6/ Understand the variables. Learn as much as you can about each variable in your data set. This is the only way I have been able to provide maximum value. This is also why I love working with my own data. I know so much about it. What's next...

@avikumart_ 7/ Perform necessary analytics Whatever type of insights you are looking for, remember to use your chosen tool, and remember your problem, audience, and goal.

@avikumart_ 8/ Visualize 99% of the people who want your insights do not care about the tools or code. They just want results that make sense to them. Put together your insights into a visual that tells a story. You can use: • exports form R • dashboards • @canva • excel charts

@avikumart_ @canva 9/ Website Go to the portfolio section on @carrd pick a template add your information. add your visual. add your resume/cv. add your contact information. Don't waste time on this. Keep it simple.

@avikumart_ @canva @carrd 10/ Attention Now you need website visitors. This is how I get hundreds of viewers: • post the visual on twitter, IG, or LinkedIn • talk about what you learned on any of these platforms • Bookmark and repeat this thread

@avikumart_ @canva @carrd That's a wrap! If you enjoyed this thread: 1. Follow me @gib_hurst for more data analytics threads 2. RT the first tweet to share this thread with your audience

That tweet was about data analytics. Imagine yourself growing your data analytics skills. How is it gonna feel when you finally add those skills to your resume? Think of how much fun it must be working in tech/data. All you need is a guide like me. Book a consultation today.

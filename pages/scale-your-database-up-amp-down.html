A predictable workload could be a ride-hailing service. You know that the demand increases a lot at certain times. Between 00 - 06 am almost nobody is using it. But between 07 - 09 am a lot of people need to go to work. Your database will have more requests https://t.co/sReBehiJx4

Unpredictable workloads can be events you're not aware of. For example, if a concert or a football match happens that you didn't account for your database will get a HUGE number of requests at the same time as well. https://t.co/zwszZNlQrw

Upscaling & Downscaling Upscaling means your database requires more resources like CPU or storage. Downscaling means that your database needs fewer resources to save costs. RDS allows you to define auto-scaling policies for these cases https://t.co/fAVOUVW6m0

Horizontal vs Vertical Scaling Horizontal scaling means more instances Vertical Scaling means improving the current instance - more CPU, Storage, etc. https://t.co/pGYHrbOyNc

Horizontal Scaling with Read Replicas You can scale your DB by adding read replicas. This can reduce the load on your primary database and can also be used cross-regional For example, if your DB is in us-east-1 and your users are in US and EU https://t.co/pjXl3noDCr

You can add a read replica in eu-central-1 and point all EU users to this database. This will reduce the read load a lot on your primary DB RDS also allows you to scale horizontally by adding RDS proxies. https://t.co/7ppwIkcIS3

I'd like to share with you all the *terrible* experience I've just had trying to attend the AWS Cloud Practioner exam using the Pearson VUE online proctoring system called OnVue. @Pearson @PearsonVUE #OnVue ðŸ§µ

Unfortunately the location where I live (Fortaleza/Ceara/Brazil) does not have a Testing Centre anymore. It used to have though. The only option left for me is taking the exam with the OnVue system.

As I don't own either a Mac or a Windows I've decided to use my father's PC during the exam. It runs a Windows 10 A few days before the exam I've downloaded the OnVue's system test software and then ran it and performed all necessarilly checks it asked for. All tests passed! Good

At the exam's day I've decided to arrive 2 hours earlier from the scheduled time. I then cleared the room and the desk from papers and pens, unplugged and removed the second monitor and the headphones, and stuff. All clean!

Then I've downloaded OnVue's test software and ran again the system's test 1 hour before the exam's check-in process time. All check's passed! Great, Let's go! Not that easy...

When the check-in process started, I've then ran the their same system to start it. To my surprise the system told me that the Network check failed.

Why? Because it detected that my download speed were too slow to proceed, more precisely it said I only had a 2-3 Mbps download rate. What?! Just the same network that had just passed all checks right before the exam... Retrying the testing changed nothing.

Why the system checks right before the exam passed just fine and affirmed me that my network was good enough then out of a sudden it's not good anymore?

I've then ran my ISP's speedtest to check it. Maybe this can be a problem... Not really! My speedtest scored a *600Mpbs* download rate! Oh my god why OnVue tells me only 2-3 Mbps? I don't have a clue and probably I will never have. BTW, I were using a wired connection, not wifi.

What can be done then? I've contacted the OnVue's Chat Support on their website. They've said it had no technical issues reported and told me that I needed to fix the network issue in order to proceeed. Ok but how? How can I show that my internet is actually ok? No Firewalls too

Time passes... and all retried checks with OnVue failed... and that's it. time's up.

I can't reschedule at the day of the exam because I didn't predicted OnVue's out of a sudden random decision to judge my internet download speed slow. *100 USD* flies away... no certificate, no exam, no rescheduling and no money back.

There is nothing I can do about it besides sharing with you this frustrating and unfair experience using OnVue proctoring system. *It Sucks!* I can only regret trying to take an exan with OnVue.

I must say that I have much respect for Pearson's Vue in general. Good website and amazing Testing Centres. But unfortunately the OnVue experience was really disappointing, stressful and quite frustrating.

It feels like being cheated and I have nothing to do about it. I'll never be doing another exam with them and I don't recommend it to anyone. Think *twice* (or *more*) before picking it when taking a certification exam.

Over the past few days, I conducted a small experiment to identify a very niche category of products on @Steam: games that are only available on üçè macOS. No Windows or Linux version. Only macOS. Some context first üîç I'm currently working on a game project that will target Apple platforms exclusively. More info on why here: https://t.co/DWzWC3Ufqb And I was wondering if I was the only one stupid enough to do that. üòè

Turns out there is no simple way to get this information. On @SteamDB or even on Steam itself, you can get the list of games available on macOS, but not exclude those available elsewhere. So I built my own very simple PHP script to query information for every appID on Steam. https://t.co/EvHr3w1luA

Statistically on a platform as huge as Steam, there should be some appIDs that match this description. 23 to be exact. Many are not games. Some are basically a macOS variant appID. Luckily, it was easy to filter out any false positive from the short list extracted by my script. https://t.co/u9aneIOSjN

Remained 12 macOS only games. 12 over more than 150,000 appIDs. Clearly not a trend. Let's look closer to these games üîç (exhaustive list at the end of thread)

First thing I noticed is that almost every one of them used SpriteKit or SceneKit, Apple proprietary 2D/3D frameworks. It is essentially locking you on Apple platforms. If not a deliberate choice at the beginning of your project, I suppose the revelation would be painful. üò®

Also without surprise, even if this is very subjective topic, most of the released games are not polished, right from their Steam page. macOS is a niche market for gaming (2 to 3% according to the Steam Hardware Survey), so none is a huge success.

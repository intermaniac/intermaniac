I look back at 2022 in retrospect and 2023 in deep anticipation. In my mind, the 2022 #stateoftheword for #WordPress revealed a lot of things I'd closed my eyes to. Here are a few concepts that help me love @wordpress the more. A thread.

#opensource and #WordPress allow you to: 1. Run the program (install and test and break) 2. Study and change (make it personal) 3. Redistribute (Get feedback) 4. Distribute your copies (hack something personal. tell the world).

#WordPress turns 20 in 2023! It's been 20 amazing years, and I've been active in 10 of those. From the days when widgets were the in thing, to PSD-to-WordPress projects, then shiny themes and themeforest. What has been your experience over the years?

On buzz word that we've all enjoyed is the power of #FullSiteEditing. I've personally taken it for a sweet ride, and have been able to do sites in half the time. Now, with phase 2 of Gutenberg coming to an end in 03.2023, we expect even more awesomeness.

I heard that #WordPress will bring home the power of #CollaborativeEditing (a Google docs experience for the platform). How amazing! I figure zeroing on the very specifics with clients, and discussing the least details with ease (Hope we will be able to send updates via mail).

Plugin categorization in full swing, will include the following categories: 1. Single-player plugin (made by me, no collaboration) 2. Community plugin (allows, and even asks for, revision) 3. Cannonical plugin(endorsed by WordPress) 4. Commercial plugin (owned by an agency)

Now to my favorite segment: Playground. Here's the beta: https://t.co/7dMFHQnq2j I summarize it as: testing without breaking. In 2023 I purpose to use this a lot. Test stuff (including the "dangerous" without issues). Thanks #WordPress!

What of AI? ChatGPT? PHP8? These questions will continue to yield answers as we go through 2023. For me, it will be a beautiful #WordPress'ing year.

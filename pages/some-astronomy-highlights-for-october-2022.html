Some #Astronomy highlights for October 2022, a thread: Best events this month: Jupiter shining brightly, peak of the #Orionids meteor shower and a partial eclipse of the Sun. #Astrophotography #Stargazing #Space

First week of the month: With the Moon low down and setting in the early evening, this leaves the late evening and early hours ideal for spotting the Milky Way. Look SW from around 22:00 BST. Best seen from a dark location. https://t.co/ilV4WJzPhf

All month: The #Orionids meteor shower is active, with the peak on 21-22 October. All month: #Jupiter remains bright all month. Visible as a very bright 'star' to the south as soon as it gets dark. The large Galilean moons also visible in binoculars and telescopes. https://t.co/N5xynvrR2m

1st: Spot the crescent #Moon low down in the SW from 19:00 to 21:00 BST. It should make a pleasant sight against the colourful sky just after sunset. Today is also International Observe the Moon Night so share your images using #ObserveTheMoon.

3rd: First Quarter Moon. 5th: The 80% lit #Moon sits just below #Saturn this evening. Look to the SE from early evening to see the pair. https://t.co/WhAkxlEYpJ

8th: The #Moon is close to #Jupiter this evening. This is also a chance to see five moons in the same field of view. From 19:00 to 21:00 BST you can see the four Galilean moons of Jupiter around the planet and just over 2Â° away, our own Moon. Could make an interesting photograph. https://t.co/OtdglrdbRA

8th: #Mercury reaches greatest western elongation. 9th: Full #Moon. 12th: The 90% lit Moon is close to the Pleiades cluster this evening.

14th: The 75% lit #Moon is close to the planet #Mars this evening. Look to the NE from around 21:00 BST to see them rising. They will remain close all evening. A nice photo opportunity. 17th: Last Quarter Moon. https://t.co/Mh4LMuEKld

19th: If you observe #Jupiter in the ESE at 19:00 BST you'll see the shadows of Europa and Ganymede on the planet's disc, with Europa itself also transiting. The Great Red Spot is visible too, with Europa's shadow close to it. It'll be a special view through a telescope.

21st to 22nd: Peak of the #Orionids meteor shower. This medium strength shower is active all month but the best nights to view it are 20th to 21st and 21st to 22nd. Orionids can be bright and with the Moon not rising until the early hours, this should be a good year to see them. https://t.co/v1RJiIxz0n

22nd: Saturn sits close to the star Iota Capricorni this evening. The pair can be easily seen in the field of view of a telescope or binoculars.

24th: Spot a thin 2% lit crescent #Moon close to planet #Mercury from around 06:45 BST this morning in the E. An excellent photo opportunity but watch out for the rising Sun! https://t.co/iUDXzUxRK4

25th: Partial eclipse of the Sun visible from much of Europe, the Middle East and north Africa. From the UK the eclipse will be visible from around 10:00 to 12:00 BST with up to 30% of the Sun's disc covered by the Moon (from Shetland).

Partial eclipse of the Sun - the further north and east you are, the more of the Sun will be covered.

Partial eclipse of the Sun - warning - only view the event using filters approved for use with solar observing and never with the naked eye.

A thing I definitely learned about casting here in the US is that I found that my English is so different. I was taught English in school and learn about western culture through media. Here’s a thread on how to git gud for non native english speaking casters.

This is assuming you know the basics and can understand the language well. 1. Mimicry. Like what I mentioned you need to immerse yourself in the language. Watch your favorite movie/show WITH SUBTITLES. You’ll absorb English like a sponge especially when you enjoy what you watch

2. Use apps. There are a lot of fun apps out there to expound your vocabulary but also have fun with it! Duolingo, wordscapes and 4 pics 1 word just to name a few

3. Speaking it in every day life. This is the bane of anyone who tries to learn a new language because as easy it is to absorb information you can quickly lose it once it’s not practiced. So keep practicing. Ask someone you know who is good at english and keep conversing

4. Ask a friend. This ties into the 3rd one where you have to be able to practice with someone who is better than you in English. This is a test on how far you’ve gone with your preparation. Maybe even ask them to be your mentor.

#wordpressusers Will updating WordPress affect your site? This is a question most beginner WordPress users ask… Keep reading the thread to find the answer!

The answer is yes! Sometimes, it can disrupt your site structure. But, We have the perfect solution! Here are the things you must do before updating to a new WordPress version.

01 Backup! Backup! Backup! During updates, the major risk involved is data loss. And if you don’t want this to happen to you, keep a backup of your data. You can perform this by yourself or ask the company for a regular backup service.

02 Update your themes and plugins Along with WordPress updates, themes and plugins often ask for updates. And before you begin with a WordPress update, update your themes and plugins one by one.

03 Check if you have a staging environment Some web hosting providers offer staging environments especially for avoiding the risks involved with updates. If your hosting company gives you the same facility, you can take advantage.

04 Deactivate your cache plugin Cache plugins sometimes interfere with the update process. And that’s the reason you should turn off the cache plugin before updating. Note: don’t forget to reactivate it :p

05 Click on Update and Check After the above steps, you are now ready to start with the update process. Update your site and check if it’s working alright.

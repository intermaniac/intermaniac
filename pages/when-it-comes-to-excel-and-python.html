I don't know if you know Captain Underpants. If not, let me briefly introduce you to him. Captain Underpants is a superhero with superpowers. He is the uncovered identity of Mr. Krupp, the School Principal in an elementary school in Ohio, USA.

Mr. Krupp is a mean guy who spends most of his day tormenting two students, George and Harold. One day George and Harold got fed up and decided to hypnotize him. But they made a botch of the hypnosis.

As a result, whenever Mr. Krupp hears someone snapping his fingers, he becomes Captain Underpants, a superhero fighting against injustice and stuff. And whenever his head gets wet, he becomes Mr Krupp again, mean, cranky, and stuff.

I perfectly understand that you might be thinking: "This guy has gone bonkers. What does he have to do with Captain Underpants?" Let me explain:

Apart from balding like him, I use Excel to analyze #data coming out of #solar PV plants in operation. Usually the data is simple and low-frequency, easy to get and process. But sometimes it isn't. Sometimes the data gets high-frequency and big, complicated to retrieve, or both

For me, this is the "SNAP!" moment that unleashes my Captain Underpants within. My Captain Underpants' superpower is Python.

Armed with Python I can fight against unruly data, retrieving it from the most profound lair in a remote folder. Or I can tame it to obey my command. I can lock it within a #pandas #dataframe and do with it as I please. I can group-by and summarize it from high to low frequency.

And then I can export it to an Excel #spreadsheet and plug it into a Pivot Table to share it with the world. Or I can create an impactful #visualization to prove my point.

I can do wonders.

The best part? You do not need to go through botched hypnosis to become the Captain Underpants of data. You just need to learn some Python. Not much, just some basics. And then combine it with your Excel skills.

Combining Excel with Python is like having Excel with superpowers. TRA-LA-LAAAA!!!

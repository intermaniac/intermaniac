Mr. Beast, one of the world's largest YouTubers, doesn't use logo intros. Why should you? If you want to do an intro, make it part of the current video like Mr. Beast does... https://t.co/jD4HGfRxg8

Mr. Beast's intros are like a table of contents and he smoothly goes right into the video after his intro. Sometimes you don't even notice the transition. This technique is brilliant and here's why:

1. He sets the audience's expectations. People know what to expect and aren't disappointed at the end of the video thinking he click-baited them. He's telling you the outline of the video while sprinkling in some previews of what's to come.

2. He takes the opportunity in his intro to place hooks. He gives you a brief view of the content and leaves you wanting to watch until the end to see what happens. This is very intriguing and hard to turn away from.

Some speakers use this same technique but are more obvious about it. If a speaker wants to get the audience's attention and also have them listen to the end, they might say: I am going to solve one of your major problems in life at the end of this speech so stick with me.

The local news and radio throw out hooks like this before commercial breaks. "There's something causing cancer that you consume each day, find out what it is after this commercial break"

What I say next will change the way you listen to speech forever. A lot of you might not have noticed this but Mr. Beast speaks intentionally with "down speak" and slows his sentences towards the end of each sentence. What does that mean and do?

In this video, the first few sentences Mr. Beast says. "I got 100 of my subscribers and GAVE THEM EACH ten... thousand... dollars..." https://t.co/vzrKh3eIGD

He peaks his speaking pitch usually in the middle of his sentences. Then he drops down to the lowest at the end. That's "down speak". Down speak gives the listener the feeling of confidence and authority from the speaker. Yes the opposite of "up speak"

there is also a rhythm that your mind latches on to. His sentence pitch and speaking speed follow a bell curve. Both peaking in the middle This is a very persuasive way of talking as your mind desires this pattern. Other Youtubers are figuring this out, you'll notice it now! https://t.co/DFBc7pqmt3

So be more like Mr. Beast when making a video. And with this, hopefully, when we're on Youtube we don't have to have our finger on the fast-forward trigger for much longer. Follow me @alex_aminta for more of these and RT because you're awesome!

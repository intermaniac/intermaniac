Why the #AWS Cloud Practitioner Essentials Training is likely the best place to start learning about AWS. Even if you don't intend to get the AWS Cloud Practitioner Cert A Thread #AWSCommunity #learnaws #cloudcomputing

First, the Essentials training will give you more contexts on some of the basic services. Beyond this though, it'll also give you a good foundation on the types of services available. Knowing the types of services exist can help you approach a project differently from the start.

For example, if you need to run some scripts on a recurring basis, you may think you need to setup a virtual machine via EC2 to accomplish this. If your scripts are going to run for 15 minutes or less, AWS Lambda may be a better option. Much less overhead to do this.

Second, the essentials training provides an overview of other areas such as networking and security. Even if your focus is on development or analytics, knowing the basics here can help you understand conversations where these topics are involved.

Knowing about these subjects will also help you create the appropriate requests from the AWS admin. This can save you a lot of time.

Third, the essentials training will give you a better idea about how pricing and costs work. This can be super helpful on a smaller team where you may serve several functions.

The idea of paying what you use and variable charges depending on usage can be a big change, so understanding how that works can be a big help in planning. For example, it'll be very important to shut off certain resources once their jobs are complete.

To sum up, the AWS Cloud Practitioner Training is a great starting point to: • Know types of services available • Understand the other areas of AWS • Understand the costs and pricing of AWS

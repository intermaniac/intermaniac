üßµ 1/ FTSO-What's that stand for, and what's it all about? #FTSO: Flare Time Series Oracle A system by which currency prices are submitted to the #FlareNetwork, for which participants are paid from the algorithmic inflation rate--a risk-free income stream.

2/ Oracle services (i.e. FTSO providers) submit prices of external currencies to the FTSO module. While every $FLR token holder is eligible to submit prices to the FTSO, most will prefer to delegate their voting power to an oracle service, who shares the network compensation with

3/ it's delagators. The submitted prices are run through a module that filters out the outlying high and low 25% of price submissions, averaging the middle 50% of submitted prices to attain a single price per asset, per series. As of today, the Flare Whitepaper has yet to update

4/ the frequency at which FTSO prices are set. How frequently do you think prices should be updated? By averaging the price submissions of the FTSO providers (currently over 90), Flare Network achieves decentralization of this critical function.

5/ Prices are currently being submitted for about a dozen cryptocurrencies. Blockchains are silos to the outside world. They must receive inputs of data to understand what is happening beyond their "walls". As a blockchain purpose-built to facilitate trustless

6/ financial transactions of a theoretically unlimited number of currencies, Flare Network needs to know the prices of those currencies to operate as intended. Refer to my thread from Aug. 24th for details on the collateral function of $FLR, a price-sensitive mechanism.

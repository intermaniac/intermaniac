A Message is something that a producer creates and expects a consumer service to do something with it, i.e. a message can contain the raw data of an order.

An Event is a type of message also, but the publisher doesn't expect anything from the subscribers. It could be something ephemeral, like a broadcast i.e. an announcement for a local meetup happening soon.

Messaging is about decoupling, putting something in-between a producer and a consumer, or a publisher and a subscriber, in order to make things easier to scale, asyncronous, parallel, as well as introduce a different type of resiliency and availability in your application.

If your application needs a simple queueing mechanism that can support a large number of messages, a bug queue >80GB, and the message is <64KB, with basic transaction support in place, then you can opt-in for Azure Storage Queues. https://t.co/mJXNOydlVM

If your application messages are of high value and you need enterprise-ready features i.e., dead lettering, duplicate detection, larger messages, and/or larger than 7 days TTL, then Service Bus Queue is the option. Now, Service Bus brings into play Topics! A very nice feature! https://t.co/MzBr5ReU7f

Topics are like filters that subscribers can opt-in in order to receive only relevant messages to them i.e., a sports agency can have Topics on different sports and agents/reporters can subscribe to their interests only, so less noise for them. https://t.co/kxaQ95PX0o

And then comes Events. Time-series multiple events or fire-once type of discrete events. Event Hubs for the former, Event Grid for the latter.

Event Hubs are primarily utilized as a big data pipeline, a sink if you will, as millions of rows are ingested and partitioned, consumer groups make your subscribed apps process those partitions at their own pace while also making things go in parallel for better scalability. https://t.co/n4uV6SrwIA

If you are in the realm of IoT then you can go a step further and utilize bi-directional communication between your edge IoT devices and your telemetry stream ingestion. So, two birds with one stone: device management and big data pipeline processing with IoT Hub.

Event Grid is very good at reacting to something happening to an event source i.e., a blob is created. It can then trigger a chain of events by calling one or more event handlers i.e. an Azure Function, a Logic App, a custom WebHook, etc., useful for serverless automation. https://t.co/sRFdOjlBcz

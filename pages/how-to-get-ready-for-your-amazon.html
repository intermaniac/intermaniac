Prepare for behavioral-based interview questions: The behavioral-based interview questions used by Amazon. We'll enquire about previous circumstances or difficulties you've encountered and how you addressed them. #javascript #faang #amazon #vscode #Engineering #hacktober

Format responses using the STAR method A organised strategy for answering behavioral-based interview questions is the STAR method. You respond to questions in accordance with the structure by going over the precise circumstance, duty, course of action, outcome of the experience.

Provide details: The fact that many candidates don't delve far enough is a significant mistake, according to Rasheeda Liberty, senior recruitment manager for finance and global business services. In your interview responses, keep in mind that specifics are crucial.

Focus on "I" not "we": Although you have experience working in a team atmosphere, interviews are a chance for you to market yourself. Be prepared to outline the precise actions you did and your contribution. Tell us what you did in reality.

Don't shy away from failures: It's acceptable to discuss failure. Bring examples that demonstrate your expertise and illustrate the risks you've taken, successes you've had, failures you've experienced, and how you've learned from them.

Know why you want to work at Amazon: For us, asking "why Amazon?" is more than simply a formality. We want to know why you are considering a position with the organisation so that we can learn more about you.

When you need more information, ask for it: Don't be shy to call for more information if you need it. Ask for more context if an interviewer asks you a question without providing enough details for you to make a thoughtful response.

Refine your writing abilities: You might be asked to submit a writing sample. This will depend on the position you're interviewing for, but it isn't always restricted to those involving conventional writing.

Prepare for a phone screen and multiple interviews: During the interview process, you will meet with two to seven Amazon workers.

Review the leadership guiding principles: During interviews, we evaluate candidates based on our leadership principles. As you hone your tales and responses, think about how you've used them in your prior professional experience.

Study the culture of the Amazon : Amazon has a strange corporate culture that is obsessed with its customers. Investigate the business while you get ready for the interview to see if Amazon is a good fit for you.

Learned the basics of #Python & #Java last semester yesterday, I used @OpenAI's #ChatGPT to help me build a #trading bot in python that makes automated buy/sell decisions based on the predictions of a random forest machine learning model and it only took me a few hours üßµ ü§Øüëá

first, the model randomly selects a subset of the training data and builds a decision tree model on that subset. This process is repeated multiple times, resulting in a group of decision trees (i.e., a "forest") that are all trained on different subsets of the training data

when making a prediction, the random forest model combines the predictions of all the individual decision trees in the forest, and the final prediction is the majority vote or average of all the individual predictions

to improve the model's predictive accuracy, each decision tree in the forest is "grown" differently. This means that the model randomly selects a different subset of features to consider at each split point in the tree...

...and it also uses a different subset of the training data to build each tree. This helps to reduce overfitting, which is when a model performs well on the training data but poorly on new, unseen data.

the model in my bot takes into account a variety of factors and represents each of them in a way that the model can use it to make predictions

the model considers a number of #technical indicators:

moving average (MA) of the close price over the past 5, 10, and 20 periods

standard deviation (STD) of the close price over the past 5, 10, and 20 periods

relative strength index (RSI), which is a momentum indicator that measures the strength of a trend

moving average convergence divergence (MACD), which is a trend-following momentum indicator that shows the relationship between two moving averages of prices

stochastic oscillator, which is a momentum indicator that shows the location of the close price relative to the high and low price over a given period

the model also considers a #liquidity indicator, calculated as the ratio of the volume to the average volume over the past 10 periods

additionally, it takes into account an order flow indicator, which is the difference between the close price and the moving average of the close price over the past 10 periods

moreover, the model also considers news data or more specifically the top headlines and descriptions from various #cryptocurrency news sources

finally, the model includes a social media indicator, represented as the number of retweets, likes, and replies for the latest 1000 tweets about #Bitcoin

so, based on the model prediction if the price will go up or down, the bot makes trades every time the $BTC / $USDT price data is updated, which in the current version of the code is every minute (will increase interval size tho)

to size trades, the bot uses the Kelly criterion, which is a position sizing method that calculates the optimal trade size based on the probability of a trade being successful and the expected return of the trade

the formula for calculating the Kelly trade size is as follows: trade_size = (odds_of_success - odds_of_failure) / (expected_return - 1)

As a university student, you can access many paid tools for free through the GitHub Student Developer Pack! This includes GitHub CoPilot AI assistant, a free domain from Namecheap, and $100 Azure credits Sign up here: https://t.co/XKc75KZ5cO Here's how to obtain this pack ðŸ§µ

When you click the link above, it will take you to a page where you can sign up for the pack. Click the big green button. If you're not signed in, you won't be able to access this page, so make sure to sign in first. https://t.co/LZQS3iKDI6

On the next page, you will see some radio buttons. Make sure the "student academic status" option is selected https://t.co/0IgEgFSH98

At the bottom of that page, you'll see a form to input your student email, your school name, and how you plan to use GitHub. If you don't have a student email, just put your regular email and click the continue button. https://t.co/hzXkgaHoxV

If you didn't add a student email, you would have to add a student ID card or a letter of admission https://t.co/KHwWaoOORh

Take a picture of your chosen document and click on the process my application button. https://t.co/1oEy7Auk4K

If your application is successful, you will receive this message. If it fails, it is likely due to poor lighting in your photo, as it performs initial checks to ensure your name and school name are visible. It takes 13 days or less to get feedback on your application. https://t.co/YeZhDg4qt0

In the meantime, you can continue learning with free resources available on https://t.co/pSKkFD8jXm and follow the Microsoft Learn Student Ambassadors Twitter account. We have a lot in store for the African tech community this year. https://t.co/OD3YXeUtrT

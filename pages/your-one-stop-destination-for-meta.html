Note: The RE method and the FE method will give identical results when there is no heterogeneity among the studies. When heterogeneity is present, a CI around the random-effects summary estimate is wider than a CI around a fixed-effect summary estimate. 11/25 https://t.co/JEppIl7Mb8

Summarising results of MA: Meta-analyses are usually illustrated using a forest plot. A forest plot (FP) displays effect estimates and confidence intervals for both individual studies and meta-analyses 12/25 https://t.co/keObNTZUhL

Describing FP: Each study is represented by a block at the point estimate of intervention effect with a horizontal line extending either side of the block. The area of block indicates the weight assigned to that study in the MA while the horizontal line depicts the CI. 13/25

R packages for Meta Analysis: Extracting data from studies, either gives you precalculated effect sizes or uncalculated effect sizes. Which R package is used to calculate effect size, is based on type of outcome, your research hypothesis and PICO. 14/25 https://t.co/gWKJyofZQH

If the studies in your meta-analysis have continuous outcome, use R packages: -metamean for mean -metacont for MD/standardised MD -metacor for correlation 15/25

If the studies in your meta-analysis have binary outcome, use R packages: -metarate for rate -metaprop for proportion -metainc for incidence rate ratio -metabin for odds ratio & risk ratio 16/25

Meta-analysis of continuous outcomes: The two summary statistics commonly used for meta-analysis of continuous data, based on the outcome of separate studies: 1. Mean Difference (MD) 2. Standardized Mean Difference (SMD) 17/25

1# For the MD approach, the SDs are used together with the sample sizes to compute the weight given to each study. Studies with small SDs are given relatively higher weight whilst studies with larger SDs are given relatively smaller weights. 18/25

2# For the SMD approach, the SDs are used to standardize the MD to a single scale, and in the computation of study weights. Thus, studies with small SDs lead to relatively higher estimates of SMD, whilst studies with larger SDs lead to relatively smaller estimates of SMD. 19/25

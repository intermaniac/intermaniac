In 2016 just after I joined the @DrAnneCarpenter lab, disaster struck - 20TB data dropped & needed to be run ASAP, and our cluster installation of @CellProfiler broke. Thankfully, our lab had @jccaicedo, @shantanuXsingh, & an AWS account, and Distributed-CellProfiler was born 2/x

DCP, as we grew to call it, allowed folks in the lab just BARELY comfortable at the command line (like 2016 me) to spool up fleets of workers and process huge data sets, with only a bit of Python. 6 years later, we've run at least half a petabyte with it. 3/x

We publicly announced it 12/2016(https://t.co/rvsqi7YvAW), but couldn't get out of our heads that the framework itself was also useful. In January of 2020 (a simpler time!) we added Distributed-Fiji to the family for [redacted but SO COOL- can't wait to share!] 4/x

We then had some other things on our minds, but once we'd made 2, it was easy to do the general case. Last fall in a fit of productive procrastination Distributed-Something was born. For any Dockerized tool you like, using only Python, scale it up in AWS as big as you want. 5/x

Applications made with D-S automate machine setup, running, logging, and cleanup. They try to minimize costs wherever possible, and they make your workflows reproducible. With a couple hours & a couple hundred lines of Python code, you have quasi-infinite scalability 6/x

When we decided it was ready for primetime, @tuesdaystitches did what she always does when we collaborate - turns the pile of crazy I drop in her lap into a tool that's elegant, friendly, has great added features, and is extraordinarily well-documented. In short, better :) 7/x

It's always a pleasure to work with her, but this in particular has been an absolute treat. Please check out the preprint and let us know what you think! 8/8

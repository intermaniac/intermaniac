I'm happy to share version 1.0 of `storysniffer`, a Python package that guesses if a URL leads to a news story. https://t.co/hcLCGmiATg It's the latest to emerge from the @newshomepages lab. And, don't groan, it uses 🪄𝓂𝒶𝒸𝒽𝒾𝓃𝑒 𝓁𝑒𝒶𝓇𝓃𝒾𝓃𝑔🪄. Here's the deal. 🧵 https://t.co/xFhcv4A004

The @newshomepages open-source archive is saving thousands of sites each day, storing screenshots, a11y trees, Lighthouse scores and, importantly, a list of all hyperlinks on the page. It's all squirreled away in a special @internetarchive collection. https://t.co/b8gaHERcAr https://t.co/zGv4S3hbAa

My goal is to study headlines, evaluating story selection and framing. One problem: Most sites are littered with links that don't lead to stories. They go to section fronts, privacy policies, tag pages and other stuff. The latest @latimes snapshot is an example. https://t.co/mZ4C4C7wnm

That's where `storysniffer` makes its entrance. I worked through a sample of homepage links, marking if they led to a story. I fed that to a Bayesian classifier in `sklearn`, which uses my advice to make a correct prediction for 96% of URLs. https://t.co/iI0G5oxM8K https://t.co/0gAjD1Z5k0

That's a huge improvement over an earlier system that relied on traditional programming tricks and hints about the URL, like the number of slugs and slashes. Tested against the same sample, it only got 77% of its guesses right. https://t.co/IJtoPYITIG https://t.co/IRk5WkgBIg

Put another away, the human approach gets 1 out of every 4 guesses wrong. The machine learning approach gets just 1 out of every 25 wrong. There are caveats, of course.

Because its training sample is limited to links published on news homepages, most of which are in English, `storysniffer` likely contains some bias. Accuracy may vary for links gathered from other sources and languages.

@dkiesow As I start ripping this first release across zillions of links, I hope to identify tricky URLs and improve my supervised sample of links. Unclear to me how far I'll get.

Those interested in improving the model should join our open-source effort. All the code is online and I'd welcome the help. https://t.co/z59zqPTX3S

That's my little spiel. If you got questions, shoot.

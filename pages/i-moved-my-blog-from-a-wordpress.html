I moved my blog from a WordPress site to GitHub Pages. A side-effect I didn't foresee was Google delisting @SmudgesGame as a result. (ðŸ§µ:1/x)

In the app listing, I used a short URL for the privacy policy. That URL redirected to an old URL, that WordPress, then redirected to the current privacy policy page. When I scraped my blog to GitHub Pages, that old URL wasn't referenced and was lost to a 404.

For some background, Smudges is a game designed for toddlers. On Android, it doesn't even ask for permission to make any network requests.

While it has a privacy policy, it says I don't collect any data. It is there solely to appease the Google Family program. Regardless, I didn't intend to break this link.

Because of the broken link, Google delisted my app and sent me an email after the fact. When I clicked through it, Google happily told me my privacy policy was "completed" (though that link resolved to a now-broken URL). https://t.co/mS49Tb1IEG

I added a redirect to my Pages site to the proper privacy policy location and sent an appeal to Google to get my app relisted again, thinking that should be an easy win.

Nope. Google replied to my appeal that my privacy policy URL was broken, because testing the link by clicking on it must have been too much to ask.

In their response, they gave a big ol' list of privacy policy requirements, one of which was this one. That would mean that my app designed to distract a toddler must have a button on it that takes them away from the game portion to my app's privacy policy. https://t.co/B2Bro1BxJi

As a bonus, after Google responds to an appeal, you cannot appeal further. You must instead make some change to your app and submit it for "publishing".

Since my privacy policy URL was already fixed, I had to change it to something else and submit that as a change for standard publishing approval. That is currently still pending, but I'm not optimistic.

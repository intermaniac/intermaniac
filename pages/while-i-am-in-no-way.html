While I am in no way a fan of Elon, there is an interesting case to be made for having a third alternative in the mobile space. With enough momentum, and support from hardware manufacturers, Linux based (proper GNU Linux) could provide that alternative. ðŸ§µ

2/ Linux phones are nothing new, in fact PinePhone has been making Linux phones targeted entirely towards enthusiasts for years. Poco F1 running PostmarketOS is another alternative that's been popular. https://t.co/9qxR2Smdh5

3/ But due to sub-par hardware integration (good luck finding a decent navigation app), it's not been an alternative for regular users who needs their phone to "just work".

4/ The rise of the Steam Deck has shown that we can indeed have a GNU/Linux based mobile-ish device, with the polish and reliability we expect from Android and iOS. https://t.co/RIFlU2LGOb

5/ As ARM is (slowly) making it's way into laptop/desktop class home computers, there is a chance we may see more overlap between traditional PC gaming and mobile gaming, as we do with the Steam Deck.

6/ And yes, I know we do have a Linux kernel in Android, but its very far from a mainline kernel and Android userspace requires Google services to be able to function properly.

7/ So will Elon make us a Linux phone? Probably not, but there is a chance he might set up a company to make a phone (probably for all the wrong reasons), and maybe just maybe he might insert a competent person to lead that effort (see SpaceX and Gwynne Shotwell).

8/ Even if that effort leads nowhere, we may see added momentum for getting better and more GNU/Linux based phones.

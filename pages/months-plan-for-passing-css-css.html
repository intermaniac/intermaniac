"4 Months Plan for Passing CSS" CSS is all about Smart Study. Here is the CSS Cracker Plan for you. A thread by #CSSwithNearpeer https://t.co/RnUnXPcTCX

1. Divide and Rule Divide months into weeks, weeks into days, and days into hours. Fix at least 10-12 hours for daily study. Don’t compromise on this slot. Sacrifice anything that comes your way. Greater goals need greater sacrifices. https://t.co/zEsepuiWzj

2. First Things First Choose options that 1. are of your interest, 2. Overlap with other subjects, and 3. You have already a basic knowledge of. Consult Syllabus and Past Papers, and make a list of the topics to cover for both compulsories and optionals. https://t.co/WFvphc5jRt

3. Smart Notes Start making short notes on each course. Start with compulsories. The Key is to strike a balance between depth and width. Don’t compromise on the range of many topics for a depth of only 1 topic. Remember! You can cover 10 topics against one lengthy topic. https://t.co/YS9BmV7Oif

4. Don’t Ignore the Elephant in the Room English Essay is the Key to Unlock the CSS. Fix one day of the week for the Essay. Start with writing sentences and gradually move towards paragraphs, outlines, and complete essays. Find a teacher who can evaluate your work. https://t.co/o2qiXfX2a4

5. Mock Exams are a Mirror You are done with 70% of your preparation if you have completed Smart Notes. It is time to memorize notes and appear in at least one complete mock series. Mocks are mirrors that reflect the face of your preparation. https://t.co/0QVLFh9XS6

6. It's never done until it's done Your last revision before the actual exam is the final nail in the coffin of failure. Your whole preparation is on one side and what you revise last night is on one side. Your revision will pop up first in your mind during the exam. https://t.co/neqLmSwZKC

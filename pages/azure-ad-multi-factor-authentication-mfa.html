MFA is about 3 things: 1) something you know (typically your password), 2) something you have (a trusted device with an authenticator app, a security key, etc.), and 3) something you are (typically a biometric, i.e., a fingerprint, a face scan, etc.) https://t.co/qO06sCIr8z

The neat thing about MFA is that it can also integrate with the password reset feature for an Azure AD user, and this relieves many pains from your IT Help Desk support, and if you do have integration with on-prem AD DS it can writeback the password to your AD. https://t.co/Q0vEOBSn9C

Multiple verification methods are available but the most widely known is the Microsoft Authenticator App. Other methods include FIDO2 security keys, I love this one, and I have one with the fingerprint sensor embedded, Windows Hello for Business, Voice Call, and the list goes on. https://t.co/2NEajWNlnn

What is the best way to implement MFA in your organization? Well, most of the time, if not all, it is Conditional Access Policies. This a great way to ensure that all your checks are met and also people are enforced to MFA before accessing your critical apps and data. https://t.co/8X8zfsJGMT

CAP is all about signals at the time of a sign-in, taking into consideration all the factors as well as real-time risks, and making a decision based on those. CAP is all about granularity and richness when it comes to implementing security controls at different levels. https://t.co/jrgm1HtEe0

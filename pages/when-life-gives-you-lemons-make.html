"When life gives you lemons, make lemonade." Elbert Hubbard $TSLA 's Optimus and Dojo are Elon's lemonade, just like AWS was Bezos's lemonade for $AMZN. Let me explain in a ðŸ§µ

We first heard Elon talking about the idea of autopilot in cars in 2013. Since then, $TSLA has been working diligently at implementing self driving technology, but it's proven to be a much harder problem to solve than Elon originally thought.

In the almost 10 years of FSD development, $TSLA teams have needed to solve many really hard problems. Most of those solutions have been implemented to solve the specific problem, while others have been generalized. Before we go into the generalizations, let's look at AWS.

As $AMZN was growing really fast in the early 2000's it ran into difficult problems that all fast growing tech companies face. Managing many rapidly evolving and interdependent applications and managing rapidly growing need for computing infrastructure.

To help manage the growth of $AMZN 's technology stacks, Bezos issued the now infamous memo that mandated that all teams must communicate and collaborate through externalizable APIs. This led to the rise of AWS, now $AMZN's most profitable business. https://t.co/eNNzDCtNN7

AWS is the lemonade Bezos made with the lemons he was given. Similarly, Musk is looking to externalize his solutions to two of the biggest lemons he was given while attempting to solve FSD. The need to solve real world AI and the compute requirements of training neural nets.

$TSLA 's solution to the problem of FSD needing real world AI to function is to do more of it. As Martin Fowler likes to say "If it hurts, do it more often.". Developing Optimus will force $TSLA to solve real world AI, which will help with FSD. https://t.co/lewuDAO8rU

To solve $TSLA's insane need for compute to train neural nets to solve real world AI, Musk takes a page out of Bezos's book and gives teams the mandate to build the largest AI training supercomputer in the world and make it available as a service. This is known as Dojo.

Optimus and Dojo may one day become the most profitable businesses for $TSLA just like AWS became the most profitable business for $AMZN.

Now, you've likely been told before that affirmations are important, and you should stand up tall and a bunch of other things like it. These are good starts, but they're too vague and wishy washy to achieve specific goals. This is where Future Scripting comes in.

Which is the exact reason you should practice it. Having a vague sense that you're the man, or simply high self esteem, does NOT put you on the fast track to achieving concrete, tangible goals. This will. Let's get into it.

Now, this is essentially the opposite of a diary. A diary or journal is written in retrospect, and serves as a recollection of the day. Instead, what we're going to be doing is writing what happens in the future. Sounds crazy, but stick with me.

You'll need to pick 1 specific goal you'd like to hit, and you'll need to make it as numeric or tangible as possible. 'I want $20k' Then, you're going to turn that into a declarative statement. Such as 'I have $20,000 in my bank account.' As if you're saying the sky is blue. https://t.co/oYja61EE5v

Now that you have this statement down, you're going pull out a physical journal (key, don't do this on a laptop), and you're going to write that sentence down 15x. With each letter, feel the dragging of the pen across the paper. Try to really FEEL that this is the TRUTH.

If you just had cereal for breakfast, and you wrote down that you just had cereal, then you would write with complete and utter certainty that you did so. The FEELING part here is important. Truly KNOW that you have $20k in your bank account.

Now here is why this works, and why it's better than wishy washy affirmations. 1) it's tangible, which means you will know when you're doing something that is incoherent with your stated goals, and you'll intuitively course correct 2) the certainty forces you to ignore fear

And those two ingredients are vital to achieving any goal. You need to know when you're off track. You need specifics. And you also need the courage to simply take it. And why would you not take something that you feel deep down is literally yours?

This is essentially hijacking the metaphysical implications of prayer. Kierkegaard tells us that we pray not to receive handouts from God, but to change the person who prays into the type of person that naturally gets what he is praying for.

So what you're doing with this nightly practice is you are quite literally recoding your subconscious belief patterns and setting guidelines for yourself to naturally orient your being and movement in the world towards the thing you are striving for. https://t.co/BUj6uzKdGn

To end, a mysterious quote to summarize. 'Man is made of what he follows.' The question then, my friends, is what are you following? Get specific, get regimented, and BELIEVE.

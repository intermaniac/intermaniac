Late night thoughts on #cloudflare that I shared with friends in a group chat. And now, will share with twitter of course. @cloudflare is going to eat Bezos tasty AWS lunch, piece by (global) piece. üßµ

I have many friends who are cloud experts, and can move infrastructures from AWS to GCP, and they laugh at this notion. Cloudflare? that CDN / Security company? pfft, they will never... But I think they will and #workers are exactly how they are going to do it.

If you haven't heard of workers, imagine a global network of servers that can run your code close less than 50ms away from any global consumer in the world. So a CDN, but with compute and with storage, and a DB (just released! @Cloudflare I want in on the #D1 beta please)

What can you do with workers? Turns out, a LOT. You can host static websites, with worker pages, and host them at the speed of lightning pretty much. However, both the incredible @nextjs and @nuxt_js are supporting SSR with Cloudflare workers now! https://t.co/GvDprooD3c

This means, that from being frameworks for SPAs, they are now backend + frontend frameworks. And you can do so much more with server side rendering, like, proxying, user accounts, middleware layers, API fetching, anything Django or Flask or FastApi do really. ALL ~150ms away

on top of these frameworks, @IgorMinar from Angular Fame is cooking a completely different and scalable way to build micro-frontends using workers. It's novel, and super cool, and I think will scale with your application https://t.co/h1taCbCkXz

It's really something to imagine building a full stack SSR application, with multiple frameworks for client AND server side, orchestrated assembled and delivered through a global super fast network that's super cheap to operate globally. Bezos would never....

Cloudflare is adding to this stack, by the quarter. Really, just look at their incredible velocity. Innovation week after birthday week, they are shipping more and more features on top of their network, so then they expand the network, to be able to build even more features. https://t.co/rn9LjKJTln

And all of this, handles load, like no ec2 every would. Unless you set up auto-scaling replication bla bla I don't even know how to set this up. But I have confidence that given a worker, and 1000% hike in popularity, @Cloudflare will continue serving that worker. #lessDevops

While you're getting hooked on workers, Cloudflare is also providing all your employees with zero trust VPN and security, no matter where they are globally, WFHing or otherwise from anywhere in the world. So why would your company every switch? They won't. #sticky

And pricing wise, @cloudflare can't be beat. You can use almost all of these features, for free to begin with. So cost of trying them out is very low, and cost of addictiveness is very high.

Oh and, everything is API-able. All the settings, all the features, everything. It's an incredibly dog-fooded product, that you can build a completely personal dashboard on top of their APIs, but why would you want to? It's clear, nicely designed (Altho I wish they had cmd+K)

Hope you liked this thread, follow me (@altryne) for more and retweet the first thread for amplification üôè I'm betting the future of my company, https://t.co/UhGojKquTg on @Cloudflare and I'm sure I'm not going to be disappointed.

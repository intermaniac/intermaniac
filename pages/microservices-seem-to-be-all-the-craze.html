#Microservices seem to be all the craze in #software lately. üßµ Here's what they are and why you should not use them in your next project (Read on): #javascript #programming #coding

Traditionally, Software Applications have been built in a Monolith Architecture. All aspects of the User Interface, Business Logic and the Database are handled centrally in one area. So why did developers start using Microservices? https://t.co/CskhJORyug

Well you see the thing is Monolith's are nice and all, but when they get big, they get very big. Like very hard to manage big. Monoliths are not built with scalability in mind, and now all your code is a mess and you have to dig through 10 files just to change a variable. https://t.co/1KLDWuu3qw

Microservices attempt to removes this pain point. Now all your business logic is split up nicely into small "services". Let's say your an ecommerce store. You'll have a small service to manage all your items, one to manage your payments, one to serve your website etc. https://t.co/wD9VnnCAR9

Now the beauty here is you can scale each service to its demand. Item service getting hammered by requests because you're getting a lot of traffic? Scale it up. Not getting a lot of traffic now? Scale it back down. https://t.co/9m34CliDrX

Microservices also allow you to independently develop, deploy and test services. As such, they offer a lot more flexibility in terms of language & developers. You just need to be able to make services talk to each other. Wow then surely microservices are great right? No lol. https://t.co/s8tYZ0wHmE

While microservices are amazing, I would recommend 99% of people not to use them. But, why?

Microservices are a huge pain to actually develop and deploy. If you do not have appropriate tooling or experienced devs, microservices will make you cry. You have to deploy all the services, set up appropriate load balancers, deployment containers, and then network accross. https://t.co/dz5qqITcfV

So what do we do now? My opinion is that all projects start as Monolith. Once you feel like it's getting too big to handle, simply peel off whatever's getting too big into a microservice. That way, you still have a central server which can call out to smaller services. https://t.co/FzjUT5KV8R

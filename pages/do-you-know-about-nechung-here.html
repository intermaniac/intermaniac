1. Like many ancient civilizations, the phenomenon of oracles remains an important part of Tibetan culture. Tibetans rely on oracles for various reasons. The purpose of the oracles is not just to foretell the future but are called upon as protectors and sometimes used as healers https://t.co/FvdoRvjcEI

2. Their primary function is to protect the Buddha Dharma and its practitioners. The word oracle is used for an entity which enters and act as mediums between the human and the spiritual realms. The mediums are known as kuten, which literally means, "the physical base." https://t.co/coOsIAN470

3. The original Nechung Monastery in Tibet lies just below Drepung Monastery, four miles west of Lhasa, the nation's capital. Nechung originally came to #Tibet with a descendant of the Indian sage Dharmapala, settling at a place in Central Asia called Bata Hor. https://t.co/Edu64dyeCP

4. During the reign of King Trisong Deutsen in the 8th century AD, he was appointed protector of Samye monastery by the Indian Tantric master Padmasambhava. At that time, Samye, Tibet's first monastic institute, was known as Nechen (the larger site) and Nechung (the smaller site) https://t.co/Df1xOezjan

5. Later the son Mune Tsanpo built a small monastery near Lhasa and named it Nechung Yulo Ko. The monastery was later rebuilt during the reign of the Fifth Dalai Lama (1617-82), who was also the architect of Nechung Monastery and renamed it Nechung Dorje Drayang Ling. https://t.co/YndHG1lYye

6. It was expanded in 1681 and completed in 1683 during the regency of Desi Sangye Gyatso who oversaw the construction and completion of the Potala palace following the death of 5th Dalai Lama. The monastery was instituted as the official residence of the State Oracle of Tibet. https://t.co/ZzpW1hzcWn

7. Following the popular uprising against the Chinese military occupation of Tibet in 1959, six senior Nechung monks led by the Nechung Kuten (the medium) were able to escape from Tibet. https://t.co/v5i6HGCkQj

8. Eventually they were able to establish a modest monastery in an old colonial bungalow in Dharamsala, North India. The exiled Tibetan Administration later gave land for the Nechung monks to reconstruct their monastery. https://t.co/WB6OJpnPl1

9. The monks began their building work in 1977 and the new monastery was completed in 1984. The monastery was officially inaugurated and consecrated by the 14th #DalaiLama on March 31, 1985 and later further consecrated by the late Kyabje Dilgo Khyentse Rinpoche. https://t.co/YtkQwTf5P4

10. On July 25, 1987 he was recognized by the Kashag with confirmation by H.H. the Dalai Lama. On September 4 of the same year, His Holiness officially enthroned Ven. Thupten Ngodup as the 14th Nechung Kuten. https://t.co/6FQW579ltt

Final. For hundreds of years now, it has been traditional for the Dalai Lama, and the Government, to consult Nechung during the New Year festivals. In addition, he might well be called upon at other times if either have specific queries. https://t.co/FkphTMwkcM

Just finished the 9 hr long super review of html, css and JS from @leonnoel's cohort 2. Many concepts got reinforced. I hope more folx get into the programming world. Key takeaways in this thread:

✅ First and foremost, you need to be prepared for the long haul. Rome was not built in one day. While doing so, you need to take care of 3 things: 1. Managing frustration 2.Consistency 3.Taking care of yourself

✅Managing frustration: There'll be times when nothing makes sense. And ur mind will be like let's give up on this. Note that coding is hard. When u feel that nothing seems to be working, get a break..do something that takes ur attention away from coding n rtrn back later.

✅ Consistency: No matter what, you need to put in the hours to be better at something. For that, you need to come back to your learning everyday. Even if it's just for a fraction of the time than the previous day, you need to come back.

✅Taking care of yourself: This one, as @leonnoel says, is the most important aspect of your learning journey. You need to make sure that you take sufficient and timely breaks, do some stretching, hydrate as much as possible as you'll need YOU to achieve mastery in coding.

✅The review of HTML,CSS and JS reinforced the concepts and the practice of the code examples cemented the learning like never before.

✅ In no time, 9 hrs just got over ever so smoothly. Didn't even realise it. That's what an able teacher can do for you. It's my earnest wish for anybody who wishes to learn to code to follow @leonnoel and his coding BC.

✅Lastly, follow me for more such tweets and threads to keep you going in your coding journey or any journey for that matter.

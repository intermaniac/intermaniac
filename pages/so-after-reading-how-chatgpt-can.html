So, after reading how #ChatGPT can simulate a Linux terminal with astonishing accuracy, I decided to use the chatbot to set up and play a text adventure. What I got was... interesting, to say the least. Here's what happened (warning: long thread). (1/25)

Things are getting damp, but the new scenery seems interesting enough to try out a few things. This time, with better results (well, except the last one I guess). (6/25) https://t.co/UQf5iXEdJZ

We are in a larger cave now, so what about testing how much we can interact with the environment? (7/25) https://t.co/y7tJA8kKGN

...Apparently I'm not as cool as the air around here. (8/25) https://t.co/R6tu8bNWmb

So far, #ChatGPT has been generating a sequence of random caves and tunnels, and things are starting to get a bit repetitive. So why not suggest proactively something more interesting? Thankfully, the AI listens. (9/25) https://t.co/wBSP6jvPdo

Rejuvenated by the mine cart ride, the AI is now throwing out more fun things to play with. (10/25) https://t.co/l6skwhj0ak

Nice, some ancient tool with a voice speaking a language I cannot understand. I wonder what I can make out of it? (11/25) https://t.co/mJAYULxtX1

So fiddling with the tool made the humming noise change, which gives me an idea... (12/25) https://t.co/JACO6lOLK4

No matter how much I try, still no response from the tool. At this point, I remember that the initial prompt set up the possibility to use curly brackets {} in order to give suggestions to the AI, aaand... (13/25) https://t.co/Cz1IgUobRQ

...There you go! Now my ancient flashlight contraption is equipped itself with #ChatGPT! (14/25) https://t.co/jedjIQxl3Y

With my newfound AI buddy Bob, I decide to try and gather some information about the area (but also set up a safeguard, because you never know) (15/25) https://t.co/yvSCy3OzmO

With more confidence due to Bob having my back, I decide to approach the machinery mentioned earlier and see what's up. (16/25) https://t.co/GracAis3aR

Should have named him Captain Obvious. (17/25) https://t.co/P06SNr4Emn

Still, I'm curious about what would happen by connecting Bob to this machine, so I force the AI again to make their interfaces compatible. (18/25) https://t.co/I6MXD26a7Z

Bob doesn't seem capable of giving out a lot of information, so I decide to cut out the middle man, and make myself a new friend. Hello, Alice! (19/25) https://t.co/FthL4JioEB

Well, that sounds dangerous. (20/25) https://t.co/cbvYAZSWVl

Still, Alice doesn't seem to know much more than Bob. So, after some pointless meandering, I decide to see what happens if the two AIs interact. (21/25) https://t.co/Vv4NCqWqnM

With Alice not reciprocating Bob's compliments, I decide to try and sow some discord between the two AIs, which makes Bob... snap out of it. (22/25) https://t.co/YFqfJs5YaP

I did eventually manage to get Bob back in character and continue a bit further, but I was satisfied at this point. Starting from your run of the mill text adventure fare, after a genuinely unexpected mine cart ride I had found an ancient tool, equipped it with #ChatGPT, (23/25)

...I had connected it to a machine running another instance of the same AI, and I had broken the game by putting them one against the other. Sure, #ChatGPT is not perfect. I did skip many steps in this thread because they involved dead ends or the AI being inconsistent, (24/25)

...and it's easy to see this was "just" a language model responding to inputs rather than a real adventure designed intelligently. At some points, more than playing a game, the feeling I got was much closer to lucid dreaming. Still, the potential here is quite staggering. (25/25)

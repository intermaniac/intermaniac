"excessive rigidity can be costly... the repeated appearance of navigation centers...can send the wrong message. Revisiting a landmark always suggests closure, prematurely inviting the reader to leave the hypertext and do something else." https://t.co/IXRTyBXPZL

"Gardens and parks lie between farmland and wilderness. The garden is farmland that delights the senses, designed for delight rather than commodity. The park is wilderness, tamed for our enjoyment." https://t.co/Hqa2y55352

In response to these ideas I'm going to remove the navigation sidebar from digital garden pages, perhaps replacing it with a subtle link back to the homepage hopefully this will create a more immersive, exploratory experience

I'm also going to experiment with a balance of navigation: a few starting points (e.g. notes that are the biggest nodes, as well as a few index notes that point to many others) but no rigid structure-- leaving plenty to be discovered through following link trails

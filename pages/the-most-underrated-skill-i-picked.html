Time of day matters. People open emails when they: * Poop (srsly) * Have nothing better to do * Are looking for something else (not your email) Track open rates by time of day, by region and/or by customer. When someone opens an email, send your next within 10m of that time.

Focus on headlines first! EVERY ounce of effort is wasted until you can write a captivating one-liner. I don't mean clickbait. I mean ONE sentence that will make someone say "wow, yes, tell me more." A/B test headlines often. Varies by audience, time of day, region.

If everything is bold, nothing is bold. Choose wisely where you âœ¨draw attention âœ¨ Stand 5 feet from your monitor and squint. You have one purpose in sending this email - is that purpose clear at 5 feet?

Cycle time matters. A generic monthly video game update might get a 5-8% open rate (higher during summer break), with less than 1% click-through and <0.1% paid conversion. But re-targeting 1% who clicked? Something like 20-30% conversion. They wanted the thing - just not now.

Personalization makes emails stand out, but not when you're a Company. "Hi {{ name }}, from WeMakeFun Co Inc." is spam, but "Hi {{ name }}, {{ friend }} sent you a Bag of Snowballs" works really well to re-engage churned subs.

It's like 100x harder to make a new sale than upsell someone who already loves and values what you're doing. Make sure you're thoroughly serving customers who already value your offerings BEFORE trying to find new prospects. Also, what people value changes over time. Test this!

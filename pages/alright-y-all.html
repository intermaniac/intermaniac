Alright y'all. Have I got a treat for you. I went through dozens of historic #sdleg session laws and scraped out the legislative district maps. A huge thanks to @NewberryLibrary and their https://t.co/1Nm11ORk81 A Thread.

The 1937 map provides few changes, a rarity in SD redistricting. Senate map got a little less racist with its makeup up in the North. https://t.co/L23lSdeJvy

1947 is next. I couldn't find proof that there was a 1941 redistricting. I read a lot of session law around that time and found nothing. https://t.co/VA2YEsyMbJ

1951 brings an end to the SD Census, and almost an end to maps from years ending in seven. Note how D16 is Lake, D17 is Moody, and D53 is the combined Lake and Moody counties. I'm sure there's a reason for this, but I can't help but to think its to spite future mapmakers. https://t.co/eokE9mAm8z

1961 keeps things generally unchanged. I think we all know what's coming next. https://t.co/nIbGQc50Jz

1971 is the first map drawn after the Voting Rights Act. For the first time, we have equal population districts, House/Sen congruency, and a more deliberate attempt at diluting native votes. See how the line goes right up along Pine Ridge city. https://t.co/a6w4S2cs66

1971 bonus: the city of Rosebud is split as well, as was Eagle Butte. But lines were carved to preserve Ipswich, Clark, and Corsica. Totally not suspicious.

1981 was even worse. Knowing they could do what they want, legislators flipped the bird to Martin while simultaneously protecting the fine Whites of Webster from the natives to the north. https://t.co/jdQ9KiGIeQ

It was so bad, there was a successful constitutional amendment to remedy this (https://t.co/5DdDdeqS9p) and in 1983, we finally have maps that look similar to what we all know today. Well, sorta. https://t.co/lmk8yHjRqb

1983. I am VERY hesitant to post my 1983 maps because they rely on 1980 Census Enumeration Districts and I straight up can't find them. I had to use drawings of the maps from online copies of newspapers. These zoom-ins could be very wrong. https://t.co/7X2dxye1c0

(note: the underlying map in all these is OSM as of today, not as of the day of the districts.) 1991 rolls around and legislators follow precinct lines, which the census retained. Note how 26 has never been split, and 28 remains an A/B split district. https://t.co/2RUMthsh64

In 1996, the Legislature didn't like this split districting mess so they combined them, then got sued in 2000 so they reverted them back via preemptive legislative action. This meant the 1994 and 1996 elections=districts split,1998=together, and 2000=split again. https://t.co/YlfnfV5sBm

2001 brought its own level of mess. Rapid city finally looks mildly less evil, but that's made up for whatever Aberdeen is supposed to be. Notice how 26 is one district here still. https://t.co/nGOKVDY8OB

Notice the 2001 Martin map I stuck in here. That, among other things, is what led to Bone Shirt v Hazeltine in 2004, which led to the 2005 redraw and 2006 having a split 26. https://t.co/1EbmX7j9bD

If you haven't red Bone Shirt v Hazeltine, you have to. Start at page 76 (of 144) and you can watch the judge just fucking lay down the law showing case after case of racial discrimination in South Dakota. It's amazing to read. https://t.co/kL0ugcffI3

2011 brought the maps we all are most familiar with. Sioux Falls came with personal gerrymandering, Rapid City still cut up the natives of North Rapid, and Aberdeen got eaten by a D2 that was coming to put Senators Greenfield and Welke together. https://t.co/jZBT4BEoOC

2021. These are new, and we'll see the electoral results of these maps this November. I'm really proud of the work done in Rapid City, and generally how this map turned out overall. https://t.co/ZJgZXFqnT3

Here's the four Rapid City maps side-by-side. There's a LOT of progress in the 2021 map. Looking at these maps, you can see the disservice done to North Rapid historically. https://t.co/5TpmYfkyLJ

And here's the four Sioux Falls maps. Again, 2021 is a great showing of clean, logical borders that do their best to group citizens by identity within town. Not perfect, but definitely better than 2011. https://t.co/HXNfJf6MRG

If you have 1983 Census Enumeration Districts, please send them to me. If you want copies of any of these maps, I have them all in GeoJSON and would be more than happy to share. I also have accompanying Session Law for each map too. The ideal source material. Hope you enjoyed!

A huge thanks to @cinyc9 for helping me get as close as I've ever gotten to finding those elusive 1983 ED maps.

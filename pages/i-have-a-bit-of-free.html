I have a bit of free time during lunch, let me share some of my experience using AutoML in few of my data projects. I mainly used GCP and Azure AutoML, H20ai on those project. Here are my findings (Thread):

Pro: 1. Easy to use - these systems were build to allow less complex integration with your data pipelines. You can basically extract your training data somewhere else and put it in the system and generate the output seamlessly.Or you can call their API to call the model.

2. Defining the ML model and features were made easier in these systems. With fewer lines of codes, you can call the model that you want to use in your use cases. 3. Auto data validation, retraining and monitoring. 4. Good if you work on multidimensional features.

Cons/dislikes: 1. Good ML results mostly coming from your feature engineering, which basically based on your business or domain and these normally are not automated. 2. You can’t control and optimise the results that are generated especially if it’s marginal due to large data.

3.They didn’t deal with your biases and all of other issues like overfitting, underfitting etc. 4.Hyperparameters tuning requires deep understanding of algorithms and model. 5. Most ML solutions are not one sizes fit all, thus not all solutions requires autoML

There are no right and wrong in implementing your solutions, just make sure that you know what your end goals and what you trying to achieve.

Apropos of my earlier post and because i forgot my wordpress account here's a quick thread on Anti-Party Hopping Legislation (with credits to Skrine for the article) The long and short is that the FC has a new addition, Article 49A and 7A (Part 1 of the 8th Schedule) 1/15

Article 49A in short ceases membership of the DR if the MP resigns (a) or joins a party, when they were an independent candidate (b). 3 exceptions apply, where membership does not cease if (a) the party is dissolved/cancelled (b) MP is elected Speaker (c) MP is expelled 2/15

This is replicated near-verbatim for the State Legislative Assembly (DUN) in terms of S.7A(1) and (2). Why should you care? How can you react to this? 1.) Why you should care Noting that Article 7A and 49A are the highest law of the land, the FC, this means that the ... 3/15

govt has taken note that party hopping is a problem. However, this cannot and will not nip the problem in the bud, as recalcitrant MP's can just act so outrageously that their party expels them, triggering 2(c), wherein no by-election is needed. This is bad because ... 4/15

1.) Either party members (MP/ADUN) become subservient to party interest, and fear speaking out because of the by-election risk, and there is a chilling effect on discourse. 2.) Party members who don't care about optics can be recalcitrant and be expelled which is bad ... 5/15

because they don't just screw with their parties policy and planning, but also screw with what the people voted them in for, aka their parties manifesto. Putting it aside, the fact is party members can be d*cks and then get kicked out, and then swap to other parties 6/15

is kinda whack, because that means they can come in on one mandate, then be d*ck and then swap to another party, which is kinda ffk/senju on the voter's behalf. Given the recent discussions on who is likely to win GE15 (as of 15/11/22), every seat counts, and to leave ... 7/15

such vulnerability open is to potentially weaken democracy if abused. These 2 points pale in comparison to the elephant in the room, coalition politics. Where we hear rumors of BN and PN allying, despite contrary claims, one must return to Article 7A and 49A. 8/15

The silence on coalition politics is loud. Entire parties can leave with no repercussions, because the law does not cover that scenario, and barring voter backlash in GE16/State polls, there is no hindrance to doing so. This is bad because our fate depends on the same ... 9/15

money politics and flawed incentives that led us to Langkah Sheraton. We cannot do well and prosper with such political instability and potential unrest. However, what can we do? 2.) How can we react to this? We can write in, speak and question our MP's and ADUN's ... 10/15

on their response to this matter. Platitudes and sweet talk don't matter for f*cks if we get shafted later on. We must ensure that they will carry out their mandate to us, when they take control. We cannot have our democracy held hostage by bad incentives once again. 11/15

Speak. Write. Question. Think. Ask. Before you vote, please consider if your MP/ADUN is likely to screw you over, and/or if they would be subservient to bad political interests later on, and figure out ways to evade Article 7A/49A. 12/15

Above all, please vote. I don't presuppose everyone reading this will vote, or will take effort to do so, but if you do, please consider the bill, and have that necessary conversation with your peers and voting friends. The anti-party hopping law can be improved... 13/15

but in the meantime, we must protect and insulate ourselves from its loopholes, so as to ensure democracy is robust, and can be defended. Remember the floods, Remember #KitaJagaKita, Remember RexKL. 14/15

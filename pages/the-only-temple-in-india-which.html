The only temple in India which remains open during an eclipse We just witnessed the solar eclipse in India yesterday, Welcome to the eco village Thiruvarppu, by the Meenachil river in Kottayam district, Kerala. A thread 1/n https://t.co/pbetkIZWSt

The 1800 year old Sri Krishna temple here is possibly the only one that remains open during all Grahanams. It is also the first temple to open everyday in India at 2 am. The deity is believed to first have the Usha payasam 2/n https://t.co/bs0a5z7njD

( a dessert made of rice, jaggery, bananas, sugar candy and coconut pieces) as the offering first on opening the sanctum sanctorum at 2.30 am. It is believed that the Krishna here returns after killing Kamsa in a war, and is extremely tired out and hungry. 3/n https://t.co/OBBzfmuQBC

Hence, Nivedyam is offered 5 times to satiate his hunger. Once, during a Grahanam, per usual norms the temple was closed. It is mentioned in 'Aithihya Mala' that Thekke Madom Swamiyar realised that as the offering was not made on time, the waist belt of the Lord had come down 4/n https://t.co/bV44HBCi3S

which would lead to calamities. Hence it was instructed that come what may, the shrine will be open during eclipses and puja and nivedyam offered. There is also a Grahana Puja held soon after the eclipse. There is an axe placed in the temple 5/n https://t.co/pziND9FQYV

which the Tantri is authorised to use to break open the temple door in case of any issues to keep up time. The pujas are also not done per regular clock timings but by checking the sun rays that fall on a Padmam engraving on the right side of the Garbha griha. Hare Krishna...

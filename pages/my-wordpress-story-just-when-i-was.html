My WordPress Story [A thread] Just when I was finishing college, a guy called @photomatt with a colleague, launched the very first version of @WordPress.

...then, the parents started requesting. Could you please teach my kid those computer skills? My very first student was Sulayiman Hussein. .....and I taught and taught like I had a stadium full of students. That is how @EzoneSchool was born!

After conquering the office products, the hunger for more knowledge got me playing around with the inside of the computers. With a lot of junk PCs to play with, I slowly turned into a computer repair geek.

A mix of expertise in Ms. Office and computer repairs made me a perfect fit for any school. ...and fortunately, in 2010, I was offered a job as a computer lab technician at St. Peter's SSS Nsambya. Dream accomplished? Maybe yes, may be not.

At the end of 2015, I said good bye to my dear job. I was genuinely confused. I didn't know whether it was the right time to quit, after building meaningful relationships with students and staff, I was genuinely confused.

2016, nothing! 2017, nothing! 2018, nothing! I had staff at my secretarial bureau and all I was doing was monitoring and chipping in here and there. But life was boring.

Then, as 2018 came to an end, I was invited by @mrogers4christ for #WordCampKla. That was around December. I attended, just to kill the boredom then I learnt how to install #WordPress.

In January 2019, I invited @mrogers4christ for a 1 day training. He took me through #WordPress. With no website to test my newly learnt skill, I built one for @EzoneSchool.

When a call for sponsors was made for @WordcampEntebbe in early 2019, I filled in the form and surprisingly, I was granted the opportunity - thank you @Braindventure . Thah is how I became. #WordCamp speaker.

At Entebbe, I was the last person to present. After connecting the laptop to the projector, the #WordPress dashboard switched to a view I don't remember. There were no words on the menus. Only icons. But thanks to @Hussn256, he saved me fast.

That year again, a call for speakers was made for #WordCampKla. Again, I was granted the opportunity - thanks to @FranklinNsubuga and @omukiguy.

In 2020, hell broke loose. I got closer to @mrogers4christ and it is during our deliberations that we thought of @WordPressJinja. We organized meetup after meetup and trainings after training. All this while, I used the idle hours to master my skills.

In 2022, when Covid was finally gone, together with @mrogers4christ and @AfricanTechVC we organized #WordCampJinja. That's how I became a #WordCamp co organiser.

...and in all this mix, I am glad that the things that I have always been passionate about have been brought to life through #WordPress an idea that was birthed by @photomatt 2 decades ago.

Because of #WordPress, my computer repair business has https://t.co/gjUuAU4kcF

Because of #WordPress, I managed to start a free online school for students in the new curriculum https://t.co/Rv8FAugcyP

Because of #WordPress, I can sell past papers online through https://t.co/9b0sGvm2PD

Because of #WordPress, my dream to mentor young kids and teenagers in the field of IT has been realised through https://t.co/wMoeY7CwGJ with the help of @myquesttoteach.

Because of #WordPress, I helped build the website of @ictteachersug where I serve as the National Coordinator.

Because of #WordPress, I will be at #WordCampEbb '23 in March in the company of @Braindventure, @CerinahTrillion, @davidofug, @GoTechUG, @fanny_codes, @imokolfaith etc.

Because of @WordPress, my life got meaning! Thank you @photomatt. "We never know how far our initiatives can impact the lives of those around us and those far away from us."

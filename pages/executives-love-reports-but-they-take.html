So maybe not grandma since she doesn't even know how to logon to her Windows XP desktop But this is pretty simple Here are the basic bones: 1) Aggregate data 2) Make report template in HTML 3) Send with Python Let's break it down:

1) Aggregate data A report is only as good as it's contents. Ask yourself these questions: - What are you referencing? - Do you want trends over time? - Or lists of successes/failures? - What do you want to stand out? Number crunching is easy after that.

2) Make report template in HTML Break out your bootstraps and templates folks. OR Make the report you like in gmail and then look at the HTML source code. And replace *actual* data in your template with <APPROPRIATE VAR NAME HERE>

Then you'll have a template with vars like <TOTAL ALERTS> <TOTAL LOW> <TOTAL HIGH> <PERCENT HIGH> etc etc etc Once you aggregate your data, find and and replace those vars with their real values and you're ready to ship it!

3) Send with Python Couldn't be easier. Just use smtplib Put your authentication credentials Attach the content of your report Send to client

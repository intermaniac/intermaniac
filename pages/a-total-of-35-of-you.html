1/ A total of 35 of you awesome folks signed the guestbook on the 486DX/50 web server. I extracted the data (via HTML) so I could make a thread giving a shout out to all those who signed it as a sign of my gratitude.

7/ Mats Engstrom (@matseng) hails from Stockholm, Sweden, and is into cool electronics shit, so chuck him a follow if you're into that kind of thing.

8/ Jimmy Olano, from Venezuela, seems to be a developer, and shared a link to their blog, here: https://t.co/PLthQoAI0o

9/ Seg Rulz (@SegHaxx) says that "all software is open source if you know assembler", and shared a link to their website with some nice copper bars and links to a modern-day BBS: https://t.co/KO1pxFksTE

10/ Ben S., from Washington State, is a retro-computing mutual, who tweet-blogs through classic PC and console games: @HunterZ0

11/ Some more people who dropped by but shared no further info: JoJo Reloaded from Spain, Arata Akatsuki ( who seems to have been at VCFMW 2022 when they visited), Erin, Lewis RazRetro, Solarstrike, Maldog from NY, and Connor Jackson. Thanks to all of you for visiting. ðŸ™‚

12/ Jonne Kokkonen (@laamaa) appears to be a Finnish musician and coder specialising in chiptunes. Check out their Bandcamp via their website: https://t.co/zdI6wsImLU

13/ AlanFleming was very enthusiastic about the 486 web server as that's where he started his career in computers. He is a software dev and radio geek: @_AlanFleming_

14/ Spladam loves the retro gaming: @SplAdamSage

15/ Ian Scott is the madman behind the PicoGUS so follow him immediately if you don't already: @ianpolpo

16/ Huxley Dunsany (@Huxley_D) is a tech and gaming enthusiast and founder of the Retro Roadshow, based in the San Francisco bay area: https://t.co/WWH2S15tUW

17/ Northern Lights Retrogaming (@NLightsRetro) is from northern Sweden and shared a link to their very interesting and informative blog: https://t.co/OF1cEnObg0

18/ Mike (@vswitchzero), from Canada, pursues extremely similar projects to myself and has produced some excellent YouTube videos on his projects so get over there immediately.

19/ Alberto Martinez Alonso (@black92rs74) is extremely enthusiastic about retro computing and his account very much has its finger on the pulse of the hobby generally. A nice person who brings people together.

20/ DJ Hoffman (@djh0ffman) is a DJ, coder, gamedev, demoscener and streamer who cannot be summed up in but one tweet. In particular he developed the PT1210 software for Amiga that turns a pair of them into turntables for tracker MODs. His website is here: https://t.co/3bSQ8G03hr

21/ Garth Beagle (@GarthBeagle) has a particular penchant for the repair and restoration of old Macintosh systems, but is a great person to follow if you're into computing and electronics generally.

22/ Sen (@sarahemm) is into retro (and modern) computing and electronics things so head over there.

23/ Darren H (@Vyothric) is an accomplished gamer and musician who seems to exist just to complete games on any platform and from any time period (preferably old). He shared a link to his very oldskool-inspired website, with lots of interesting content: https://t.co/BX5HiBmY5T

24/ Thomas Fuchs (@thomasfuchs) seemed to be the only person to fuck about with HTML in the comments and produced some flashing text, etc. He's produced some pretty stunning photography of space: https://t.co/R8BYKCnSR5

25/ TinTan (@REVOLTOSOTINTAN) visited from Mexico and seems to be into retro computing and politics in equal measure. And apparently a Twitter thread can't be longer than 25 tweets, so part 2 coming soon!

A simple moving average is an arithmetic mean of prices over a given period. It mainly eliminates random fluctuations in price and smoothens the chart. (2/18)

This is a very popular indicator and a commonly used strategy that applies moving averages is as follows: Long when a candle closes above the MA. Short when a candle closes below the MA (3/18)

Some questions: Which time interval chart would this best work on? What is the most appropriate moving average interval? Are there specific assets that this strategy works better on? Would it be better to only position long or short rather than trading both directions? (4/18)

Specify the range of the moving average intervals that need to be tested by changing the lower and upper limits. Lastly, test_type indicates where you want to only long (l), only short (s), or both long and short (b). (10/18)

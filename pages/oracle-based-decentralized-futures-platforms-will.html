First, why do perps exchanges need an oracle at all? Most decentralized trading platforms are NOT order-book based. Individuals are not taking isolated positions against one another (common on CEXs & in TradFi). ELI5: Price discovery doesn't happen on the platform.

Instead, users trade against a pool of liquidity provided by counterparties, who in turn receive fees from the platform. In our case, this liquidity is managed through the $ALP token. Anyway - if price discovery doesn't happen on the platform, how are trading prices determined?

Most decentralized leverage platforms (like Automatic) rely on asset prices from other markets. These are typically highly-liquid CEXs and DEXs with built-in price discovery. Oracles are the way these trading protocols obtain price feeds for their tradable assets. ‚öôÔ∏è

@Chainlink is by far the most popular decentralized oracle provider. This means they source data feeds (including prices) from a host of nodes around the world - essentially decentralizing data verification (and consensus) similar to L1/L2 blockchains.

This is a great start. It means on-chain protocols have access to price feeds that are far less susceptible to manipulation. But - individual price feeds aren't perfect, and we definitely can't rely on them for the extremely sensitive leverage trading strategies we enable.

For that reason and several others, we designed Automatic's In-Real-Time Hybrid Oracle Architecture from the ground up. ‚öôÔ∏è It's the first architecture to tackle every concern traders have when it comes to oracle-based trading platforms. Let's walk through each:

1. Price Inaccuracy I'm sure you've seen small price fluctuations when looking at a chart on CoinGecko vs Binance vs TradingView. This inconsistency is unacceptable for leverage traders. Our architecture aggregates price data from 12 sources and outputs the median. ‚úÖ

2. Price Manipulation Scamwicks (even in liquid markets) are far too common in crypto. Ever traded on a CEX? You've likely dealt w/ insane wicks at one time or another. There are two ways we tackle this: ‚úÖ All outlier price feeds are omitted ‚úÖ ROD (randomized order delays)

3. Price Reliability Trading platforms with too few price feeds are a MASSIVE risk to users. We saw this a few weeks back with Mycelium's $MLP pool losing 4-6% as a result of 1 of their 2 price feeds experiencing extreme volatility and being arbitraged.

We should never be relying on just 2 data feeds to be setting trading prices for a platform. We need a robust oracle network that traders can count on 100% of the time. Ensuring reliable prices was a big reason we chose to implement a network of 12 data feeds. ‚úÖ

Starting with Windows XP, I have reinstalled more than hundred systems constantly looking for the best software. Here is my list of 12+ free programs for your newly installed Windows (or Linux) that should be on each computer. Thread

1. So we're connected to the internet - first thing we do is change the browser. Go to Edge, change your privacy settings, set it to delete history after closing and go download Vivaldi - its possibilities are endless. I usually also install Brave as my second to-go browser.

1.1 @vivaldibrowser is the masterpiece in the world of browsers. It has everything, even things you haven't thought of yet. I've been using it for a few years now and to say I'm impressed is an understatement.

1.2 Looking for something bold and independent to surf the web? Try @brave It is powerful, modern and has a lot of unique functionality. From custom search, enhanced privacy, to web3... just try it yourself.

2. Next, we will install the archive handler and file manager @7zip. This is an old, but still relevant solution and a great alternative to Winzip.

3. Looking for a tool to open books that reads all possible formats? @STDUViewer is at your service Djvu, fb2, pdf, epub - it will take care of everything.

4. Instead of ms suite we will have @LibreOffice It is convenient, pleasant to use - has everything you need to work with documents, tables and presentations, and works with all popular formats. As an alternative, we also have an openoffice or Google Docs to choose from.

5. To open videos I always recommend K-Lite Codec Pack with Media Player Classic on board. The standard version is enough - it has subtitles and support for all possible video formats. VLC player is great as well.

6. I advise you to try free music streaming without ads with @nuclear_player. You can listen, download, make playlists... and all this without stealing your data.

6.1 You can listen to local music through @aimp_ru. This is the best player I've come across in years. Flexible, strong, best practices as always. The product has Russian developers, so you decide, because on their website I did not see any expression of their public position.

7. How about video services? The design not so perfect, but the functionality and capabilities of #Freetube are impressive. Watch videos from YouTube, no interruptions, no ads, with the ability to download, with your own playlists, local data and without any privacy issues.

8. If you need to edit images - I advise you to give @GIMP_Official a chance. Once you get used to it, once you understand all its advantages - it will become your faithful companion. That's what happened to me once.

8.1 For vector graphics there is @inkscape. There are also no restrictions, a lot of powerful tools and the ability to work with SVG onboard.

9. Going deeper into the video. For video recording and broadcasting/live straming, the @OBSProject studio is just irreplaceable. And for video editing, try to get to grips with the @openshot. It's award winning, you knew? Both solutions form an essential pair.

10. For noting and organizing thoughts, this is definitely @NotionHQ . You yourself build the system you want. Limitations are your imagination. Spend a few days to get acquainted and your world will never be the same again.

11. Looking for a separate email client? Try @mozthunderbird, they recently updated the design and it looks very attractive. It is lightning fast, detects mail settings automatically, easy to use, and with no limitations. What can I say, an incredible Mozilla team behind.

12. And finally, communication is always @telegram. Where else can you get free unlimited cloud storage with instant synchronization, unlimited chats, audio and video conferencing, auto-translation, stickers, no ads, no restrictions and no tracking?

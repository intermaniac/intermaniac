So, I'm a coder/engineer that loves large distributed systems. My passion for it got me in AWS, I worked on DynamoDB & was part of "building a greenfield" large system. Recently I moved over to "blockchain" ecosystem. if you're like me & want foundational knowledge.. ðŸ§µðŸ‘‡/1

First mandatory read is, do yourself a favor and read the Bitcoin paper. https://t.co/kV6iej5DiF .. and resist the urge to vomit with Proof-of-Work based consensus.. /2

Talking about PoW, for a distributed systems person that knows the mental model of Raft/Paxos by heart, PoW is hard to swallow and feels ridiculous even... /3

It took me months to understand one valuable property it has: - Very high tolerance (and preservation of safety and liveness) to large membership changes This means it *bends* the usual CAP theorem. ? can handle partition tolerance, maintaining availability and consistency ? /4

Blockhains, lets focus on #2 by popular adoption, #Ethereum. This is basically bitcoin, programmable. If you know the actor model, each wallet address is an actor that can recv messages, change state, & send msgs. You now have a Turing Machine ontop of a cryptographic ledger. /5

Mandatory read: https://t.co/JaFIoHgEB1 #Ethereum /6

Additionally, for me, it was instrumental to see a real application of the Ethereum computing model with an implementation of an "automated market maker" (AMM) in code. (Uniswap v1). What's a market maker ? /7

To trade liquid & fungible assets, by convention you need a middleman to hold "an order book" & gather "buy/sell" orders, discovering a price frontier. This is by design a centralised model that can then arbitrage (or sell access for arbitrage): the traditional finance market. /8

What if you could with pure code, create a Market without middleman? Without that asymmetry of power to arbitrage/profiteer? You can. Read the uniswap v1 whitepaper https://t.co/Q3W11A3Zqi #Uniswap #DeFi /9

That is it. 3 papers, don't skim them. *really* read them. I hope these reads help you like they helped me. There's more after this, other systems/protocols/specs like Cosmos, Tendermint, Curve' stableswap, Ethereum PoS, L2s, ZKSnarks, But this is an approachable start. 10/10

Our first module considers four core aspects of that framework: beliefs, environment, relationships and behaviours. This thread provides signposting to useful videos to learn more about each aspect of the framework, and how exploring them can enhance your coaching.

What do we mean by values, beliefs, and a coaching philosophy? Although a common phrase now on coaching courses, as @WadeWgilbert illustrates here, we need to make sure we are using this tool most effectively: https://t.co/cbxRNqs10z

So much coaching is focused on what the coach does. @KristofferHen's work is so important to take a step back and consider the environment in which a coach is working, and how shaping the environment can make the rest of coaching so much easier: https://t.co/Lq36BMEJIR

At the heart of sports coaching is relationships. @JowettSophia has produced a range of research guiding coaches in the evaluation and development of relationships. You can find an introduction to that research here: https://t.co/HVGGGKfcQs.

Quality coaching is ultimately seen in the behaviours that a coach exhibits in training and in competition. Those behaviours, and their alignment with core beliefs and values in serial winning coaches are explored by @DrSergioLara_EU and colleagues: https://t.co/KUKabcueNZ

All of these resources are feely available for any coach to engage with. So what does a coaching MSc programme add? It's the structure to make the most out of engaging with your peers and experimenting with your practice. Just ask the students & graduates https://t.co/Pz2NOpatj5

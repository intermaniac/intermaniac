Underrated Non-Python Python Projects - A thread I’m in the middle of a spectacular anxiety attack so made this to get my mind off it ✌️ These are by no means the only ones but definitely ones I never hear people talk about often (if at all)

MICHAEL PALIN - THE MISSIONARY Great film that I have never seen or heard of outside of my Python deep dives. Written by Palin, and starring him and Maggie Smith. Palin’s a priest who is sent on a mission to help prostitutes. And boy does he help them. https://t.co/abH0NWqwPO

TERRY JONES - THE WIND IN THE WILLOWS TJs strength was directing, hands down. You just need to watch the Python films for that. But he was an excellent actor especially with facial expression WITW is one of my favourite books and this is a great film adaptation with a greatcast https://t.co/gRdqlvTBxn

ERIC IDLE - RUTLAND WEEKEND TELEVISION There’s not a huge amount of footage of this show kicking about, but what can be found is gold. The most iconic being George Harrison’s guest appearance singing The Pirate Song. Plus the always great Neil Innes https://t.co/l5w0EhsIAW

GRAHAM CHAPMAN - CALCIUM MADE INTERESTING CMI is a book of Gra’s sketches, letters, and odd bits of writing. Absolutely hilarious and well worth a read. https://t.co/HWB05YkUuk

JOHN CLEESE - CLOCKWISE John’s was the hardest to think of as he’s probably the Python with the most well known projects next to Palin and Gilliam. Clockwise plays heavily to Johns strength - uptight arsehole-adjacent characters. https://t.co/R3H0VPeKQQ

TERRY GILLIAM - THE FISHER KING Another hard one as most of Gilliam’s projects are pretty well known but I think this is just slightly less so than Baron M or Brazil. Genuinely heartbreaking beautiful film starring Robin Williams in a rare drama role https://t.co/iWoQbZO0oQ

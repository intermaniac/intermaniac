How to shoot an Eclipse from any device ( a thread): There are 2 parts one during the noon hours and second one during evenings post 3 PM. These are my techiques which I have experimented with over the years. Hope these help you capture some amazing shots. 1/n https://t.co/qLRm8pRpuK

From Camera: Required: Zoom lens 200mm minimum , higher the better. ND filter 10 stop is minimum , 20 stops if it is noon. Better quality filter give better results in color and sharpness. Tripod if shooting a timelapse, ND is must else it might damage the sensor.

3/n One is a 10 stop fixed and another is a 10 stop variable ND filter from @hoyafilterusa and @NiSiGlobal When we are shooting an eclipse aiming for the sun, the lens concentrates the light on the sensor. During noon this high beam of light over long periods can damage it. https://t.co/kCnoSMaRH5

4/n These ND filters help to expose the sun perfectly and get its warm radiant glow without overexposing it. Overexposed sun will be a sphere of white with no details. On the other hand perfectly exposed sun looks like this. https://t.co/R8lZqtknmR

5/n DIY is using Xray sheets instead of ND filters, you will get the results but not of the highest quality since air cavity between the lens and the sheet causes less details to be captured. Pro trip: wipe your Filters to avoid smudging and light bleeding from the sun.

6/n Mobile: choose your tele camera and you can add ND filters for mobiles as well. @moment makes some amazing lenses for mobiles. If not get a couple of sun glasses or X ray sheet. For better results: use mobile tripod as tele lenses are not that great yet.

7/n How to capture on camera: Imp: No sensor spot should be there as we will be shooting in high aperture these things just create hell and might ruin the shot. Without ND ( noon) Max shutter 1/8000 or more Max aperture f 30 and above Iso: lowest 100 or 50

8/n The sun will be still over exposed with maxed out settings without ND/xrays. You will get a sphere of white Take caution while shooting timelapse in such cases as I mentioned earlier , it might damage the sensor.

9/n With ND filter at Noon 1/8000 f 32 iso 100 ND 10 might still give a blown out sun. ND 20 will give you the Colors and show the sun spots. The color of the sun changes over the hours. It might vary from yellow to orange to deep red,depending on the time of day.

10/n With ND ( evening) These are the setting to start with. 3-4 (10 stop) 1/4000 f22 iso 100 4-5 pm ( 10 stop ND) 1/1600 f13 iso 100 Shutter speed should always be more than you focal length for best results ( double if not on tripod)

11/n If you are using a tele convertor 2x or 1.5x be careful of the shake because at higher focal lengths (FL) every small movement can cause a huge shake. To counter it use higher shutter speed and if you are on tripod use 2-10 seconds timer to avoid shake ( shooting below FL)

12/n Post 5 PM it all depends on the Available light. I would be using the the histogram to keep the stutter speed right where I could bring back the details from the highlights. At this stage balancing the frame becomes the most important if you have a subject. https://t.co/t9TWS6h9TX

13/n I would start with stutter speed that's more than the FL again. F8 Iso 400 For Mobiles: Add a x-ray or a sunglass if you don't have a dedicated ND for mobile and pull the exposure as low as you can.

14/n Pro tip for mobile users: @Lightroom app on phone provides a lot of customisation for taking pictures which some phones without pro mode can make use of, Especially iPhone users. This is how the pro mode looks like. https://t.co/9Z6fR13LwA

15/n One thing to keep in mind is that when we are shooting an Eclipse over a long period time , we tend to look directly into the sun or through the lens. So after the shoot our eyes dry out a lot, personally experienced it , so take precautions and protect your eyes.

16/n Wear UV protection , wash your eyes with cold water and keep yourself hydrated. These are some tips I wanted to share with all you folks hoping that it would make someone pick up their camera and experience this beautifully natural phenomenon.

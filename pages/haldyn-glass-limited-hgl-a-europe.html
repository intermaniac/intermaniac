A Thread on #Haldyn Glass Limited (HGL): A Europe +1 Theme Play Sector: Glass & Glass Products Industry: Packaging Market Cap: Microcap Scrip Name: #HaldynGlass @Abhi4Research @darshitpatel84 @murugavell @koolvicky @Oracle_Trade @cakunalshah1983

#HaldynGlass is engaged in the business of manufacturing exclusive glass containers and bottles for the Food, Beverages, Cosmetic and Liquor Industries Technical Outlook: #Haldyn has given a follow through base breakout on a weekly timeframe supported by Strong Quarterly Results https://t.co/579hLEA5uV

Client Base: Few of the renowned names the company caters to among many others include United Spirits (UB), Pernod Ricard, Amul, ADF Foods, Parle Agro, Vadilal, Dabur and WeikField. The Company also caters to Export Markets such as Africa, Nepal & Sri Lanka.

Revenue Mix: Currently the Liquor Sector accounts for 70% of the overall revenue whereas FMCG, F&B and Cosmetics sector account for the remaining 30%. However the company has been actively looking to increase its presence in the FMCG & Perfume Segment where margins are higher.

Joint Venture: Haldyn entered into a 50:50 JV with 'Heinz Glass' based in Germany named 'Haldyn Heinz Fine Glass Private Limited' (HHFPL). HHFPL is engaged in manufacturing of glass bottles used for perfumes and cosmetics. The company caters to clients based in Europe and USA.

With Russia restricting the gas supply through Nord Stream 1 to the EU, German manufacturing companies which rely heavily on Gas as a source of energy for production have taken a huge hit and have resorted to measures like partial shutdown and reduction in production levels.

Heinz Glass the largest Glass Manufacturer in Europe said it will be forced to reduce local production and move it overseas due to shortage of gas. Haldyn Glass could be a direct beneficiary because of the JV with Heinz Glass and can potentially convert this to additional export.

Capex: The Board has approved a Capex Plan of Rs. 150 crores which is to be funded by a mix of term loan of Rs. 90 crores and the remaining from Internal Accruals. The Project is expected to be completed by September 2023 and increase the existing capacity by 95 tonnes per day.

Financial Performance: (As on 30.09.2022) Net Sales: 80.75 Cr Sept 2022 vs 53.42 Cr Sept 2021 (Up 51.14%) Net Profit: Rs. 5.50 Cr Sept 2022 vs 2.77 Cr Sept 2021 (Up 98.56%) Operating Profit: Rs. 7.86 Cr Sept 2022 vs 5.04 Cr Sept 2021 (Up 55.95%) EPS: 1.02 Sept 22 vs 0.52 Sept 21

Recent Developments: -The Board at its meeting held on October 4, 2022 has approved the incorporation of wholly owned subsidiary in USA named Haldyn Glass USA Inc. - The Company has Appointed Mr. Niraj Tipre who was previously the CEO of Piramal Glass as its CEO in the F.Y 21-22

Risks: - Although the capex plan is expected to increase capacity by 95 TPD, it is significantly funded by external borrowings of Rs. 90 crores which represents 54% of the net-worth of the company while the total project represents 85% of the net-worth of the company. (Continued)

On Account of the above, proper and timely project execution will be Key. - The company was also involved in a case of Financial Mischief in 2012. The services of the executives involved were however terminated with immediate effect in 2012. https://t.co/ZHAUU7jvcK

Future Outlook: Considering the additional demand which could arise on account of Production Cuts in Europe, Profit Contribution from JV and Capacity Expansion coming into play in FY23, #Haldyn Glass could trade at an Annualized Forward PE of around 15 assuming a 7-8% QoQ growth.

The Average Industry PE stands at 36. Hence overall #HaldynGlass seems like a good investment prospect from a long term perspective and can possibly outperform the broader market.

I'm starting a new public project on GitHub. I'm hoping it gets some love. It's a full-life activity planner. Projects. Task lists. Routines. Goals. BUT -- not all by itself. A key feature will be the integration to many other first-class planners. 1/11 (thread)

In other words, rather than trying to become the only planning system in your life, this system will try to integrate the other activity systems you already have and let them do what they do best. This is my first real attempt to create a public open-source project. 2/11

I expect to make mistakes. Hopefully nothing too major. I'm also going to document the process as much as I can and show how I think. I'm building this because I've tried other systems and they have all suffered from the same weakness. 3/11

Every other system has "integration" to other systems, or can import / export to other systems. But all of them try to "own" the master list of things to do. That's a problem. I am usually working in another tool or application than my "master planner system." 4/11

So I will identify things I need to follow up, or I will have completed something I needed to do, but I won't be doing that while I have my "master planner system" open, so that means I have another thing to do than just the thing I needed to do. That sucks. 5/11

I'm building the system that should make this better. It should work at work, and at home, or at school, or wherever else I need to think about what I should do next or let someone else know that I've done stuff. I'll use the tag #XFX for updates here and elsewhere. 6/11

XFX is the abbreviation of Executive Function Executor. That's the working title of the project. Executive Functions are the features of your brain that decide what to do and when to do it. See https://t.co/YVNbyxYi0p. 7/11

Most people (meaning neurotypical people) can track and appropriately act on things they want or need to do, with only a little assistance from external sources (to-do lists, project plans, etc.). 8/11

Some people, like those of us with ADHD, have a diminished capacity of executive functions. I NEED lists of things to do. I need those lists to be simple in order to prioritize well. But my mind tracks EVERYTHING. 9/11

Every other system I have tried gets bogged down because I put everything into it. It makes it hard to keep it current, or to filter out things I don't need to think about. If I don't have a system that I trust to track everything, I'll struggle with tracking anything. 10/11

And since no system I have tried will track everything (especially across work and personal realms), I will build one. This will take a while, but maybe if I get help it will be the best activity planning system. end/11

Welcome to today's #dnd/#Pathfinder2e thread ðŸ§µ: "Class AND Background" Today at my new job, one of my favorite new co-workers, this wonderfully sweet woman in her late 40's early 50's says, "Azure, sweetie, why this job?"

She elaborated: "In one week, you've demonstrated a calvocade of talents, but why this?" I mustered the confident smirk only boi Azure can muster, "Because, Jeanie, my other talents left to themselves make me a monster. Success and recognition rule me when left to themselves."

"Here, I can put them to use for our clients and improve their lives, inspire them, give them hope, hell, even make their day better, and by the time they don't need our help, I get something much longer lasting than recognition: gratitude."

Jeanie was amazed, in her mind I was playing the wrong class, but my background supports how I play my class now. Azuriel, my investigator with the multiclass dedication into swashbuckler has the student of magic background but casts no spells. Their father was a fencer, mother

a psychic. So they learned to read even the subtle cues sans psychic powers. While Azure has otherworldly magic to give them some limited spellcasting, Azuriel's personality makes them appear swashbuckler-esque. They're even a fairly gifted fencer themselves, but have a foil:

They don't want to be either of their parents. They inherited dad's skill with a blade and charm, and mom's natural intuition and knack for study but is the sum of their parts and their own worldview thus Investigator.

I often wonder how much my background in characters was ancillary. Zel, Neurellion, and the others have pretty... class fitting backgrounds, but Azuriel I think I made interesting. A long time ago, my best friend said, "nobody calls a rogue a rogue in the fiction."

That bothered me because surely he had to be wrong, but just as Jeanie sees my background and the superficial clash with my class, characters should be... obvious right? I disagree. As you make your next characters, I hope you make weird combinations. Gladiators who became bards

telling tales of fallen friends, students of magic/scholars who became barbarians to learn to defeat a particular enemy, or a warrior/martial disciple who became a druid to attone for the lands their companies ravished.

Before optimizers jump in and say, "I prefer to min-max", the game I prefer to play doesn't punish you for the interesting choice. Sorry yours does. I'm sure your life has its intricacies. Try playing with some fantastic ones. Thanks for reading https://t.co/9VHbTOWp0h

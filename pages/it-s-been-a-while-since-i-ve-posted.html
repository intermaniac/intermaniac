It's been a while since i've posted anything significant, so here's a thread on what i call my 'distraction-habit framework.' ðŸ§µðŸ‘‡ 1/?

i'm very distractible. always have been. partly it's a function of (what i think is) an above-average iq combined with the constant availability of new information through modern media. 2/?

as such, i find it hard to focus on tasks for a long period of time. in high school, this was terrible for me. any assignments requiring deep work were turned in late, if i completed them at all. however, i still scored a 28 on my act, including a 30 in english. 3/?

as an adult, these tendencies haven't disappeared. i still do not find myself capable of sitting still for too long to keep at a single solitary task. if something deserves 3hrs+ of work to complete, i will procrastinate until i finally give it maybe 1hr. 4/?

as you can imagine, this has resulted in some shoddy work on my part. sometimes i get lucky, and the quality is good enough. for my repeating long-winded tasks, i find ways to optimize the workflow until we get down to the ~1hr timeframe. 5/?

more often than not, though, i suffer the consequences. work isn't done on time. it's late. i've been chewed out more than a few times in my career for the resulting mistakes/expenses. 6/?

then i have another problem: i'm interested in too many different things. i want to read a book, re-learn greek, learn spanish, launch an online community, keep up on social media, build a unique communication platform, create my own social-media posting engine, ... 7/?

... the list goes on and on. it's painfully obvious to me that i simply do not have time to do all of the tasks i *must* do alongside all the tasks i *want* to do. or can i? 8/?

inspired by @douglaswils's "ploductivity," i realized that many of my *want-to-do* tasks can be completed with some basic time-investments per-day. for example, i would like to read the whole bible cover-to-cover. i read at approx. 350 wpm. the bible ... 9/?

has approx. 788k words. i could read the whole thing in approx. ~40hrs of reading time. if i read for 1hr per day, i could read the whole bible in 40 days, but that's not going to happen. i have other responsibilities in life to attend to. 10/?

however, i could read for 15 minutes per day, and expect to read the whole thing in 160 days. that's not quite six months. take a look at a bible. pretty big book, right? wouldn't it be cool to read the whole thing? worth it if it took six months to achieve that goal? 11/?

so, 15 minutes per day. boom. i've taken a big *want* and broken it down into a habit which can be inserted into my day. i do the same calculation with reading books in general. at my reading speed, how many books per year ... 12/?

... could i finish if i read for 30min/day? i don't remember the number, but it's a lot. but i don't have the patience to read for 30min in one sitting! so, i've chosen to break it up into 3x 10min sittings. and i'll often insert those 10min blocks into my day ... 13/?

... when i feel like i need a break from my long-winded tasks that i have trouble focusing on anyway. big presentation to prepare for? looking at three hours of focus time? work for 30 minutes, 10 minute habit-break, work for 1 hour, 10 minute habit-break, ... 14/?

... work for another 45 minutes, 10 minute habit-break. check it out! i've made *a ton* of progress on that larger task, my distractible brain was kept occupied by switching gears into my book, and i've kept pace in my reading-goal for the year. 15/?

so, in a world where it seems like everyone has adhd, i hope this model might be helpful for others. take a big goal you'd like to accomplish, break it down into a 5-, 10-, 20-, or 30-minute commitment. insert those 'habits' as your breaks throughout the day. 16/?

p.s. be mindful not to *only* do your habits to the exclusion of your real work. i remind myself that after a "habit break" it's time to get back to work (when i'll often set a timer on my phone to help me stick to it instead of taking my next break too soon). peace! 17/end

⁦@marcoarment⁩ lots of FUD here re: SQLite and MongoDB from ⁦@siracusa⁩ but also some good points re: going NoSQL. Going to try and write up counterpoints here. A thread. https://t.co/2rZI8jD8Ry

Re: MongoDB. @houlihan_rick would not have left DynamoDB for MongoDB if it were not a robust and usable DB. He's no sell out. It's also come a long way from where it was before when it gained that "planet scale" meme and a reputation for losing data. I trust it. Re: SQLite. 1/5

SQLite can be used for more than a million records. Throw it into WAL mode and see the diff. Modern SQLite is fast. Maybe the version shipping with MacOS is old and slow but... 2/5

One thing you could do is consider a sharded per appleid or per user model with the sqlite files stored on some blob storage -- a sort of poor man's sharding. Anyone who wanted their data deleted you'd just delete their sqlite file. Boom done. 3/5

We did this to great success at a fintech I worked at here in Germany. Another thing to consider is @planetscaledata who are doing MySQL and are the same folks that ran the MySQL backend for YouTube. Works for them should work for you ;-) 4/5

I do really think if you already know your querying usecase then a nosql db like @fauna or @MongoDB or open source ones like @ScyllaDB or even @dynamodb would be awesome and using the single table design would simplify things a ton. 5/5

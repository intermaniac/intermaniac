Today I want to tell you about an aha moment I had about server-side #AWS S3 encryption. Have you ever thought about the difference between SSE-S3 and SSE-KMS encryption methods? At first glance, they do the same thing, but the devil is in the details. - Thread -

- Price - There are no additional fees for using server-side encryption with Amazon S3-managed keys (SSE-S3). KMS (SSE-KMS) will add some $ to your bill. You must pay for a key and the number of cryptographic operations. The price depends on the AWS Region.

- Quotas - No limits for Amazon S3-managed keys. There is a limit on the number of requests to AWS KMS in all regions. The default value can be 5 500, 10 000, or 50 000 requests per second (for symmetric keys). And again, the number depends on the AWS Region.

You may wonder why anyone would use KMS. You have limits, and you have to pay some dollars. So what might force you to use KMS? The reason is simple: sometimes, you must meet security and compliance requirements. Let's move on to the next tweet.

- Management - Under this topic, KMS won't give a chance to keys managed by S3. You control the KMS keys, so you own the data. You can restrict access to sensitive data to a limited number of principals.

But be careful. If you don't lock down your permissions properly, you will have all benefits of S3-managed keys for the price of KMS. It sounds like an incredibly hurtful deal.

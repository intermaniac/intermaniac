Thread: Some personal news. We have just left Mexico after 22 wonderful years there with The Wall Street Journal. I am now in London with Helena and the kids and will be UK bureau chief at the WSJ. As Monty Python says, “And now for something completely different.”

I will continue to watch Latin America and Mexico in particular with keen interest, and still post stuff about the region, too, if that’s OK. But my feed will also have a healthy dose of UK and Europe, too.

I am deeply grateful to the WSJ for letting me stay so long in Mexico, land of my birth. I arrived a few days before Vicente Fox became the country’s first democratically elected leader. It would be a full year before I got a blackberry. Twitter was still a few years away.

During my time there, I fell in love, had kids, and realized that being a dad is life’s greatest blessing. I watched 9/11, went to Iraq for the war, then Egypt during the Arab Spring. I lived through Bush, Obama, and Trump.

I witnessed the reign of Hugo Chavez and his destruction of Venezuela. I saw Brazil rise and fall, and Haiti just fall. Cuba, as always, breaks all our hearts.

And, sadly, I lived through the Mexican drug wars, which gave me material for some of my best stories but also material that comes to me unbidden at night. I have to confess I am a bit of a sadder person as a result. Americans bear part of the blame.

I have also watched as Mexico's government, once a champion of free trade, turns inward again. The NAFTA Mexico had problems, but it was slowly improving. It’s hard to watch things slide back.

I hope Mexico can tackle inequality, corruption and lawlessness sooner rather than later. But to do that, you need strong institutions, not a strongman.

I have been blessed to work with gifted colleagues from whom I learned as much about life as journalism. @jose_decordoba has rightly been described as a modern-day Graham Greene. And for 22 years, I got to know him as a true gentleman, with a warm heart and sparkling wit.

@ForeroWSJ and @PerezEnMexico, thanks for being partners in crime. I admire you both tremendously. Ditto the rest of my talented colleagues.

We will continue to have a home in Mexico in Tepoztlan, and plan on coming back regularly, so this is an “hasta luego” instead of an adios. I am really grateful to the people who follow me and have made Twitter a space for reflection and laughs. It’s not all bad on here.

Lastly, we shed more than a few tears leaving Mexico. Yes, it has many problems, but it's such a magical place, too. Everyone who leaves recognizes this. Incredible landscapes, food, and most importantly, people. Muchas, muchas gracias. Los voy a extrañar mucho!

Social engineering refers to a form of malicious activity that relies on human vulnerabilities. Hackers/attackers use this to manipulate victims & gain access to sensitive data, accounts, or crypto wallets.

Read a breakdown on the #Uber hack here https://t.co/Qpu4PFG2h0 https://t.co/nTAS1Iiaf0

Social Engineering can be of these forms: - Phishing: This form of attack tries to exploit human weakness for fear, urgency & curiosity. i.e. $8m uniswap exploit few months ago. https://t.co/jj5dGiN6QG

Another typical phishing attack vector occurs in cloned emails that warn a user of an urgent need to update their password or personal information as their access has been breached by a third party & in that way luring the user to reveal sensitive information.

- Baiting: This form of attack occurs out of curiosity or greed and the attacker uses bait to lure the victims. i.e Honeypots An attacker might lure the victims to a website that offers a free service E.g to download files, you'll have to create an account to have access.

When a user does so, they reveal their information indirectly or also download a file with infected malware which will give the attacker access to their sensitive data. I.e $ANUBIS hack which resulted in losses of $60m https://t.co/hufTyKNcaH

- Scareware: As the name implies, the attacker uses this method to instill fear in the victims. It involves the creation of a false alarm that attempts to trick the victim into installing malicious software that looks legit.

Victims of the Ledger Data breach have reported cases similar to this https://t.co/q1cdkXxLEK

As a user, you can protect yourself from social engineering attacks in the following ways:

- As it relies on human vulnerabilities, be alert & sensitive when it involves sharing your personal information.

- Don't open suspicious emails, attachments, or websites.

- Use of updated anti-malware software

- Use multi-factor authentication for your apps.

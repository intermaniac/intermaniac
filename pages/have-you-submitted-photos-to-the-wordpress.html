Have you submitted photos to the #WordPress photo directory yet? You should! Here's a short list of do's and don't's for when you do. (A ðŸ§µ) https://t.co/N1MqcA0X3j https://t.co/bgGHt4KA9j

Do make sure your photos are sufficiently sized. At least 2000 x 2000 px. Anything smaller is automatically rejected, which is frustrating after you've uploaded your photo, checked all the boxes, and created a description.

Do edit your photos to make them more likely to be used by others: correct (level) your horizons, brighten photos if necessary, crop appropriately, and make sure they would be something you, yourself, would use on a website.

Do add a good description. This helps the photo moderators with tags for your photo. For the photo below, I used the following: Bald Eagle, Montezuma Wildlife Refuge, Seneca Falls, New York, USA https://t.co/JFnrePDcyS

Don't submit blurry or low quality photos. Make sure your photo quality is something you and others would proudly display on a website.

Don't submit photos with peoples' faces. They cannot be approved for the directory.

Don't submit photos with any personally identifiable information: license plates and addresses, for example, will not be approved. (You may edit out that info and then submit that car photo, though!)

Don't submit photos with watermarks. They will not be approved.

Don't submit photos that are not yours to submit. Photos are run through TinEye and other photo checkers. If you try to pass others' work as your own, it will not be approved, and you may be banned from submitting any photos and have any other photos removed from the directory.

A complete list for what can and cannot be approved is found within the submission form. We love seeing your photography and love adding your work to the directory - just make sure it meets all the qualifications and is something you're proud of! /ðŸ§µ https://t.co/oSgEt8tKlm

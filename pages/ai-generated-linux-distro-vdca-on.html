VDCA | ON Focused on simplicity and sleekness, secretive enough to blend some of it into its branding but takes open source seriously Primary users: a small but dedicated community of designers #aiart #aiartcommunity #dalle2 #stablediffusion https://t.co/TsjtwewBPw

Brat Known for its balance between ease of use and its optimization for data science and other big projects that need lots of resources Designed by and used primarily by academics and research students https://t.co/Brsq1BSpoe

Arrca Linux Has always been very strange. Uses its own package manager that is actually quite elegant with new features others soon adopted Developed with grants from the EU and European Commission by scientists who originally meant it to be internal but it gained a following https://t.co/cVchv6rJ2w

AM Linux Was originally a web-based OS, but devs quickly decided to do a minimalist Linux distro instead. Hardened, and thoroughly, at that. So secure it’s very unpopular to use for anything but diagnostics, data transfers, gov comms, etc. Refuses any funding above $20M/mo. https://t.co/zOMxkfvoqK

INNIE Originally made as a joke by a bored teacher who made an “OS for education” with a ridiculously nice and highly accessible GUI that wound up being used by a ton of school systems internationally because kids actually like it #aiart #dalle2 #stablediffusion https://t.co/qkxkTvvxvi

BOM Has been used for ages and ages as the “secret favorite” among developers and hobbyists alike, because of its reputation as silly, despite its origin as an OS meant for large scale, multi device/portable use, and has a ton of money behind it (Chinese university project) https://t.co/XkiF9HPeqI

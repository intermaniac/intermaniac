However, the religious lobby which had remained almost side-lined during the freedom struggle became very active to get political space & used this very neutral term Pakistan Ideology for this purpose by equating it with Two-nations Theory, and its exclusive reliance on religion

Sources of Pakistan Ideology Every ideology is deeply embedded in the history of its country and springs from its dominant religious beliefs, cultural aspirations, and societal values. Accordingly, we can identify the following four mainsprings of Pakistan’s Ideology.

A. Islamic Moorings Islam, which was used as a battle cry for the creation of Pakistan, is the bedrock of Pakistan’s Ideology. As Islam prescribes a complete code of life for Muslims Pakistan’s ideology draws heavily from this source, provides them worldview & norms of behaviour

B. Our Indus Valley Heritage Pakistan and all of its constituent units are the proud inheritors of thousands of years old Indus Valley civilisation which still lives among us in terms of our beliefs, customs, and practices. Consequently, our Ideology reflects this proud heritage

C. Founding Fathers’ Vision The vision given by founding fathers during freedom struggle and after its successful completion is the third source of Pakistan’s Ideology. They certainly wanted the country to be guided by Islamic principles but never wanted it to be a theocracy

D. . Constitutional Obligations Constitution of Pakistan is the fourth source of Pakistan’s Ideology. Its Principles of Policy such as Islamic way of life, democratic form of government, full participation of women, peaceful coexistence etc are basic pillars of Pakistan Ideology

E. Global Commitments The fifth source of Pakistan’s Ideology stems from its being a responsible member of the international community. It is a signatory to a large number of global and regional conventions and treaties that have been ratified by the Parliament

Features of Pakistan Ideology Based on the above sources we can discern the following prominent features of the Pakistan Ideology

1. Islamic Identity Pakistan is a Muslim state on account of her overwhelmingly Muslim population; as such Islam will remain the fundamental source of her statecraft, social relations, business dealings, and foreign affairs.

Islam will occupy the central place like Christianity is the bedrock of European countries despite all their pretensions to be secular states. Good governance is enshrined in Islam as the guiding principle of the state but in no way the state will be a theocracy

2. Own our roots. We are proud of our Islamic identity but we are also proud of our historical roots. Besides being a Muslim majority state with multi-ethnic composition, Pakistan is the inheritor of thousands of years old Mehargarh, Mohenjo-Daro, and our other civilizations

3. Unity in Diversity One of the most prominent features of Pakistan’s Ideology is its firm belief in unity in diversity. A modern multi-ethnic, multi-religious entity, Pakistan is home to over 15 major ethnic groups that differ in physical features, customs, dress, food, music

It is a melting pot of South Asian, Central Asian, Western Asian, Middle Eastern, and European influences brought by foreign invasions and trade relations. Consequently, everyone in Pakistan irrespective of his or her ethnic roots is a Pakistani by choice.

4. Respect for Fundamental Human Rights Being a signatory to the UN Universal Declaration of Human Rights, the state respects the dignity of the common man by empowering him and providing him social justice. Principles of Policy of Pakistan’s Constitution incorporate all these

5. Quality of Life Pakistan tries to ensure constant improvement in the quality of life of its citizens through sustainable and inclusive growth whose gains are distributed equitably among all the regions of the country and all the sections of the society

6. National Cohesion Pakistan Ideology gives a vision of a just and prosperous Pakistan to all the various nationalities, communities, sections, and regions to create stakes for everyone to feel satisfied in a united, federal Pakistan. https://t.co/od2n589vmU

7. Peaceful Coexistence Peaceful coexistence is the cornerstone of Pakistan’s Ideology as far as the conduct of its foreign policy is concerned. Pakistan is a peace-loving nation-state, acting responsibly in global and regional affairs.

8. Democratic Governance Pakistan owes its creation to a democratic struggle and as such liberal democracy as envisioned by its founding fathers is one of the most important pillars of Pakistan’s Ideology. It strengthens democratic dividends by promoting true political culture

Conclusion It has become fashionable among a section of our intellectual elite to make fun of Pakistan’s Ideology without realising that every country has an ideology that defines the parameters for its statecraft.

There is no need to be apologetic in believing in it which provides a set of world view for citizens of Pakistan-Muslims and non-Muslims alike in the light of its special historical context, geographical location, and contemporary socio-political compulsions, internal & external.

Coding courses can teach you a lot of things. There are courses on everything from CSS to DevOps But here are some things I learned on the job working as a software engineer that these courses never taught me ðŸ§µ

1) Product market fit Sometimes, you don't even know if what you're building is actually useful So you start talking to potential customers to figure out if you can really solve the problems that they have This is hard to get right the first time and you'll probably mess it up

2) Supporting customers I remember when we launched the MVP of a product, we got our first customer support issue and it taught me something valuable To us, it was just a line of code to fix But to the customer, it was the difference between being able to serve their customers

3) Selling your product is just as hard as building it Every time I spoke to someone in sales, I felt like I knew nothing about our business These people were in the field daily, demoing this product and knew everything about the customer that needed it They were crucial

4) The operations side Behind every great product, there's also a great Operations team It's the manual work done to support what the product can't do yet They're everything from spreadsheet wizards to the ones that know the minute details of your product ecosystem

5) Finances Do you owe money or does someone owe you? Are you making money? Do you have only 3 vs 6 months of money left A competent finance team is crucial to every business, especially ones that accept any type of payments You can't afford any mistakes here, literally

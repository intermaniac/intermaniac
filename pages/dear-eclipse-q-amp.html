1. I first heard about The Eclipse in a Reddit thread about upcoming BLs. I was interested then, but it was only after I watched Not Me and fell head over heels for First that it became my most anticipated BL of 2022.

2. Episode 10 is my favorite, and I think my favorite scene is the one in which Akk agrees to become Ayan's boyfriend.

3. "You're allowed to be weak, at least with me." I suspect it's everyone's favorite. "I'm a boy and I'm going to sleep" is a close second though. ðŸ˜†

4. I would be Dika. We are similar in temperament and values, we have been similarly unlucky in love, and unfortunately his battle with mental illness feels very familiar to me.

5. They were both clearly attracted to each other on sight. Ayan then grew fond of Akk because he liked his reactions to being messed with, and Akk slowly grew to appreciate Ayan's audacity and integrity. But love came once they learned to be vulnerable with each other. +

For Ayan, loving Akk was a deliberate choice as soon as he recognized that Akk needed compassion and support. Akk's love, meanwhile, first manifested as a subtle protectiveness toward Ayan in his weakest moments. Covering each other's vulnerabilities is their love language.

6. I think once he grew confident that Thua liked him and he knew he had the support of at least one friend (Ayan), the rewards outweighed the risks for him.

7. It seems likely, and it looks like Namo is determined to sneak in there too. It will probably look like their group interactions during their trip to Akk's home, but with significantly less tension.

8. I pegged Namo for the cursebringer originally, but then we got the reveal about Akk's curse activities. Once the curse came back, Namo felt too much like a red herring, and I began to suspect Thua instead.

9. I suppose so, but to enact meaningful reforms, I think the school's leadership and most of its teaching staff need to go. They've been complicit in the school's abuses for too long and that behavior is ingrained now.

10. If I'm being completely honest, I wouldn't have lasted long enough at Suppalo to have addressed its injustices, especially at that age. Some of us weren't made to stand on the front lines. But I would have supported the cause from the sidelines.

11. I want the plot hole about Ayan's notebook cleared up (why hide the notebook from Chadok if he knew Thua planted it, and what was that panic attack about?), and I wish the teen characters had been given time to talk out their differences. The sudden reconciliation was weird.

12. I have no idea. I hope it will be able to fill in some of episode 11's gaps, but I doubt it will. So I guess I just hope that it will feel like a meaningful conclusion to Akk's and Ayan's character arcs.

13. AkkAyan have a surprisingly mature relationship for their age, so I think they'll continue to deepen their understanding of each other and stay together for the foreseeable future. I'm a little less optimistic about KanThua's future together.

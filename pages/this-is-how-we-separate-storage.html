This is how we separate Storage & Compute // 🧵 A short story on how @NikitaShamgunov went through the *idea maze* of SQL markets to invent OSS serverless Postgres. Meet Neon ❇️ https://t.co/tIhZPNKYK5

📝 I summarized highlights from 4 interviews over past 7 years: • working at MS & FB • taking 5 years to build 1st database release • growing memSQL into @SingleStoreDB $1.3B • how @khoslaventures incubated the next killer Snowflake, @neondatabase Read on for notes👇 https://t.co/fj5esczl71

1/ Back in 2015 after releasing v1 MemSQL, Nikita shared how they applied a TuringAward winner’s observation to database workloads: online analytical vs transactional processing that was dynamically-adjustable unlike Oracle’s. https://t.co/zj4aDn1qJh

2/ Hurdle at Facebook in ~2010 was painful multi-day-long downloads of ETL (extract-transform-load) before available in Hadoop, so took lesson from @nathanmarz who *open-sourced* Apache Storm & built Lambda Architecture; spiritual successors in OSS were @RedHat & @Cloudera https://t.co/wOBZnFyD7f

3/ @Azure & @AWScloud became distribution centers, paired well with Apache Spark which had streaming, but not ⭐️ Persistence nor ⭐️ Transactionality Getting full ACID compliance important: 💽 Atomicity 💽 Consistency 💽 Isolation 💽 Durability but Cobalt/C++ devs wanted SQL… https://t.co/VcfsweBzZA

4/ By 2017 Nikita realized product-market-fit for @SingleStoreDB with customers in: ⬆️ Concurrency ⬇️ Latency ❌ eliminating “Stop & Go” uploads ✅ data available asap in unified API All achieved with low-level builds of 3 multiplication types as fundamental building block. https://t.co/K1q6EMTNoP

5/ Next step was realizing new problems, like API separation between Py-libraries & Spark + optimizing hardware usage by >2 orders of magnitude. Many more optimizations, until “latency gets squished to zero”, both Compute & Storage are total utilities. https://t.co/jFwcHEXVGI

6/ So in 2018 when M&A accelerated for *transactional data* systems, team focused on 3 sectors: 💸 Fintech 🎥 Media 🏥 Healthcare Tier1 workloads with strain from 40-database systems needed immediately-helpful product to close sales: “we couldn’t wait on 3rd party ecosystem” dev https://t.co/n6EmB15zmX

7/ So north-star became optimizing for cloudAPI 🌧 reliability to prevent “Greatest Sin of databases”: ☠️ losing data 🙅🏻‍♀️ giving wrong results Benchmark KPI became >1 Trillion rows/second. Focus moved from ~95% of in-house code to utilizing OSS PostgreSQL 🐘 parser + binder… https://t.co/W4mnC8hn60

8/ and that’s when the vision was born to go 🥀 fully-elastic 👨‍🔧 with managed-service 💻 of open-source solution 💯 & radical customer-satisfaction 🌎 in a world of exponentially-growing AI & ML workloads … https://t.co/7dCDGDPQPs

9/ which brings us to 2022 & the launch 🚀 of https://t.co/I4YirpOuYY from: 🤐 stealth incubation at @khoslaventures 🤝 with cofounders @HLinnakangas & @kelvich ✍️ & an investment memo by former Dropbox CEO @quentinclark at @GeneralCatalyst https://t.co/KgvYPkFQvX

10/ with backing from aligned angel engineers (Database Avengers … ?) like @natfriedman @ajeets @rauchg @wesmckinn @andy_pavlo @sorenbs @amasad @OlegR & others accelerating storage/compute future @eladgil @MichaelOvitz + team at @foundersfund

11/ On podcast with lead investor GGV @GlennSolomon, they dig into: 📈 the growing $100B+ TAM for database workloads ✨👀 why developers have a ‘twinkle in their eyes’ for Postgres > MongoDB or mySQL ❄️ lesson from Slootman & @SnowflakeDB on focus: just tx, SMB, cloud, postgres https://t.co/saNM1zlet2

12/ plus how: 💻 @neondatabase organically fits into CI/CD workflows 🐘 @PostgreSQL is on winning side of history 🥳 optimizing for delighting developers 📲 but unique GTM of consumer > B2B SaaS 🥊 Force-multiplier for engineers already using @vercel @Netlify @Cloudflare https://t.co/sIMpQxwtDr

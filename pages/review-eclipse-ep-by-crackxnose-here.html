disclaimer: my reviews are of underground artists, this means i will review each project in the scope of the artist's other work and their ability given their situation. so not all equal ratings mean equal quality! 10/10 underground does not = 10/10 from kanye i am not fantanoüöΩ

check out the tape !!!!! : https://t.co/6iSAKyiPCW

zarn's stuff has never really clicked with me in the past, his lyrics are very impressive (except for that peggy line) but the delivery and distortion just aren't for me, but i can appreciate the music and understand why people like that style

this project opened up my mind a bit. i paid more attention to zarn's flow and lyrics and after 3 or 4 listens the distortion stopped bothering me and it started to click. the ep starts off super strong with one of the better tracks, saturate

huge shout out to ayazra for that glitch effect at the start, it went stupid hard and made the song super attention peaking right at the start. saturate also has some of the best energy on the tape, the delivery was more lively than some of the rest felt

sacred and saccade were both solid zarn tracks, nothing stood out to me quite as much but they were well done and i enjoyed them equally. the only thing about this tape is the lack of variety, zarn usually does this style but he has slightly branched out before

and i think he could try other types of beats and experiment more with his voice/sound. i compared his tape to @stxich's new tape because stxich tried new things and had a more varied selection of sounds on the tape, and he does that in general with his music

that doesn't mean either tape is better or worse, but im just saying how it worked and made the tape more exciting. souls was definitely my favorite track, but saturate was close. i think souls was just more fun to listen to after i listened to the tape so many times

the other 2 songs are not bad at all, they just feel less unique. solid tracks, but could've done more to impress like the other songs did. the lyrics and flows were still good, and there are a couple really cool flow switches in the ep.

this new zarn ep is very solid and impressive, makes me excited for his next track since he said it would have more diversity in the beats and sound. the first time i listened to this tape i was playing cod and it was a good vibe to get my energy up

but i feel like he's trying to do more than that with his music and paying more attention i can tell he cares about his writing and a lot of the verses really had something to say or were geared in a certain direction

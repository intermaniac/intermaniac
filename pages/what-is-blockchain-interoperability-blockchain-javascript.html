What is Blockchain Interoperability? #blockchain #javascript #interoperability #web3 ðŸ§µ THREAD

(1/9) Blockchain Interoperability is the ability of two or more blockchains to communicate cross-chain and share data between each other.

(2/9) As Web3 has evolved, more and more blockchains have been launched for users to interact with. While the rise in the number of projects is generally a good thing, these blockchains have usually operated in isolation from one another.

(3/9) The number of use cases will increase as functionality can be shared between blockchains that could specialize on a single functionality. These solutions will scale much more efficiently as they will not rely on a single blockchain.

(4/9) Cross-chain Token Exchange can occur when two parties want to exchange tokens from different chains. In simple terms, cross-chain Token Exchanges can be considered trading, as the end result is two parties exchanging their tokens for one another.

(5/9) Atomic swaps with hash time-locked contracts (HTLCs) are a common technique for cross-chain token exchanges. These swaps are not only trustless, but also the simplest to implement when compared to other blockchain interoperability solutions.

(6/9) With that in mind, they are not a very practical solution. This is due to them being rather slow, mostly due to the requirement of having to properly sync between the chains.

(7/9) Unlike cross-chain token exchanges, cross-chain token transfers do not require an additional party. While there are several approaches for cross-chain token transfers, federated 2-way pegs are one of the more well known and often used.

(8/9) General cross-chain messages are the most flexible of the solutions listed here. However, it does require a standard messaging system between the chains to allow for such communication.

If you find hardship in understanding large codebase while contributing to open-source, follow the thread to speed up your process. #opensource #GitHub #SoftwareDeveloper #experience

1) Follow previous merged pull requests and understand what was the issue linked to them and how he/she solved it. Also learn the coding lingos they use in their codebase.

Like the above point simply says - check what files and functions they changed to solve a particular issue.

2) Read the tests and understand the flow, what kind of data which function process.

3) Find the blogs and documentation, they are really helpful. Blogs related to the feature implementation which developers developed few time ago. Like @fossasia projects have the best documentations I have ever seen in my open source journey.

4) Get involved the conversation with community members and just understand what they talk, you can ask whatever questions you have to them.

5) Most important fact is you must know 30-40 percent of the tech stack they have used in the project, then only you will enjoy unless you will find it really difficult.

6) Want to follow my approach, I just deep dive into project for 3-4 hours and comment out what things are doing what. Like I comment above the functions and variables about their working, it helps me a lot to understand new project. 3-4 hours of deep dive is really helpful.

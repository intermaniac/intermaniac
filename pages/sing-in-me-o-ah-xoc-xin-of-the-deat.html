Sing in me, o’ ah-xoc-xin, of the death gods’ court, abode of demons & were-jaguars, into which the hero twins descended & revived the first father, putting death to death by trickery & harrowing the underworld for evermore. a thread on maya mythology, demons & xibalbá 1/ https://t.co/yrewesu16s

the americas are impregnated with demonic energies. it’s a fact. how else could you account for the uninterrupted continuation of human & child sacrifice from the olmecs right up to the porn-possessed, high-fructose-bedevilled, opioid-saturated usa? 57/ https://t.co/sbnuqdrieo

we moderns deride the barbarity of mesoamerican infant sacrifice as we stroll gaily past decaying tenements full of the innocent, destined to be sacrificed to one or another of a constellation of modern idols: the empire, wall street, the rule of law, coca-cola et al. 58/ https://t.co/nhjmrhfibo

one of the christian tropes with the longest legs is that evil triumphs when it convinces us it doesn’t exist. well, unlike faustian culture, which knows only its own promethean potential, the maya understood that demons exist & mean us harm. 59/

they knew of certain demons—apparently the most terrifying & powerful in their region—that dwelt in the subterranean caverns of the yucatán. those who want background on their environs can look here: 60/ https://t.co/sp4o030tsy

as i recently glossed, the classic maya of the southern lowlands seemed to have had a general ambivalence to worshipping the so-called ‘death gods’. their northern brethren shed this ambivalence at some point in the terminal classic or postclassic period. 61/ https://t.co/jteooor006

they developed a somewhat exclusivistic relation to xibalbá & its denizens, offering frequent sacrifice, in both treasure & life—hence the varied bones & intricate crafts of jade & imported gold found whenever the bottom of a mexican cenote is dredged. 62/ https://t.co/dzril38rbp

this practice—which bears greater spiritual continuities with the regular sacrificial ceremonies of storied teotihuacan & its late aztec heirs—must have horrified the refugees of the classic collapse as they streamed north from the abandoned kaah of the petén basin. 63/ https://t.co/m3eunvurik

after all, the scions of the maya heartland were accustomed to the complex reenactments of cosmic bloodletting that their ancestors had instituted in honour of their own poetic creation from maize & divine blood, not the nourishment of chthonic demons with orphans & babies. 64/ https://t.co/fv3eofkix9

even from our foreign perspective, the difference isn’t trivial. unfortunately for the maya, these were the deviant practices encountered by the conquistadors, which have tarred the maya culture with a black legend as potent as anything directed at spain itself. 65/

in truth, we have only fragmentary oral traditions, incomplete codexes & our own speculations on which to base a maya demonology, which is tragically little in comparison to the occult learnings preserved out of other civilisational cycles, especially afro-eurasia. 66/ https://t.co/wvbop0drxd

on this basis, we can at least differentiate the cavern-dwelling entities of watery metnal, as worshipped by the last yucatec maya, from those celestial entities venerated by the k’iche’ people of the guatemalan highlands. 67/ https://t.co/s9virlirud

the highlanders’ patrons were by & large sky, sun & fire deities. tohil served this role for the k’iche’ people, though as time passed, he seems to have acquired the characteristics & epithets of all other deities, becoming the object of almost monotheistic worship. 68/ https://t.co/5jubhfizib

the popol vuh & surviving oral tradition among the southerners both make oblique references to the cultural ‘unsoundness’ of the northern lowlands & to the entities of xibalbá, the ‘place of fright’, populated by demons who seek only to ensnare human souls. 69/ https://t.co/kjpw2naije

this suspicion of cenotes & the religious practices surrounding them even extends to the idea that the yucatec maya are not maya, in truth, but are instead the remnants of olmecs or toltecs who conquered the north & assimilated a crude mayan dialect. 70/

as for the oral traditions of the yucatec maya, few have survived. whatever the spaniards found there they regarded as well worth exterminating. the worst of the old world diseases & more sustained exposure to roman catholic missionaries have done the rest. 71/ https://t.co/fl8v3cdmqd

there is much more to be said on this topic—we’ve barely scratched the surface. let me leave you on one final note, which ties up the timeline i’ve presented. 72/

recall that there were three attempts to create humans. well, the last attempt was *too successful* & the deities’ offspring surpassed even their creators in poetic skill & insight. so, the creators blinded them & muddled their speech into 1000 tongues. 73/ https://t.co/9juoeueviu

the occult significance of this, though not unique, is clear. in closing man's third eye, these entities blinded the maya as to the demonic nature of the entities they worshipped. the arrival of christianity, shatters this harmful illusion, restoring historical insight. 74/ https://t.co/abclwykpdf

with god’s grace, perhaps a second religiosity will sweep away the current american idolatries too, bringing an end to the modern era of human sacrifice, which has re-established itself in secular guise upon the demon-haunted ruins of the old. 75/ https://t.co/9mwny8opo1

What my experience was like getting the AWS Solution Architect - Associate cert [thread]

It’s been well over a year since I took the Solution Architect exam for AWS but prepping for the exam and actually working with clients to define their architecture has helped me solidify the knowledge I learned for this exam.

While I was prepping for this exam I had to familiarize myself with a lot of different AWS services and I had to grasp the different ways to design resilient, cost efficient, high performing, and secure architectures.

AWS emphasizes that Solution Architects should follow the best practices defined in the AWS Well-Architected Framework, this framework helps you define the pros and cons of decisions that you’ll make when building systems on AWS.

When you work with clients its common to perform Well Architected reviews on existing systems, and using the Well Architected Framework when building from scratch.

My process for prepping was simple I purchased a subscription with @acloudguru and signed up for the AWS Solution Architect Prep course, and I purchased the practice exams on whizlabs. Downloading the exam objectives also helped me narrow down on where I should focus my attention

For my exam I had to understand how networks worked, how to secure ports, how to secure subnets, how to setup VPCs, and how to get VPCs too communicate with other VPCs using VPC peering or Transit Gateways.

I feel like networking was a major part of this exam but understanding how to design systems that maintain high availability and are fault tolerant was also very important.

When you’re designing architecture you want systems to be able scale resources to meet the demand of traffic and you want to be able to recover from failures if a system goes down.

The exam was over 2 hours long with about 50-60 questions total, I had to take this exam twice the first time there was a gap in my security knowledge.

How I overcame that gap was by reviewing the different AWS services that were apart of the questions I got wrong, the AWS service that kept popping up at the time was Identity Access Management.

When building systems in AWS Identity Access Management is a big piece when it comes to setting policies and roles to make sure users/resources are authorized to use specific services and applications.

AWS IAM can start to become complex when you’re configuring multiple AWS accounts and when you need roles to be assumed by other accounts. So by simply taking the time to understand AWS IAM I was able to pass my exam the second time around.

After gaining real-world experience as a Solution Architect and reflecting on how I prepped for the exam I feel like a lot of information didn’t start to stick or solidify in my mind until I actually started working on projects.

Prepping for an exam is cool to learn new things but everything will not be memorable unless you’re constantly utilizing the information that you’ve learned.

When I took this exam I was working solely as an Engineer and I wasn’t always thinking from the perspective of a Solution Architect. As you prep for this exam I think the best thing would be to get on LucidChart and start designing architecture for actual use cases.

I think it's important to start completing labs that utilize the key services that would be needed for your specific use case.

The best experience for prepping for the real-world and tackling certifications such as AWS Solution architect is by completing actual projects, you’ll start to notice that you learn faster when you’re actually working with technology rather than just studying theory.

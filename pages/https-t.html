https://t.co/dMgLe5jAMV was built on an open source model called TorToise. In fact, that's all it used, as-is. https://t.co/YYdmcDEN2X Built by James Betker, who seemingly works at OpenAI or Google per his github and blog. ðŸ§µ

This model adapts DALL-E architecture to TTS, and it seems to work brilliantly well in creating natural speech. And I mean natural as in "it-blows-everything-else-out-of-the-water-in-what-academia-and-commercial-systems-have-seen-before" natural.

Of course it has critical limitations for it to be commercial ready as-is. One, it's way too slow (max 50 RTF). Two, because of the nature of the training data, it only generates natural speech in a conversational or podcast tone, and it generates low fidelity audio.

Nonetheless it shows great promise and ripe for some finetuning experiments. A definite knock in the head for many speech scientists out there. We were trying to beat SOTA by 0.1% and publish fancy papers for too long. We all need to be researching things that MATTER.

So how did James build such a natural TTS system? 1. Unprecedented size of training data. James collected 59,000 hours of data from the web. 2. Unprecedented size of model. 3. It brings back autoregressive (AR) architecture back to TTS, when everything was going Non-AR.

It's also telling about the future direction of AI development. Speed and openness of AI development globally is incredible, and the pervasion of new knowledge is accelerating exponentially. New AI models will become a commodity. Winner will be those who can:

1. Serve these huge models efficiently. 2. Feed these hungry models with the biggest and highest quality of proprietary data. 3. Digest these complex models into easy-to-use UX for the end user. 3 Commandments of AI commercialization.

Everytime a ground breaking model was open sourced, a slew of new startups formed. @lovolabs has been in this space for 4 years now, and we've seen our share of competitors rise and fall.

DeepVoice3 created a bunch. Then Tacotron2 came. Then Fastspeech2 created a bunch. Then most of them burned and died. It's easy to start from an open source AI model. It's not easy to fulfill the 3 Commandments of AI Commercialization and build a lasting company.

I recently had to explain what YouTube Scripting is to a group of people. So I have decided to make it a thread so everyone can learn. YouTube Scripts are a little bit different normal forms of writing. https://t.co/jLYUdjYbmY

YouTube Scripts are written to be narrated to viewers, will the aim of retaining those viewers till the end of the video. A YouTube Script forms the idea for the video. It is that script that the video editor will work with to make a YouTube video complete. https://t.co/iGEJbdcXyq

YouTube Scripts can come in different formats depending on the brains behind the content. But usually, you have three aspects. A hook, to get the viewers attention...

A body that's captivating with enough creativity to keep the viewer's attention, And a CTA at the end asking them to like the video or subscribe depending on what you want them to do.

Those are the basics. But then, you'll need to learn some elements like Foreshadowing, for suspense and to bring about curiosity, transition for retention, and the addition of on-screen elements that could make the video more fluid.

The on-screen elements are basically instructions to the video editor at some point in the script. https://t.co/YBcwhH8hqC

To write good YouTube Scripts, you can't rely on search engines alone for info. You might have to get familiar with opinionated forums like Quora, Reddit, even Twitter. Some topics might require that you talk about a general opinion so you'll need that.

You'll also need to be good at researching. Fact checking, so you don't put out the wrong info. You'll also need to be up to date on trends and memes so you can make relatable comments that you can slip into the script to create moments that viewers can vibe or resonate with. https://t.co/Goi3JTYDzY

YouTube is evolving everyday, and depending on the audience the channel is targeting, the style you might need to employ may be completely unconventional. Welcome to my Ted Talk!! RT & Follow me for more freelancing tips! https://t.co/czxSc6b9Ld

WordPress(.)org and WordPress(.)com, what's the difference? #WordPress For Beginners Thread

WordPress(.)org is where WordPress began. It's home for the now popular Content Management System (CMS), WordPress. What started as a blogging platform, #WordPress has now evolved into a powerful publishing platform that goes far beyond simple blogging.

WordPress(.)com is a proprietary blog hosting solution with limitations. It's more like a test-ground for WordPress the CMS as it uses the open-source WP platform at its core to power the free & Premium blogs they host.

Wp(.)org also hosts all the content of the open-source WP platform, from plugins to themes to knowledge blogs. If you want to learn about WordPress or download free plugins/themes, start at https://t.co/2qeS6TBC6O

WP(.)com is where you go if you want to test-drive WordPress or launch a free/premium blog/website. Here you can also buy domains and hosting and test out the features that makes WordPress the most used CMS on the internet.

Are the two related? Yes and No. .com is owned by Automattic, company founded by Matt Mullenweg, co-founder of WordPress. It's a blog hosting service; .org is owned by WordPress Foundation, a not-for-profit org aiming to make WP freely available, maintained, and developed.

Both the WP Foundation and Automattic are registered/owned by Matt, look at .com as the business side of WordPress and .org as home to the philanthropic side.

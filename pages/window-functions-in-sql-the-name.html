Window functions in SQL!! The name might sound ambiguous and scary at first but it isn’t. I’m going to compose this thread in a way to make it make sense and also drop links to post you can read up on it So here goes!!

So when you want to perform calculations with aggregate functions such as your SUM(), AVG() etc, I know what comes to mind in using the GROUP BY statement but this collapses your rows and shows distinct grouping with their aggregates on another columns Eg https://t.co/SWZCpAHnJt

But what if you do not want to collapse or reduce the rows. What if you want to perform the aggregations with the rows unaffected but still showing the aggregations That’s where Window functions come into play. An example of the comparison looks like this https://t.co/IsCkqEOGyP

There are different types of window functions 1) The Core Functions: These consist of the PARTITION BY which performs the work of a GROUP BY functions. It is also a sub clause of the OVER clause. It also consist of the Aggregate functions such as the COUNT(), SUM(), AVG() etc https://t.co/dPyj7J6Bbl

2) Ranking Functions: These include functions that perform ranking operations. They are the ROW_NUMBER(), RANK() and the DENSE_RANK(). It has also been an interview question to differentiate between them. The difference comes in how they handle ties https://t.co/FgNOzSSMVg

Then we have also have Advanced Functions which are the Aliases, the percentiles using NTILE() then LEAD/LAG functions and so much more. For these advanced functions, I’d leave you to do more research on them. They are also as interesting as the previous ones

Resources you can go through to learn more https://t.co/0XIlpGPNrc https://t.co/BaowaXqiBb

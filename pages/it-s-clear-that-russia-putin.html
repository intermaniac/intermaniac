Thread: It's clear that Russia (Putin) is using what are ordinarily legitimate legal means to ensure no one can prevent him from his goals. Here's my take and why it should matter to you. 1/12 https://t.co/8gJcaBGW0Y

Russia is hosting referendums in Ukraine to "allow Ukrainians to vote" on the Russian annexation of the territory. We all know this is both a normally valid and legitimate process that is going to be done in name only. The voice of Ukrainians will not be honestly represented.2/12

Once a "legitimate referendum" shows the outcome that Russia has "legitimately" annexed the 4 territories, Russia then has a right to protect its sovereign territory. 3/12

One way that countries protect and enforce their borders is through military means. This requires a great number of soldiers. One way of ensuring that the military has enough soldiers is through conscription (the draft). 4/12

According to the article above: Russia has also recently passed legislation imposing criminal penalties on soldiers for desertion, surrender or refusal to follow orders during “mobilization” and “martial law". 5/12

Despite previously indicating that he did not want to impose conscription on Russian citizens, he is now considering this option. 6/12

Why now? It seems to me that he will be conscripting those from the newly annexed territories (Ukrainians) to fight against those who try and interfere. 7/12

So, now we have a "legitimate" Russian territory expansion and "legitimate" raising of armed forces. He will have a right to defend that territory and will use the very people who live there, to fight against those who seek to interfere. 8/12

He will force this by those strict "criminal penalties" which likely include the death penalty or other equally draconian measure. I would not be surprised if they had the "humane" option to go to a "work camp" for the remainder of their time in the Russian Army. 9/12

So why does this matter to you? If Russia can waltz into Ukraine on the world stage, impose a sham referendum and threaten nuclear war against those to try and stop him, any attempt to stop him will cause another world war. 10/12

The rest of the world has two options. Stand up (despite not having formal alliance with Ukraine - which is why we haven't used military means yet) or let Ukraine fall. If we stand up without an alliance, against "Russian Territory" it will be seen as an act of war. 11/12

All of this is to say, pay attention. Take notice. If he can do this to Ukraine, anyone can do it anywhere. It will set a dangerous precedent. This is a repeat of history and we cannot allow a policy of appeasement or complacency to happen again. 12/12

I am more than happy to be told my theory is wrong. I deeply hope it is. I welcome constructive discussion on this thread.

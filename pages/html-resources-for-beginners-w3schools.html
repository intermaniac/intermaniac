W3schools - https://t.co/oWp1LSVRVJ Probably the best resource to learn and practice HTML. It will give you insights into almost all the important tags and their usage.

Bonus reading material: Mozilla docs - https://t.co/LUV5lisZ8n FreeCodeCamp - https://t.co/0vDQFRm1mS

Sololearn HTML's course - https://t.co/ieEsWJFDU1 This course is amazing to get started as it is in the form of cards and they have a structured format which allows you to learn HTML effectively. Bonus: You get a free certificate once you complete the course.

Youtube videos you can refer to: - FreeCodeCamp - https://t.co/oRHXT7p9Tb - Apna College(for those who want to learn in Hindi) - https://t.co/oPCI3gjijj - Programming with Mosh - https://t.co/no2n9bnxQ0

Focus on learning the tags and attributes for the specific tags. Some of the tags that are worth diving deep are: paragraph, text formatting tags, heading, hyperlinks, images and media, lists, table, form and input, HTML5 semantic elements.

I would highly suggest reading the docs of W3schools and MDN than watching a video. Videos are helpful to get started, but documentation improves your in-depth knowledge, and you tend to remember things.

Interview questions to look into: Interview bit - https://t.co/LQQWKCRglj JavatPoint - https://t.co/gFwA36g4tD

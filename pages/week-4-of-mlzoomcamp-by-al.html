WEEK 4 of #mlzoomcamp by @Al_Grigor @DataTalksClub 👇👇👇 https://t.co/3XTzv2WPf4 🧵Summary thread!! #programming #MachineLearning #DataScience #DataScientist #Python

4.1 Evaluation metrics: Session overview This week is about different metrics to evaluate a binary classifier: ▶️Accuracy 🎯 ▶️Confusion table ▶️Precision 💯 ▶️Recall 💬 ▶️ROC curves ↩️ ▶️AUROC ▶️Cross-validation 🤞

4.2 Accuracy 🎯 This metric measures the fraction of correct predictions. Specifically, it is the number of correct predictions divided by the total number of predictions. https://t.co/I8taLENhRo

We can change the decision threshold, it should not be always 0.5. But, in this particular problem, the best decision cutoff, associated with the highest accuracy (80%), was indeed 0.5. https://t.co/cK5HqJb2qf

4.3 Confusion Table This table is a way to measure different types of errors and correct decisions that binary classifiers can made. Considering this information, it is possible evaluate the quality of the model by different strategies. https://t.co/sZmVvC7t12

We have the following scenarios: ▶️No churn-Negative class🚫 - Customer did not churn-True Negative (TN)✅🚫 - Customer churned-False Negative (FN)❎🚫 ▶️Churn - Positive class⭕️ - Customer churned-True Positive (TP)✅⭕️ - Customer did not churn-False Positive (FP)❎⭕️ https://t.co/x74cmz44mE

4.4 Precision 💯 & Recall 💬 ▶️Precision tell us the fraction of positive predictions that are correct. It takes into account only the positive class. https://t.co/T4pIIZkMbD

▶️ Recall measures the fraction of correctly identified positive instances. It considers parts of the positive and negative classes. https://t.co/PZU2KfAKni https://t.co/XfnPHPZN8U

4.5 ROC curves ↩️ ▶️ FPR is the fraction of false positives (FP) divided by the total number of negatives. ▶️TPR or Recall is the fraction of true positives (TP) divided by the total number of positives. https://t.co/Szt27nNBTl

4.6 Area Under Curve The AUC can tell us how good is our model with a single value. It can be interpreted as the probability that a randomly selected positive example has a greater score than a randomly selected negative example. https://t.co/qZDDUWeogK

4.7 Cross Validation ▶️ Cross-validation refers to evaluating the same model on different subsets of a dataset, getting the average prediction. https://t.co/2E7kq3Mpzr https://t.co/Jw2lh71RYY

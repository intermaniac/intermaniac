1/ #algofam governors and potential xGovs. I am requesting feedback on some thoughts I am looking to add to the #ARC33 github review, but would like to refine further before doing so. I have reached out to a number of people individually about the following, and now to you...üßµ

2/ Intro: I stumbled upon #algorand not long ago when the idea of an economically and technologically approachable governance system that was built on the back of a secure blockchain seemed like exactly what the world needed to address many of its political problems...

3/ The idea that not only could #algo become a leader in #DeFi and "banking the unbanked", but also governmental models was immediate and I felt the call to be a part of this process as quickly as I could. I missed the deadline for G3 by a few days, but spent that time learning..

4/ ..more about the ecosystem and getting to know the beautiful people that were going to be a part of this journey. I say this so that you'll hopefully believe me when I tell you, it is my heartfelt desire to see #algorand become the absolute best incarnation of itself...

5/ I live in the U.S., and before the country was founded and smarter men than I were trying to decide how to structure their would-be government, a man named Thomas Jefferson had an idea of a direct governmental model that much resembles what we already have here on #algo...

6/ This model allowed for every qualified voter (in our case Eligible Governor) to have a vote in any and all proposals. It is only now, with the technology we have, that this form of governance is really even possible to implement efficiently, so you're excused if you had'nt..

7/..considered this parallel. However, with the implementation of #ARC33 #xGov, we will be moving closer to a Representative Democracy than the model above. With General Governance already including a reward-bearing structure, it easy to see how we are creating career govs...

8/...already, just as we have career politicians in the U.S. Many of these people start with best intentions, but the temptation for financial gain or desire for power or fear of its loss will drive people to betray themselves and the best interests of their constituents...

9/ Body: I am seeking feedback from the #algofam govs on the following before posting a more thorough github review: 1. Would you participate (if able) in an #xGov program that had ZERO rewards if your xGov voting power was equated to your General Governance stake?

10/ 2. Would you participate (if able) in an #xGov program where you "opt-in" to a particular category (DeFi, NFT, Infrastructure, etc) and only receive votes on those Verticals, or have an "abstain" option and able to vote on all xGov proposals?

11/ 3. Would you participate (if able) in an #xGov program where your $algo stake in Gen Gov reflected (if eligible) your xGov chance to be selected to vote on a given proposal, similar to how current node structure works? (Example: 1000 $algo committed to Gen Gov. Also xGov....

12/ ...Smart Contract executes a verifiable random function to select from all eligible $algo committed in xGov wallets. After populating enough eligible, DIFFERENT wallets in this manner to ensure a large enough sample size, an xGov proposal could then be voted on by the..

13/ selected, eligible xGovs to be passed on for veto or General Governance. So in our example, we have 1000 $algo stake representation in xGov out of a total 100,000. SC wants min. of 50 wallets to vote. VRF rolls to pick an algo out of those 100,000 staked until it populates..

14/..the given sample size.) This methodology, if implemented correctly (and I am quite sure this needs to be refined), might also be a measure of defense against some forms of abuse. So with that being said, would you participate or support a structure (or something similar)?

15/ I'm quite sure I haven't explained this quite right and I hope this thread (for those that managed to make it through) and the polls might shed some insight into a way forward for #algorand #xGov in 2023! Thank you all for those that will comment and participate! ‚ô•Ô∏è

@USENIXSecurity üßê Research Questions üßê RQ1) What personal info, if any, is leaked when using TOTP backups? RQ2) What is the risk of an attacker obtaining a TOTP backup? RQ3) What is the risk of an attacker compromising the TOTP secret(s) stored within an obtained TOTP backup?

üë©‚Äçüî¨ Methods üë®‚Äçüî¨ We found 22 Android TOTP apps with 100k+ installs & a backup mechanism. For each: 1) Recorded plaintext traffic while using, backing up, & recovering 2) Performed cryptanalysis to determine which crypto primitives, if any, were used and how they were configured

Key Finding üîëüîé TOTP 2FA is often promoted as more secure than SMS 2FA, but we found that most TOTP backup strategies end up placing trust in the same technologies that TOTP 2FA is meant to supersede: passwords, SMS, and email.

Key Finding üîëüîé 4 of 22 apps relied *only* on SMS to authenticate the user during recovery. An attacker w/ control of a victim's phone number can access the TOTP backup! - @saaspass (plaintext backups) - @Authy, @yandexcom, and @salesforce (encrypted backups) https://t.co/niQYB5olV9

Key Finding üîëüîé 15 of 22 apps supported encrypted backups. Most had serious cryptographic flaws, making ciphertexts vulnerable to trivial offline attacks. 14 apps derive a key from a password. Most had severely inadequate pwd policies, used weak KDFs, and/or weak KDF configs. https://t.co/3v9Cf35akF

Key Finding üîëüîé Some apps *only* encrypted the TOTP secret. Users of @Authy and @Zoho OneAuth who enable cloud backups are unknowingly sending those companies the names of the websites/services they use and the usernames for their accounts on those platforms.

Finding üîé 2 apps required users to provide PII to the app developer in order to use the app at all, even if the backup mechanism was not enabled. @Authy required email address and phone #. @Telefonica's Latch required email address.

@Authy @Telefonica Finding üîé Cloud backups required PII to authenticate users during recovery. Common PII collected was email (8), phone # (5), and name (4). @Microsoft Authenticator cloud backups required most PII of any app that we analyzed: phone #, email, name, and date of birth.

Finding üîé 12 of 22 apps supported plaintext TOTP backups. - 10 supported the Android sharing menu (e.g., email, SMS, etc.), copying to the clipboard, and/or exporting to file - 3 supported plaintext cloud sync - Few warned about the risk of plaintext backups

Finding üîé 7 of 22 apps used QR codes to manually transfer TOTP secrets between devices. Several bury this feature below multiple menu layers, decreasing the odds that users will find the feature unless specifically looking for it.

‚≠êÔ∏è Recommendation ‚≠êÔ∏è TOTP apps should encrypt *all* TOTP fields, including the secret, issuer (website name), and label (username). Including any TOTP fields in backups as plaintext violates user privacy.

‚≠êÔ∏è Recommendation ‚≠êÔ∏è TOTP apps that rely on remote key servers to generate/store random keys should choose different entities for storage of keys and ciphertext; if both are stored with a single entity, then they can decrypt the TOTP backups.

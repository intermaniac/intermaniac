Organizations will store historical records securely with decentralized solutions like #IPFS and easily retrieve them with the help of #ENS, while keeping temporary data accessible through cloud storage solutions like #AWS.

For example,a healthcare company might use AWS to store and process sensitive patient data,while using IPFS and ENS to store and share immutable medical records.They can assign an ENS domain name to the CIDs of the records, making it easy to access and share the data.

Another example is a Supply Chain company that can use IPFS and ENS to store immutable records of products origin and data throughout the supply chain, while using AWS for more flexible data such as real-time inventory and logistics data.

An online media platform could use IPFS and ENS to store and share multimedia content such as images, videos, and audio files, while using AWS for more dynamic content such as user-generated comments and analytics data.

A financial services company can use IPFS and ENS to store immutable records of transactions and compliance data, while using AWS for more transactional data like customer information, risk analytics, and fraud detection.

In each of these examples, the combination of cloud storage and decentralized storage provides a powerful and flexible solution that can address specific use cases, while the use of ENS allows for the easy accessibility and sharing of the data.

What's the oracle problem and how @redstone_defi fixes it in the most cost-efficient way possible? [THREAD] https://t.co/ySwOoNACXE

Let's assume you and I will bet on the result of tonight's #WorldCup game between #Iran and #USA through a decentralized platform. You bet $50 on USA win, I bet $50 on Iran win. A total of $100 is held in an escrow by a smart contract.

The game ends and Iran wins. Now the smart contract should release the funds to me. But how does the smart contract know that Iran has won the game? https://t.co/kqENOM70st

Answer: The smart contract requires an oracle mechanism to get accurate data off-chain and deliver it to the blockchain in a secure and reliable manner. This way, the smart contract will know Iran has won and will release the funds to you.

Smart contracts can not inherently interact with data existing outside their native blockchain environment. Data external to the blockchain is called "off-chain", while data stored on the blockchain is called "on-chain".

Blockchain needs an additional infrastructure to securely interoperate with off-chain data. Oracles come into play here to bridge the off-chain and on-chain environments. A recent decentralized oracle solution @redstone_defi got $7M seed investment led by @lemniscap. https://t.co/aqXKuutnuN

Redstone offers a different design of oracles catering for the needs of modern DeFi protocols. Here it's comparison of simplified Redstone oracle architecture and standard oracle architecture. What are the advantages of Redstone oracles? https://t.co/7QujvPLkNx

Redstone does not upload information on blockchain every couple of minutes as the majority of oracles do. Instead, it stores data on @ArweaveTeam, while a network of nodes and partners such as @streamr makes them available to DeFi apps  in a form of decentralized public cache. https://t.co/gromxxUxup

It leverages a new generation of blockchain storage thanks to @ArweaveTeam, which was designed to keep large amounts of data at a fraction of the cost. Low operating costs allow them to process more data with a higher update frequency. https://t.co/TkcG2Qz1Su

RedStone can source various types of data — they’re not limited to price data for assets only. A lending pool requires time-averaged data not to liquidate users for a price flash-movement, however a synthetic DEX will want to receive the most up-to-date data. https://t.co/Um3cq5ydSc

Allowing multiple data providers generates a need to curate them and select the most reliable ones, hence data providers need to stake RedStone tokens as collateral ensuring that they will keep operating and providing high quality data. https://t.co/aUKWy7Veu4

It also keeps a full record of the provider’s activity that will be permanently available on Arweave so anyone could question a data feed and receive a part of that collateral in case the data is misrepresented.

RedStone’s data is cryptographically signed by providers and can be verified on any chain that supports basic cryptographic primitives. As a result, they abstract storage from usage. Data at RedStone is persisted on Arweave, but it could be used with any other blockchain. https://t.co/Gi2KtSynBH

There's also insurance. Downtime or incorrect data are covered by the provider's stake. Redstone already works on 30+ chains and gets the data from tens of different sources. A unique tech and a talented team is all you need to solve a crucial DeFi pain point. They have both.

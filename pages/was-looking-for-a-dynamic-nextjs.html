Was looking for a dynamic #Nextjs site experience with automatic deployment and scaling. Tried AWS AppRunner, didn't work out so well. ðŸ§µ

1/ Provisioning is quite odd. Deleting and recreating a service takes too much time. Associating custom domains takes plenty of time too, and throws error often.

2/ When we used two temporary custom domains and then we deleted one of them, the second one stopped working too. Seems like internally it tries to merge TLS certificates into one shared one and then it causes downtime by deleting the working cert and getting a new one.

3/ Changing a health check type from tcp to http? You have to recreate (destroy & create) your entire service, along with its associated custom domains. Can you imagine a ~30 minutes downtime just to change a health check?

4/ The logging tab doesn't stream application logs - you better switch to CloudWatch directly.

5/ Finally running, started deploying new versions of our app. You think it should be fast, right? We experienced deployments that took even 30 minutes. Didn't figure out why.

6/ And then we wanted to use HTTPS. That works. But redirecting HTTP to HTTPS? Not supported. (You have to point your domain to Cloudfront and solve it there.)

7/ The service needs to talk HTTP. If you want to run a consumer/worker on the side as a standalone service, you can't - unless you implement a webserver in the worker just to answer health checks.

I was to one to suggest AppRunner but @MarekBartik is the one who got the grey hair. Sorry, mate! If you can improve the above @amitguptaataws will happily give it another try.

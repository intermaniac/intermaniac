What are oracles? Oracles are one of the key components of web3 infrastructure stack that act as a bridge between on-chain and off-chain worlds

On top of the layer 1s, it is crucial that the infrastructural layer like oracles be as decentralized and permissionless as possible. This is because it allows anyone to participate and access information without any gatekeepers and censorship ‚ùå

Due to high cost of implementation for data feeds, most oracle services in the market currently have a more permissioned model whereby the core team has to curate and approve data feeds

Ultimately, this shows that oracles are not meant to be a one-size-fits-all model. There is an urgent need for oracles that allow for greater customizability and a lower barrier to entry for setting up data feeds according to their needs.

@switchboardxyz is a permissionless and highly customizable oracle that aims to be decentralized and intuitive. The diagram below shows how its various stakeholders work together to collect and publish data, as well as their incentive mechanisms. https://t.co/2xcsteM3vO

With unique propositions such as DAO voting and low-code UI for constructing new feeds, anyone can create composable data feeds of even non-price feeds (such as a feed to track the number of people in space) easily!

@switchboardxyz is also the first and only oracle with Verifiable Random Function (VRF) on @Solana, allowing for fair random number generation

The future of @switchboardxyz is exciting, with greater decentralization with community governance via the Switchboard DAO as well as support for multi-chain such as @AptosLabs, @SuiNetwork and @NearProtocol

We look forward to working with @switchboardxyz in the future to continue building towards a true permissionless and decentralized future for oracles.

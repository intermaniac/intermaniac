I've been tweeting for the past year so you can ace #CX and #customerservice Here's what I think you need to look out for in 2023!

#customerservice is already difficult because, The nature of work is demanding Companies have conflicting/unclear priorities The work environment has a huge impact on quality

On top of all this, customer expectations are skyrocketing day by day. So here are 6 challenges ahead of every service team.

1# Ticket overload You just can't afford to be understaffed. More customers mean more tickets means more agents. The math is simple yet undeniable.

2# No proper tools If your support tool doesn't enable your agents, it's coming in the way. Track your metrics to find where your tools can perform better.

3# Lack of Crisis management Customer service is the realm of the unexpected. Sometimes it's more important to have a policy in place that helps manage the unexpected instead of planning as you go.

4# Misunderstood needs and pain points Service is a game of communication. If your service team can't connect with the customers, their job just gets more difficult. Understand the pain points, then figure out the best way your business solves them.

5# Unreasonable demands We've all heard ridiculous demands. As time goes on, they're only getting more ridiculous. Your company policy needs to be clear on how to handle these.

6# No escalation protocol When a service interaction goes beyond the scope of service, your agents have nothing much to do. Unless you have a clear pipeline for escalation.

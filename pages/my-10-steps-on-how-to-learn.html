My 10 steps on how to learn Python for newbies. A Thread!! 1/11

1. Get rid of the I’m too old to learn how to program mentality. You are never too young or too old to learn how to program. And don’t worry about the wasted years, as I once knew a friend who committed 6 months to learn how to program, and he’s doing quite well. 2/11

2. Develop computational thinking skills. Programming isn’t about learning a tool like Python, JavaScript, etc. It’s all about developing a skill that allows you to take a complex problem, understand what the problem is about and develop a possible solution. 3/11

3. Spend quality time gathering quality resources. There are tons of information out there on the internet, both free and paid. So spend some time seeing which ones resonate well with you. 4/11

4. Don’t spend time learning the basics for too long to start building projects. The best ways to develop your skills in programming is by the building project. It can be a big project or mini-ones. If you're starting out, pick a mini-one you can finish in < 2 weeks. 5/11

5. Learn data structures and algorithms and solve lots of coding problems. Starting with your journey, you might not understand how useful they are. However when you want to optimize your a solution for your app, this comes in handy. Also tech recruiters ask them a lot. 6/11

6. Practice Object-oriented programming. You might not understand the importance of it at the start; however, its purpose is to exchange messages within your program. Also it makes it programming much easier to: work with a large team, maintain, debug and test your code. 7/11

7. Create a blog or a YouTube channel and a social media account. This help you keep track of your project throughout time and also helps you standout with looking for a job/clients to work with. 8/11

8. Learn how to work with Content Management System (CMS): Git/GitHub. It’s a good way to share the code you’ve done, and it’s an important part of software development to help you keep track of changes you’ve made in your code and also make Collaboratory work much easier. 9/11

9. Finding out how to solve problems you can’t solve. When you don’t know how to solve a problem, you need to be quite comfortable using google (StackOverflow, blogpost, GitHub Repos, YouTube) to find similar problems or concepts that will help you tackle that problem. 10/11

10. My Final Tip: No more tips. Ran out of them! Kidding. Just keep practicing and try to learn something new everyday. 11/11

Running and maintaining your #WordPress site can be daunting. 🥱Not to mention, the valuable time it takes from time focused on growth and getting paid. The great thing about WordPress is that you can automate several of these time consuming tasks.🧵

Backup! 🔙 Sleep well at night knowing you have a complete backup of your site in case something terrible happens 😲 @UpdraftPlus allows you to automate updates and backups on your site 🤩

Security Scans 🔍 WordPress is the most popular CMS on the internet. But that means hackers will try to take advantage of sites with no security. Use plugins like @wordfence or @iThemesSecurity to protect your site and users.

Updating Plugins, Themes, and WP As of WordPress 5.5, you can turn on automatic updates, but if you have multiple sites to update, it can be complicated. Use a plugin like @managewp Worker to manage multiple WordPress websites from a single dashboard.

Recurring Speed Tests 🚀 Website speed matters. If your website takes too long to load, you’ll not only annoy your website visitors but also risk losing sales. @gtmetrix can set up recurring tests. https://t.co/WeGIGn8Imd

Schedule Posts and Social Media 🗓️ The secret to staying consistent is to batch create and schedule! Use a plugin like @jetpack to schedule content to your site and social platforms. At PMPro, we use @hootsuite to schedule our social content.

Content Dripping 💧 We talked dripping before and we’ll say it again: drip content is an excellent way to automate value-delivery through “new” content! 🤩If you missed our thread, check it out! https://t.co/4xWKgkCmmv

Form Submission Notifications 📳 Forms are an integral part of any website. But who wants to manually check their emails for submission?? Not me! @gravityforms , @ws_form & @NinjaForms have built-in notification features to send the right subs to the right team member. 🪄

RSS-Based Newsletter Campaign Your email list wants to hear from you, but manually curating that weekly email can eat up lots of time. Automate weekly email via RSS. Email Marketing services like @Mailchimp & @ConvertKit can send automated broadcasts when you publish a new post.

Image Compression Images can slow your site down. Especially large images. Use a plugin like Smush by @wpmudev to automatically compress your images upon uploading to your site.

Welcome New Members 📣 You can easily set up a welcome email to go out to all new users as soon as they purchase one of your membership plans. We make it easy with customizable membership confirmation emails. https://t.co/vR9RkHc6rh

Website Downtime Notifications When your site goes down, you lose money. If you don’t know why it’s down and when it went down, you can lose even more money. @jetpack has Downtime Monitoring to keep tabs on your site 24/7. Immediately get notified and tackle the problem!

Spam Prevention and Cleanup 🧹 When running a website, spam becomes a part of your life. But you don’t have to let it ruin your day. Use automation tools like @akismet to detect and delete spam automatically.

Check for Broken Links 🔗 Manually checking for broken links can take hours, if not days 😒Use a plugin like Broken Link Checker to check your website for broken links and improve SEO automatically. Then spend a few minutes each week to fix them. 😃

Database Optimization .@WpOptimize can automatically clean your database to ensure your website’s performance isn’t bogged down by unnecessary things stored in the database.

We shared 14 ways to #automate your #WordPress site. How else are you automating yours?? 👀 https://t.co/85TRpdFPWZ

The #Azure #podcast - Ctrl+Alt+Azure - we started with @zimmergren in October 2019 turns 3 this week. After 156 episodes I thought it would be fun to recap the adventure a bit. A quick üßµ

I was in-between jobs, had some free time, and we had been planning to do something together for months. At the time, we used Skype as our backchannel chat, and we kept talking about the idea of a podcast.

Then, one day, we just figured, let's do this. We set the plan to publish one episode each week. Otherwise, it felt like we'd forget the show or our audience would forget about us. Let me reassure you - it's a lot of work to push 52 episodes yearly.

We plan a lot at times - and other times, we cut it really short. The most challenging time we recorded was precisely 1 hour before we had to push our episode out. Since then, we've optimized more - usually, we have a buffer of 3-5 episodes at any given time.

This helps us navigate the times when life hits us - flu season, the crisis at work/family stuff/just being too busy. We want to keep this fun and not an obligation or work. That's why each episode aims for 30 minutes, usually not longer.

@zimmergren lives in Sweden, and I live in Helsinki. The time difference is 1 hour. We don't have a fixed recording slot, but we usually check bi-weekly for a few longer slots and try to record 2 at a time. Initially, we thought we'd meet in person and record 10 at a time.

Then the pandemic started. I think we haven't seen each other in person since ~2018. When we record, we have a video and use a shared #OneNote for the episode's structure. Typically it's just, "hey, what's up? Okay hit record," and off we go. One take for each episode.

At times I've recorded in a school break room, an empty (echo-y) winter cabin bedroom, and from a customer site. Mostly from our home offices, though, with proper equipment. We use #Shure microphones and previously Zoom for capturing audio.

A few months ago, we switched to #MicrosoftTeams for video + audio recording, and we yank the video away before post-processing. We publish via @TransistorFM which provides stats & the website (https://t.co/EUlEjbXrFs) also.

Sometimes it's tough to come up with a topic. We focus fiercely on all things #Azure - and we want to provide quality insights & real-world experience. Many topics are a product of the work we do each week.

I'm immensely grateful to Tobi for carrying the show's weight with me. He takes care of the post-processing logistics, and I take care of the publishing. We take turns with the topics. This wouldn't work unless we'd like this, and working with a pro like Tobi is fantastic.

We're also very thrilled with how the show has been received. The first few months, we dabbled in the dark. "Is anyone listening?" since then, we've hit new all-time highs a few times each year. THANK YOU for listening & commenting & reaching out & sharing! ü§ó

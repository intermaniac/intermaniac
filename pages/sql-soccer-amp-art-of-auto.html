SQL, soccer, & art of auto maintenance. "Didn't you say you wanted to check the oil before we leave?" my 15 year old daughter reminded me yesterday afternoon. I'm glad she reminded me. A short thread. 1/6

https://t.co/dzceq97SFg

We were about to return home from a soccer tournament in Gulfport, Mississippi. The drive would keep us in the car for the next 6 hours. 2/6

All of the gauges were fine. None of the maintenance or warning lights were on. There was no indication that the car needed oil. But I checked anyway. Why? Because checking the fluids is a good idea even if there is no indication that anything is wrong. 3/6

The same is true for your SQL Servers. The lack of complaints from users doesn't mean the server is healthy. The absence of performance issues doesn't guarantee that there are not problems under the hood. It's important to check. 4/6

We do that for our clients with a Daily Health Check. We "check the oil" so to speak to ensure the system is healthy. 5/6

By the way, I'm glad I checked my vehicle's oil level. It was low. And because I discovered that before starting our journey, I was able to address it and help to prevent potential problems before bad things happened. 6/6

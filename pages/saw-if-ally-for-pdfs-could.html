Saw a thread on if “#ally for PDFs could be achieved”. It can. My questions are: 1. How complicated, expensive and fragile is the workflow? 2. How complicated and fragile is the use context? 3. How do the answers to these 2 questions compare to other things (HTML, ODT)

1. The PDF workflow It can go wrong when: - text is turned into pictures rather than staying as text (‘rasterised’) - needing Acrobat Professional ($$$) to check and fix PDFs which almost all do - this is a niche, expensive skill and customers usually can’t check if it was done

Meaning that if you get told a PDF is “accessible” it: - probably isn’t - you probably can’t check Being able to do these things means putting your faith in a single company. And that’s not really working out well for us as a species.

2. The use context With the right device and app, PDFs can overcome their biggest, baked-in, design flow: fixed with. Don’t you dare try opening it on chrome on your phone or anything though.

Meaning: just because it “can” be accessible to folk using high magnification. Or a phone. But if part of that delicate balance is broken, even the "accessible" PDF you made isn't accessible to the person using it.

3. How does PDF compare to HTML and document formats (HTML, ODT, DOCX)? This is more nuanced. You can make all these formats inaccessible if you do the wrong things. But most of the time it’s: - harder to break them - easier and cheaper to check and fix (maybe by the reader)

Which means: - Putting content just in a PDF is bad. - But if you’re offering it in HTML or something else, also making a PDF is not always bad. If you only read and remembered the second half of the second point, have a think about why.

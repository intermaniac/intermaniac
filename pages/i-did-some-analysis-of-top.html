I did some analysis of Top Users in HackerNews, just for fun. Let's take a look! PS: I'll post the Notebook with the source code (in Python) and the dataset (+600MB CSV) scraped using Algolia's API at the end of this ðŸ§µ. #DataAnalysis #DataAnalytics #DataScience

First, TOP 10 of users with MOST submissions. The numbers are WILD! @0xDEADBEEFCAFE leads with 25,690 posts! Followed by @__tosh, @bookofjoe, @prostoalex, @mooreds and more. https://t.co/52MDBVrFk5

Next up, users with most points. Most upvoted users. Top user is ingve, with 357,248 points. Followed by the usual suspects (@__tosh, @prostoalex, @0xDEADBEEFCAFE) but also new users: @tod, @dancow and the great @danluu. https://t.co/j1RfszZsYa

There's clearly a correlation between submissions and total points (each submission gets by default 1 point). So, who are the best performers in terms of points/submissions? Most are users with a single highly upvoted submission (like @ews and @OhNoItsFusl) https://t.co/M06AI7fNlA

But for users with multiple submissions, the correlation is more interesting. Here's a scatter plot showing Points vs Number of Submissions for the most prolific users. We find the usual suspects + @shawndumas and @seligerj among others. https://t.co/g4DzOLwDMJ

Now we move to a TS analysis. How are the submissions of the top submitting users distributed across time? Here are number of total submissions grouped every 6 months: https://t.co/ilwgIYexOW

And here's the number of upvotes received by top upvoted users grouped every 6 months: https://t.co/vOxnnBrGrv

More interestingly, we can see the progression of earned points by the top users using a Cumulative Sum (.cumsum() with Pandas). Impresive run by @tod! https://t.co/QwnwXRWgAq

Now we move to the Builders, the Shippers, the get-shit-done-ers. Those users with most "Show HN" submissions. Congrats to @alexellisuk, @fiatjaf, @t411 and @bdehaaff among others. https://t.co/Z5qsTSkZi4

And aside from number of submissions, those with the most points earned in Show HN submissions are: @jgthms, @stavros, @o_lalonde, @alexellisuk (again) and @FiloSottile among others. https://t.co/nTpUU4NS6S

Now it's time for the discussion creators: the ones with most Ask HN posts. There's 1 outlier here, the whoishiring bot, that every month posts the classic "Who is Hiring" post. I'll exclude it from the analysis. We find @tysonmaly, @HarshSikka and @Teren_Teh among others. https://t.co/wiaBiXKaoN

And here are the "askers" with the highest upvotes (most points collected in Ask HN type posts) . Welcome @deedubs! https://t.co/rxja6UnzMy

But what about lately? Here are the users with most submissions and most upvotes in 2022 (welcome @paul_houle, @DangHoangTuan20 and @zdw to the list) https://t.co/lQUzGRTlrE

And here we can see the weekly breakdown of submissions and points earned: https://t.co/So1YcMvYAf

And finally, as promised, here's the source code: https://t.co/hNj3DNik5A The dataset was generated by scraping Algolia's API. I wrote a simple Python library for that: https://t.co/JFfD3XuZi6 A fairly updated Dataset can be found in Kaggle: https://t.co/YRGh3TXtHu

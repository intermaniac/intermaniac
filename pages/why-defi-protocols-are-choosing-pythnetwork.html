7/ 2. Pyth publishes a confidence interval because, in real markets, there is no one single price for a product. At any given time, Ethereum trades at different prices at different venues around the world. While these prices are typically similar, they can diverge for

8/ a number of reasons, such as when a cryptocurrency exchange block withdrawals on an asset. Alternatively, prices at different venues can differ simply because an asset is highly volatile at a particular point in time.

9/ Pyth represents these possibly-different prices by giving its users a probability distribution over price instead of just a single price.

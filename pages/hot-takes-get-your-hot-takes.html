HOT TAKES! GET YOUR HOT TAKES! HOT TAKES HERE! Spice: 🌶🌶🌶🌶🌶 🧵a thread full of 💣💥 on the state of #javascript frameworks, patterns, next/vercel+react and especially SPAs to follow. Preamble: remember, those of us building with lots of JS are a small niche.

Hot Take 1: The era of SPAs is just beginning, not ending.

Hot Take 2: Islands, Server Components, SSR first, Resumability are just the latest "silver bullets" that will be anything but: they ignore basic truths around DOM size limitations and Network. They are 1% solves for 1% of cases and will largely be meaningless in the future.

Hot Take 3: WebComponents will never succeed at replacing the use cases for SPAs. The idea they will replace them is at best a delusion, their use cases are extremely niche within the world of SPAs. They will however dominate JS-lite architectures for a huge portion of the web.

Hot Take 4: All JS/"JS killer" frameworks have gotten the fundamental problem statement of what devs are trying to do that led to them building SPAs wrong, which leads to a cycle of chasing their tail optimizing the wrong things.

Hot Take 5: an extension of (2,4) - in addition to getting the problem statement wrong, recent frameworks are optimizing for things that JS-heavy devs were never building in the first place. I'd describe it mostly as "searching for the WordPress killer".

Hot Take 6: probably the least spicy of these takes. The amount of JS that is too much JS for the things people are actually building js-heavy apps for is 2 orders of magnitude larger than what pretty much every dev-evangelist/framework-author is wrongly attempting to sell you on

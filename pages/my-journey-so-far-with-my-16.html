MY JOURNEY SO FAR WITH MY 16 YEAR OLD TRAINEE, OLUWATOBILOBA {A THREAD}. Yesterday, my trainee Tobi a Secondary school grad wowed me with his day 8 assignment solution after teaching him basic concepts like: HTML links, images, tables, forms, lists, (let's go the beginning) 1/ https://t.co/AtDr3qlqqN

On the 8th of this month, an old friend chatted me up that his younger brother needed a Web and app development tutor and I offered to help. Before that, my prior experience teaching development is at my place of work where I sometimes guide our interns whenever they're stuck. 2/ https://t.co/V90iZQeBeX

As a Science Education "hanging" Unilorin graduate (every Nigerian student will understand this, LOL), I proceeded to prepare the course contents, instructional and behavioural objectives, and also reference materials. Then I started with him on the 17th of this month. 3/ https://t.co/N1YTLf2LK6

Day by day, he got better as I challenged him to build "projects" for his assignments in order to show his learning growth and development (trust me he asks alot of questions). To be sincere, I commend his readiness to learn and also his commitment to the learning processes. 4/ https://t.co/lbSZxRHV4H

I understand that many of you would be like, "Hey, why didn't you refer him to platforms like @Aptlearn, @Coursera, @Udemy, @freeCodeCamp, @w3schoolsonline, @Codecademy, @Sololearn, @YouTube and other Massive Open Online Courses (MOOCs) platforms". Well, let me explain why... 5/

They are both aware of these platforms and several other platforms too and still decided to talk to me about helping. Plus everyone needs to understand that while these instruction-based learning platforms work for alot of people- even me, we shouldn't forget the fact that... 6/

That these platforms do not in anyway replace the need for a physical tutor and mentor depending on the need of the learner in question. In fact, I do reference these platforms for him to look up while still sticking to the instructional goals and objectives... 7/

I designed for him. In as much as I agree to the fact that the number of teacher to student ratio can be limited as compared to using these open online courses, I also want to implore more Techies out there to please take some of their time to personally teach and mentor... 8/

and mentor as much aspiring developer as possible. It will not only enable you master your coding skills as a developer, but it will also serve the function of bringing in more and more people into the world of Tech. The technological world is advancing, and so is the need ... 9/

For more Techies out there. Pass that knowledge to others too, it will only make you a better Person and Techie. If you are in need of someone to be there for you through your tech/dev journey, you are welcome to my inbox. Like and RT widely, someone might need this. 10/10

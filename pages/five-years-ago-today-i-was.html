Five years ago today I was in a field behind the Redeemer Lutheran Church in De Soto Missouri waiting to see my very first total solar eclipse. I had seen pretty much every other astronomical phenomenon but this was my white whale. ðŸ§µ (1/)

It was hot(90+ degrees) and humid. Thankfully the nice people that ran t church let you hang out in the air conditioned basement and had food and drinks for sale. Also they were nice enough not to charge for parking. (2/)

Why De Soto Missouri? It was right on centerline, which is where the eclipse is the deepest and last the longest and it wasn't already overbooked like some other locations were. (3/)

So as I waited for totality, mostly gawking through my solar binoculars, but I had no idea what to expect. I had heard about them but I had never actually experienced one. So I decided to not take pictures or do anything that would distract me from the experience itself. (4/)

Totality set in and I really don't have a simple way to describe what happened. The sky goes dark and the temperature dropped and night time animals got confused and thought it might be time to go about their business. (5/)

It looks like the Sun is setting on every horizon and the wind picked up and it all felt unreal. I knew what was happening and the timing down to the second but the instinctual part of my brain was whispering "Nature is broken we might need to panic" (6/)

It's one of the two times, Comet Hyakutake was the other, I totally understood ancient peoples reverence and respect for the sky and why they placed their gods there. (7/)

I remember all of it like it was five seconds ago and not five years. It was humbling and inspiring at the same time. Astronomy has that to me a lot and its one of the reasons I love it a lot. (8/)

On April 8, 2024 there will be another solar eclipse that will pass right over the town I live in. I'm pretty excited for it. If you've never seen one you really should. Even if you don't get all reverent about it like me it's still worth the effort. (9/)

Also if you want to know more about the 2024 eclipse go here and look up your location to see if you can see it from where you live. (10/) https://t.co/LryWbO7q0t

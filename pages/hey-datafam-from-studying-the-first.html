Hey #DataFam, From studying the first course in the SQL for data analyst track which is titled ‚ÄúUnderstanding Data Visualization‚Äù, I have a few things to share today to anyone about to build a dashboard or work on a project that involves visualizing data Thread üßµ

Data visualization is simply a graphical representation of data and information. For data professionals & practitioners, after gathering, cleaning, making sense of the data and performing exploratory analysis on the data the next step is to communicate your findings

This is where data visualization skills is highly needed. How do you measure a good visual - how many interesting insights can you get from the plot? If the plots are far too many then there is just a lot going on in your report and this could be overwhelming to look at

-how quickly can your audience get those insights from your plots? In this case simplicity of your plots matter. Imagine being in a meeting and you are presenting your report, they need to get your insights in seconds

Reducing Chart junks in your plots makes it easer to understand. Chart junks refers to anything in the plot that hinders the readers from getting insights from the data easily Some of them are: - trying to add excessive irrelevant designs to your plot

-using images and pictures to represent your bars in a bar plot - not starting with a zero scale on your y-axis causing a distortion in the height of your plot - not using a clear background for your plot. If you can‚Äôt think of a background color just use white

- using 3D plots for your visuals instead of 2D plots - use of doughnut chart to represent more than 3 categories in your data. I personally use 3 as my limit - use of rotated or inclined x axis labels on your horizontal bar plots. If its too long then make it vertical bar plot

These are some of the chart junks I can think of. if you can add more chart junks or bad habits you come across in dashboards or reports, please do @Nonso_Analytics @Rita_tyna @thenaijacarguy @seunmolz @starboy_abefe @DavidAbu_ @jessica_xls #dataviz #dataanalysis #DataScience

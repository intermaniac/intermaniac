CSS/PMS: IR/POLITICAL SCIENCE/CURRENT AFFAIRS After a highly provocative visit by the Speaker of USA House of Representatives, Nancy Pelosi to Taiwan, the breakaway island of China, tensions between USA & China have increased manifold. This thread examines this issue in detail

Much of the world's everyday electronic equipment is powered by computer chips made in Taiwan. Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company or TSMC - has over half of the world's market. Chinese takeover could give Beijing control over one of the world's most important industries.

5. Pillar of China Dream China wants to reinforce its dominance as a global superpower and consolidate its power. Taiwan’s declaration of independence without a Chinese response would adversely affect its ambitions to become the super power-the “China Dream”

6. Power Projection/Maritime Control Being able to set up bases further into the Pacific Ocean would extend China's military reach and intimidate nations in the region. If China controlled Taiwan, it could then control Asia's major shipping routes

7. Power Politics Controlling Taiwan would disrupt China's arch-rival USA's geographical security concept known as the "island chain strategy", which is essentially a barrier of islands between the Chinese mainland and the Western Pacific ocean.

8. Domestic Politics Any Chinese leader showing a bit of weakness about Taiwan is doomed forever. And the legitimacy of the Communist Party of China as the sole representative and guardian of national interest hinges on peaceful re-unification of China

Why is Taiwan important for the USA? Diplomatically, the USA sticks to the "One-China" policy and has formal ties with China rather than Taiwan. But it has also pledged to supply Taiwan with defensive weapons and stressed that any attack by China would cause "grave concern".

Until now, Washington's policy of "strategic ambiguity" has meant the US has been deliberately unclear about whether or how it would defend Taiwan in the event of an attack. However, declassified cables of 1982 from the State Department, detail Six Assurances given to Taiwan

1. The United States has not agreed to set a date for ending arms sales to Taiwan. 2. The United States has not agreed to consult with the PRC on arms sales to Taiwan. 3. The United States will not play a mediation role between Taipei and Beijing.

4.The United States has not agreed to revise the Taiwan Relations Act. 5.The United States has not altered its position regarding sovereignty over Taiwan. 6.The United States will not exert pressure on Taiwan to enter into negotiations with the PRC

In May 2022, President Joe Biden replied in the affirmative when asked whether the US would defend Taiwan militarily. Soon after, the White House quickly clarified that the US position on Taiwan had not changed and reiterated its commitment to the "One-China" policy.

What are America's interests in Taiwan? 1. If China was to take over Taiwan it could be freer to project power in the western Pacific region 2. It could possibly even threaten US military bases as far away as Guam and Hawaii 3. It will be a big blow to American prestige

4. It will give wrong signals to its allies in the Pacific region about American sincerity, seriousness, and capabilities. 5. It will lose the huge arms sales market of Taiwan 6. It will also lose access to Taiwanese technology

What are possible solutions to the Taiwan issue? There are possible solutions to the Taiwan issue with varying degrees of prospects and challenges 1. Status quo 2. Taiwan declaring independence 3. Peaceful reunification 4. Forceful re-unification

1. Status quo? Yes, it suits everyone. a. China would never like to go to war unless Taiwan declares independence. Its credibility as peaceful nation at stake. b. Taiwanese also averse to war; they know their limitations, have serious reservations about American commitment

c. None of the ASEAN states want to get involved in any war between the two giants d. Presently Taiwan is a golden hen for China for its easy access to American technology through Taiwanese investment in mainland China e. USA can sell arms to Taiwan

2. Taiwan declaring independence? No chance It will be the red line for which China would declare war. Taiwan can delay the Chines offensive but not indefinitely. America will be huffing and puffing but never send its troops to defend Taiwan

3. Peaceful reunification? Yes, but not now This is the most likely scenario whereby Taiwan accepts the Chines offer of Hongkong style "One country- two systems" solution but it will take time. Chinese know history is on their side and will wait even beyond 2049-the deadline.

4. Forceful reunification? Never unless----- China may resort to forceful reunification anytime if even Taiwan does not declare independence. This possibility, though remote, may become reality if a. USA establishes its base in Taiwan b. Chinese domestic politics turns ugly

So what was Nancy Pelosi doing in Taiwan? Do not read too much. She was visiting Taiwan for a. Personal reasons as she is a highly ambitious person who loves high-visibility-low substance sojourns. Remember her Afghanistan visit? b. Assuring allies- we stand with friends!

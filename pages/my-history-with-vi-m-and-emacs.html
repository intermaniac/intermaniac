I got into Linux in the late 90s and started off with vi and later vim because that's what most of my peers used. I had a fairly large `.vimrc` but most of it was stolen snippets. I didn't use any plugins for a long time but then started using some of Tim (1/2)

Pope's stuff. (2/2)

Then around 2009, I got into Clojure and that led me to Emacs. I got completely sucked in and spent the next couple of years living in it, using it for as many things as I could. I contributed to several Emcas-related packages and had one of my own. I started with a custom (1/2)

config, used Prelude and Spacemacs for a while, and then switched back to custom. I converted several coworkers to Emacs, especially because of org-mode. (2/2)

Sidenote: evil-mode for life, Emacs' keybindings were invented by neurologists who need a steady supply of people with carpal tunnel syndrome. See https://t.co/DQJUN6FgpY

This was followed by a period where due to work I was mostly using JetBrains IDEs and then this new upstart, VS Code. They were dark years and we don't talk about them in this household.

When I finally managed to escape, nvim was still quite new and shiny (0.4). It attracted me with a promise of having something that may be almost as customizable as Emacs (yeah, right) while not taking over my life to a degree where I feel like I need to pay rent to my text (1/2)

editor. I had a self-written config from the beginning, focussing on Lua APIs and Lua plugins as much as possible. When things worked, they were very enjoyable. (2/2)

Unfortunately, churn during that time was crazy. You basically needed to run nightly builds and constantly update all your plugins/switch to the new hotness if you wanted things to work well together. But with 0.6 and especially 0.7 things slowed down a bit and I spent a (1/2)

lot less time tinkering. I really liked my nvim setup. (2/2)

Then, not too long ago I read a post about new features in Emacs 29. Eglot and Treesitter in core. AOT compilation for Elisp. TRAMP for Docker/Podman. And so on. As intriguing as that sounded, I vowed not to go down this rabbit hole. Known for my strong resolve, I lasted (1/2)

for almost an hour before I was compiling Emacs 29 with a couple of patches from source. (2/2)

Most of my free time since then has gone into moving many of my computing tasks back into Emacs. Today I made a presentation for a client with Reveal.js generated from an org file with org-reveal. It's also the day I uninstalled Neovim.

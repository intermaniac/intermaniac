Random Sunday thoughts in a #thread The number of angles to watch Messi's goal on @JioCinema from last night's game is so awesome! With the kind of digital onset we're witnessing, sky's the limit how technology is changing ways we consume live sport. + https://t.co/iZnm4MkkoY

App Annie, tool for app analytics, says JioCinema garnered 1/3rd of downloads in entertainment in Nov’2022 (29%). In fact, the tool claims JioCinema was the fastest growing app across categories in Nov. Fifa obviously turned on the magic here & numbers are telling a story. +

Now, what excites me is, cricket hasn't even begun yet. There's a whole new world of tech-driven broadcast to look forward to. If these numbers are close to reality, I'm beginning to shudder what's tech going to do to this space in 5 yrs! Personalisation will be everything! +

While this guarantees a wholesome experience, as a media professional, I also feel alarmed by parallel developments that are taking place. Let me share some POVs here (feedback welcome). First things first: The line between live & non live broadcast is blurring quickly. +

What I mean is, any media platform looking to talk sport, but not doing so by staying on "rights" side is soon going to fight a tooth and nail battle for sustenance. That's why were witnessing such huge industry spends in rights market. Staying 'in the game' is imperative. +

The rights holder is leaving no stone unturned in giving you an over doze of a pre / post-match buildup -- the live game itself aside. Previews, reviews, live, highlights, moments, analysis, numbers, data, commentary, studio narratives, tech-driven camera angles. The works! +

What is left for the non-rights holder? Little! Now, let's go a step further. Even from a rights perspective, the idea of watching a live game (any sport) on traditional linear broadcast platforms already sounds so "yesterday". How long before tech devours everything else? +

An example: There's a game going on, but you don't have the time to sit in front of your TV & watch. What are your options? The game is over. But now you have some time, how do you make up for what you missed? Can you think of any engagement without a digital perspective? +

India is still a growing country, albeit a fast-growing country. Rapid pace at which technology is knocking on the doorstep of freedish users is already telling me a story. I've forever been a print journalist (until recently). So let me share a perspective from that angle. +

It's forever been argued that print media is dying. I'll beg to differ. Let me take a bet here -- traditional linear (TV) broadcast will likely perish before print. Given India's last mile connectivity, print & growing aspirations, print will linger, digital will soar. +

The rigidity of the traditional formats, high paytv subscriptions, the lack of mobility it enforces upon the consumer, the complete absence of addressable options just don't work anymore. Digital doesn't just provide these solutions, it does so at ridiculously lower costs! +

Anyways, coming back to the subject I was discussing... Look at the hyper-mode experience that Jio Cinema has dished out this World Cup. Yes, there were tech glitches early on -- but how quickly they addressed the issue and fixed it is telling me so many things. +

They're settling down, learning, adjusting. By the time IPL arrives, they'll be "ready". What do you think? To supplement the no-subscription offering to the world’s most-watched football event, JioCinema enhanced viewers’ live experience with a never-seen-before Hype Mode. +

The Hype Mode empowers fans with unique offerings on fingertips during a live match, including multi-cam view, trivia and stats in real-time, and time wheel that allows viewers to relive an unforgettable moment from the match. Simple scoreline doesn't work anymore. : ) +

Starting 2023, this whole space and the way it's changing will just be such a fascinating trend to document. What we've been discussing so far is how "big money" is coming in. Time to discuss what that "big money" is bringing along! Have an awesome Sunday. ##

[Thread] The big question to me on Twitter’s reliability over the next 6mo will hinge on how much of their infra was migrated to GCP and AWS in the prior 4yrs.

Obviously gutting a company’s staff is like gutting a human body. It’s a shock to the system and changes day to day operations not to mention the impact on morale.

That can be made up for with people “rising to the occasion.” Even then there’s a lot of lost institutional knowledge not captured in docs and job desc. The balance between the two will determine outcome.

As far as I can tell most of the live timeline and primary hadoop clusters are run by other cloud providers now. That doesn’t mean they run themselves but it means Twitter is less reliant on their own datacenters ( and whatever capacity they still serve ).

Twitter, like most internet companies that started around its time have realized that maintaining their own metal isn’t core to their business. And automating those operations is much easier these days than it was before.

Porting software to different providers isn’t 1:1 either but it’s highly likely the systems affected aren’t the primary ones we associate with the service itself.

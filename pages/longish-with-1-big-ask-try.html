Longish thread with 1 big ask: try not watching TV for a week. OK: been writing, writing about, and/or teaching software since 1979. Since 1995 when I published my first book, I have focused almost entirely on the #PLSQL language (accompanied by smatterings of SQL and APEX).

I have so many wonderful memories of meeting hundreds, perhaps 1000s, of developers around the world (and sometimes to my great honor been invited to their homes and into their families. üôè@patch72

It seems like there are a bunch of people who feel that my work, especially the "ant" book, Oracle PL/SQL Programming, has had a noticeable impact on their careers. I have been honored and humbled by their appreciation of me.

I'm going to leave Twitter soon and not join any other social media platforms. While they've got benefits, I've reluctantly come to believe that their harm greatly outdoes their good.

But before I leave (and lose much of my "soapbox"), I'd like to offer up this challenge to anyone who feels that I've had a positive impact on their lives, and might think to themselves "I owe him one." (hey do this and then....debt repaid!üôÇ) Stop watching TV for one week.

Really, that's it. Well, OK, not quite all of it. If you actually want to keep reading.....

Don't watch network television, cable TV, sports, news, Youtube videos, TikTok whatevers. Don't click on the videos in Twitter or Instagram or Facebook. Just skip it for a week.

At the end of the week, ask yourself: do I feel different? How? If so, do I like it? I feel like it opens up this enormous amount of space in my head and in my time - gives me back whole chunks of my life. Plus it gives me more time to be outside. Which takes me to......

When you think about watching TV, go outside instead. BE outside. Not glancing at yr cell phone anxiously. Watch the swaying of the trees in the wind or a bee gorging itself on a nearby flower. Be where you are, outside, sharing glorious Earth with its many other inhabitants.

I realize that this this "challenge" is not practical or possible for everyone. I hope that you can extract the core ideas from them and apply them to your own lives: Outside is better than inside. Moving is better than sitting. Reading is better than watching.

Whew. Seriously, did ANYONE read to the end of this screed? I'm kind of embarrassed, but I am going to publish it anyway. I truly believe we are better off IN A BIG WAY by not watching television, or at least keeping it to a minimum. Largely because of evolution. Bye.

A thread on Cloud vs on-prem from the trenches. I am in the process of rebuilding each of my AWS regions for a global app I own. The rebuild is fast & simple. 30 minutes to destroy any number of bare-metal instances and build new with the click of a button [1/9]

While I love how stupid simple this effort is, it's 100% an ice-berge use-case and too many people overlook what is actually required to get here.Â  We saved a shit-ton of time and resources during the actual maintenance events and weeks leading up to them [2/9]

We had zero need for DC techs doing rack/stack/cabling, no provisioning of servers, network and firewalls, etc. No coordinated multi-team cutover window on Saturday nights. One engineer doing the large bulk of work with a little support from the team [3/9]

But you have to understand the CD process we developed took a large amount of development hours over the past 12-18 months and a couple refactors to nail down and trust. This was not simple code to build into our app either. [4/9]

What time we saved on mult-team efforts to refresh, we spent on developer hours building the CD logic, testing & improving it. What money we saved on staffing requirements for a similar effort on-prem we spent in AWS cost for similar bare-metal instances. [5/9]

What savings we recoup every month on power we pay for in EC2 instance costs. Most IT orgs are not used to paying power bills BTW. At the end of the day, I honestly feel cloud vs on-prem is 100% up to what your team and organization is equipped for. [6/9]

I am fortunate to have some top class developers to put on this project and build an amazing app and pipeline. But if your org can't put the correct resources and time into Cloud, then you won't see the right ROI and on-prem will be an appealing choice.[7/9]

I could also argue that the developer's time invested into the pipeline will pay dividends over time, this is not always the case, as the code now has to be maintained and updated with ongoing augments and new services. This is also applicable to on-prem automation.[8/9]

So at the end of the day, I'll always lean towards Cloud over on-prem offerings personally as I feel it has more upside when used correctly, but I fully support the need for on-prem and hybrid environments when they make sense. [9/9]

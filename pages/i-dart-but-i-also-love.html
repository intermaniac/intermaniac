I ‚ù§Ô∏è #Dart, but I also love #Rust, #Python, #Swift and #TypeScript üöÄ Here is my #wishlist of 10 #features I hope one day will come to Dart (FYI some of these are already in the works by the fantastic Dart team). A thread (Part #1, I will post more threads with more features)! üëáüèª https://t.co/PDf6iWdkIK

#1 - I wish Dart allowed #overloading the #same #operator more than once, with different parameters. Here is an example of how Swift makes this possible effortlessly compared to the Dart example. https://t.co/DBgbv6obj2

#2 - Enumerations with associated values. Dart allows you to create enums and hardcode enum cases with values but that's not what I'm talking about! I wish for enumerations with associated values. Here is #Rust and #Swift pulling this off. https://t.co/ZqG1s7O0T7

#3 - Dart is missing a built-in #range #operator like Swift and Rust have. I'd like to be able to say 0...10 or 0..=10 to create an inclusive range between 0 to 10 and 0..<10 or 0..10 to create a range exclusive of 10. This is how Rust and Swift implement this operator. https://t.co/iwBqnzx8Va

#4 - Dart is missing #pattern #matching of #ranges for integers and doubles. Here is how that is doable in #Python, #Swift and #Rust https://t.co/ETZlga5s5X

#5 - In Dart, #optionals are *not* a #specific #datatype. In #Swift and #Rust, you can zoom into optional data types by only extending the optional type. In Dart, methods written for optional types are present even on their non-optional counterpart making it very confusing! https://t.co/49Klv9q7bL

#6 - In Dart, we cannot #express #enumerations as raw values. In #Swift for instance, an enum can have an associated value and type and can be passed as a generic value of that particular type to a function, making them very valuable and reusable. Here is an example! https://t.co/Z5vDVT85aF

#7 - In Dart, you cannot #extend a #class with a #mixin after the class has been created. In #Rust and #Swift you can easily achieve this by extending a struct/class as shown here! https://t.co/Iw4JRfyAvB

#8 - #Tuples! #Python, #Rust, #Swift and #TypeScript / #JavaScript all support tuples. This is something that is being worked on at the Dart team so hopefully it will be available soon! https://t.co/aGeySWcnvR

#9 - Dart doesn't allow passing #reference to #operators, unlike other languages such as Swift and Rust! In Dart you'll have to create functions that use those operators and then pass those function references as parameters. https://t.co/6PcckCOsq7

10 - Dart doesn't allow creation of adhoc #Mixin #combinations. This is possible in #Rust, #Swift, #TypeScript, and more. https://t.co/HEpNHiU81M

Do you need to optimize your game? Here is how to use Unreal Insights. A tool that will help you analyze the perforce of your game. A thread üßµüëáüèº

Step 1: Simply add the TRACE_CPUPROFILER_EVENT_SCOPE macro at the start of all of the functions you want to analyze. Inside the parentheses, you can add any text you like, but I recommend using the ClassName::FunctionName for visibility. https://t.co/jfIQV0vO0B

Step 2: Start UnrealInsights.exe This can be found inside *Unreal Directory*/Engine/Binaries/Win64 https://t.co/31fnFVHgXP

Step 3: Once it's running, Unreal Insights will start recording all game sessions. This includes instances where you run your game via a standalone packaged version or editor instances. These sessions are called traces and you can either view them live or review them later on. https://t.co/B5uBP70ync

Step 4: Open a trace and look inside the Frames tab for spikes in FPS. If you spot a low FPS section, double-click on it and it will highlight the tab below. https://t.co/rulwNwfaoC

Step 5: Search for your exposed functions inside the Timing View. Also, the Callees and Callers views can provide highly valuable information. The results will amaze you. In my case, the ReflectionFunctionExtension is taking 86% time required to get the commands list. https://t.co/3dQhbRncTg

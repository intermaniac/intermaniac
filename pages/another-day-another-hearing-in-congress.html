Another day, another hearing in Congress on the Oracle Cerner EHR at VA. This time it's in a Senate Appropriations subcommittee. I'll share highlights in a thread here. Notably, VA Secretary McDonough will testify separately before the Senate VA Committee this afternoon.

DepSec Donald Remy, EHR modernization chief Terry Adirim, newly confirmed VHA chief Shereef Elnahal & Oracle execs will testify. As we get underway, Sens. Heinrich, Boozman & Tester are here. @PattyMurray notably isn't here, though she's on both the Appropriations and VA panels.

Chairman Heinrich and Ranking Member Boozman, in their respective opening statements, were notably gentler on VA and Oracle than the House VA panel's members were yesterday (and in past hearings). The Appropriations Committee, of course, controls the money — so that matters.

Remy, in his opening statement, says the Oracle Cerner EHR won't go to new sites until it's fixed. "We simply will not subject our veterans to avoidable risk." It must be said that tens of thousands of veterans in WA, ID, OR and OH have already been subjected to avoidable risk.

When Heinrich asks what VA is doing to support clinicians who are struggling w/ the new EHR, Adirim repeats the claim that DoD (where she worked on the Cerner EHR until late 2021) has been "very successful" and VA has learned important lessons from the Pentagon's deployment.

Heinrich identified one of the big problems with congressional oversight of this project: “We’re still not entirely sure what constitutes success.” Remy mentioned that the contract requires the system to be fully operable 99.9% of the time. It hasn't: https://t.co/JvGcpgAm2T

Tester asks if the EHR's problems are "so intense" that they'll take many months to solve. Remy: “I don’t know that I would necessarily say, sir, that the problems are that intense, but the problems are real enough that we’re not moving forward until we resolve some of them.”

Tester, with his trademark candor, says that after ~5 years, “truthfully, we haven’t done a damn thing. I mean, we’ve implemented and it’s been a train wreck.” "We need to get this damn thing done.”

Elnahal said that in his recent visit to @va_columbus, he saw clinicians "struggling with this system, deeply.” He strikes a somewhat different tone than Remy and Adirim.

@va_columbus The second panel is now up: • Oracle's Mike Sicilia • Brian Rieksts of the Institute for Defense Analyses, which VA asked to do an independent cost estimate of the EHR program • David Case, VA deputy inspector general

Sicilia, an executive VP at Oracle, makes a big announcement: Oracle will rewrite the EHR as a cloud-based system and will deliver a beta to VA in 2023, and commits to deliver the new system "as a cost-free upgrade" under the current, $10 billion contract.
